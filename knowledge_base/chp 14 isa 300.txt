Concept Breakdown:
Planning Importance 📌
Audit start krny sy pehle planning bohot zaroori hai.
Planning ka matlab: understand karo Applicable Financial Reporting Framework (FRF).
Understanding Financial Reporting Framework (FRF):
Example: Agar PTCL ka audit ho raha hai → check karo IFRS apply hota hai ya koi aur.
IFRS 16 (Leases), IFRS 10 (Consolidation) jaise standards relevant ho skty hain.
Agar subsidy hai → IFRS 10 apply hoga.
Why FRF Understanding is Needed?
Management kehti hai: “Hum ny FS iss framework ky mutabiq banai.”
Auditor ko bhi verify krna hota hai ke compliance FRF ky according hui hai ya nahi.
Regulatory & Industry Specific Requirements:
Insurance, Banking sector → special formats regulator (SBP, SECP) ny define kiye hoty hain.
Example: Banking sector me NPL (Non-Performing Loan) regulator requirement hai, IFRS ki nahi.
Nature of Business:
Auditor ko business samajhna hoga taaky:
Performance obligations identify ho sken.
Example: Ufone → Easy load jab hota hai, cash receive hota hai but performance obligation tabhi settle hoti hai jab balance use hota hai.
Entry: Cash Dr / Contract Liability Cr (Not Revenue).
Location of Components:
Agar company ki branches abroad hain → us country ki currency aur regulatory laws ko samjhna hoga.
Example: USA branch → transactions USD me, Pakistan reporting PKR me → IAS 21 apply.
Risk Understanding through Business & Location:
Branch ki assets/PP&E impairment risk ho skti hai.
Receivables aur inventories evaluate karni hongi according to local business conditions.
Concept Breakdown:
Continuation of Planning (Strategy)
Location, business segments, FRF, regulatory requirements → sab consider krny hain.
Ye sab planning phase ka part hai taaky auditor ko samajh ajaye kon kon sy factors affect kr skty hain.
Effect of Information Technology in Audit ⚡
High-tech companies (e.g., Ufone): revenue automated system se record hota hai.
Auditor customer se directly verify nahi kr skta.
Solution: IT controls testing (ID testing) + involvement of IT specialist.
Planning stage pe hi decide krna hota hai ke IT expert ki need hogi ya nahi.
Review Engagement vs Audit Engagement 📊
Review Engagement (Listed companies – Half yearly):
Procedures: Inquiry + Analytical Procedures.
Limited Assurance → “Meri nazar me abhi tak aisa kuch nahi aya jo doubt create kre.”
Example: Revenue sudden increase → auditor inquiry karega customer base ka.
Conclusion: “Nothing has come to our attention…”
Audit Engagement (Annual audit):
Procedures: Detailed testing, vouching, confirmations, substantive tests.
Reasonable Assurance → Auditor express opinion: “In my opinion, FS give true & fair view.”
Difference in Assurance Level
Limited Assurance (Review): Inquiry aur analytical procedures → zyada deep evidence nahi.
Reasonable Assurance (Audit): Detailed procedures, sufficient evidence gathered.
Example: Teacher ne student Mustafa ka rishte wali example → Review = “abhi tak kuch negative nahi dekha”, Audit = “poori investigation ke baad strong opinion.”
Use of Review Work in Audit
Review me jo evidence mila (inquiries, observations) → Audit me use ho skta hai.
Example: Half-yearly review me auditor ny dekha student class me regular hai → annual audit me isko reliance ke tor pe use kr skta hai.
1. Review Report vs Audit Report
Review report mein bhi qualified opinion aa sakti hai.
Typical wording: “Nothing has come to our attention except…”
Agar issues hain → report mein mention hoga.
Key Point: Review engagement can also be qualified (though limited procedures).
2. Use of Prior Year Evidence
Agar second/third year audit hai → last year ka documentation available hai.
Controls ka design already documented.
Auditor should update with client inquiry:
“Any update in controls?”
If client says “No update” → auditor should still perform walkthrough.
Walkthrough = validate that controls are still operating as before.
3. Strategy & Deadlines
Deadlines matter: Agar management ki reporting deadline tight hai → auditors ko resources zyada lagani parengi.
Discuss with Those Charged with Governance (TCWG):
Risk assessment.
Fraud inquiry.
Reporting timeline.
Governance role = oversight, not management.
4. Team Discussion & Communication
Auditor apni engagement team ke saath discussion karta hai:
Identify risks.
Share fraud risks, significant risks vs normal risks.
Going concern assessment.
Team ko guidelines aur responsibilities clear karna zaroori.
Materiality aur risks sab ko communicate karni hoti hain.
5. From Strategy → Detailed Audit Plan
Strategy banane ke baad auditor banata hai:
Detailed Audit Plan = specific audit procedures.
Example: Going concern risk ke liye check karna → management plans, shareholder guarantees, bank facilities.
Audit plan = “kis risk pe kya kaam karna hai.”
6. ISA 300 (Planning an Audit of Financial Statements)
High risk areas → more direction, supervision, and review required.
Auditor needs to assess whether to rely on IT controls or not.
Strategy ka ek hissa = yeh decide karna ki controls pe rely karna hai ya direct substantive testing karni hai.
1. Sources of Evidence (Linkages)
Client Acceptance Procedures → jo info milti hai, woh audit mein use ho sakti hai.
Review Procedures → jo info milegi, woh bhi audit mein helpful hai.
Matlab: Har stage ki learning carry forward hoti hai.
2. Communication & Timing
Reporting deadlines client se discuss karni hoti hain.
Errors/Issues (missing data, hiccups, fraud indicators) → timely communicate karni chahiye, end mein nahi.
Strategy ka hissa:
Timing of communication.
Team meetings ka plan (online ya in-person, kab aur kis nature ki discussions hongi).
3. Starting Audit Before Year-End
Agar client ki reporting deadline tight hai (e.g., 15 Feb), toh auditors ko kaam year-end (31 Dec) se pehle shuru kar dena chahiye.
Example: Start in November.
Possible pre-year-end tasks:
Understanding internal controls.
Risk assessment.
Reading interim FS.
Reading Board of Directors’ (BOD) minutes.
Reviewing internal audit reports.
4. Interim Procedures
Auditor 9-month data par kaam karke interim procedures perform kar sakta hai.
Example:
Risk assessment using interim financials.
Analytical procedures (e.g., revenue/expenses trends).
Control testing for 10 months.
Logic: Agar 10 months ke controls effective hain → assume last 2 months bhi effective (but need some roll-forward procedures).
5. Effect on Sampling
Agar interim procedures se evidence mil gaya hai → sample size reduce ho sakta hai.
But: Auditor must still cover risks for full year.
Interim testing helps reduce workload, not eliminate it.
6. Materiality (Interim vs Final)
Interim planning ke liye materiality 9M profit se calculate hoti hai, then annualize to 12M.
At final audit → recalc materiality with actual annual profit.
Cases:
If final profit ≈ planned profit → no issue.
If final profit much lower → materiality reduce hogi → auditors may need extra procedures (kyunki chhoti items bhi ab material ho gayi).
If final profit much higher → no extra work needed (already sufficient testing).
