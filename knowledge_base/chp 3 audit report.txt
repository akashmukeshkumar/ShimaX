📘 Overview of Audit Report
Misstatement: Iska matlab hai ke auditor ko koi material galti milti hai jo management ko batane ke bawajood bhi correct nahi ki gayi. Jab client galti ko theek nahi karta, toh auditor apni report mein is issue ko highlight karta hai.
Evidence Obtained – No Issue Identified: Jab auditor ko koi issue milta hai aur woh client ko kehta hai ke usay correct kare — aur client sach mein correct kar deta hai — toh audit report mein yeh likha jata hai ke “no issue identified”. Yeh phrase indicate karta hai ke ab koi further issue pending nahi hai.
Modified Opinion: Jab management ne misstatement ko theek nahi kiya hota, toh auditor modified opinion deta hai.
Unmodified Opinion: Jab auditor ko tamam zaroori audit evidence mil jata hai aur financial statements mein koi material galti nahi hoti, toh auditor unmodified (clean) opinion deta hai.
Audit report: iska format pury duniya mey aik jesa hta ha takeh shareholder easily samjh saky.

📌 Definition
Yeh conditional paragraph hai jo Basis for Opinion ke baad aata hai.
Yeh tab use hota hai jab company technically “Going Concern” hai (agle 12 months tak operations continue karegi), lekin kuch specific conditions ya events ki wajah se doubt create ho raha hai ke yeh future mein bhi isi situation mein aa sakti hai. Aur iska disclosure financial statements mein management ne proper tariqay se kiya ho.(phr ap material uncertainty wla para bhy add krty hu ky wha ja kr dekhu yeh msla ha ur agr management ny discluose hy ni kia ju unky duty thy krna to yeh opnion waly para mey modified opnion hu ga not true and fair ur phr basis for modified opnion mey nature of misstatement mey bta do ga kia glty to phr alag sy material uncertainty wla para add krny ky need ni ha )
📌 Examples of Doubts
🔸 Major customer ka loss hona
🔸 Negative cash flows consistently hona
🔸 Loan payments par default karna
📌 Impact on Auditor’s Opinion
✅ Agar management ne proper disclosure kiya hai, toh auditor ka opinion modify nahi hoga. Sirf ek separate paragraph (Material Uncertainty wala) add hoga.
❌ Agar management ne adequate disclosure nahi kiya, toh auditor ka opinion modify hoga (pr phr yeh alag sy para ni ai ga).
📌 Management Responsibility
Company ki 12 months survival capability ko assess karna zaruri hai.
Doubt hone par, financial statements mein clearly disclosure provide karna management ki responsibility hai.
📌 Auditor Responsibility
Relevant evidence (budgets, liquidity, forecasts) collect aur analyze karna.
Decide karna ke kya doubt hai ya nahi, aur accordingly Material Uncertainty paragraph ko report mein include karna hai.

🔶 EMPHASIS OF MATTER (EOM) PARAGRAPH 📌🧐📄
Definition: EOM para bhy conditional hta yeh tb ata hai jab auditor ko lgy koi important matter ho jo user ke understanding(f/s ko ley kr) ke liye fundamental ho — lekin uski wajah se opinion modify nahi hota.
Condition:
Matter is adequately disclosed in F/S.
Auditor is not required to modify opinion because of that matter.
Matter is not a key audit matter.
🔶 PTCL Pension Case Example – EOM ya Modified Opinion? 🏢⚖️📊
Scenario:
PTCL privatize hui, jiske baad 500 se zyada employees ne pension ka legal case file kiya.
Government pension increase announce karti thi, PTCL ne adopt nahi kiya
Dispute filed: “Hum bhi government employees hain, hume bhi increment chahiye” Key Issue:
FS mein disclosure tha, lekin agar expected liability ka amount disclose nahi kiya gaya, to facts incomplete samjhe jayenge — jiska matlab hoga ke FS true and fair view nahi de rahi.
Auditor sochta hai: “Disclosure incomplete hai, is wajah se opinion effect ho raha hai” 👉 Result: ab EOM ka para nahi aayega, modified opinion diya jaye ga (because basis of modified opinion mein nature of misstatement explain kar diya jaye ga ke kya ghalti thi — e.g. amount disclose nahi kiya gaya — toh phir ek hi jagah explain kar diya gaya hai, isliye dubara EOM wala para nahi aayega.) 📉📘🛑
🔶 Material Uncertainty(relating to going concern) vs Emphasis of Matter 📑🔍🧾
Material uncertainty upar ka maamla hota hai (yani content mein 5th paragraph ke around hota hai), isliye usay alag se upar disclose karte hain. Baaki isky ilawa jitne bhi fundamental matters hote hain jin par auditor ko lagta hai ke user ka attention dena zaroori hai, wo sab yahan neeche EOM paragraph mein disclose kiye jaate hain.

📌 Key Audit Matters (KAM) - Intro
Definition Start:
Jab auditor audit karta hai, kuch areas aise hote hain jahan zyada attention deni parti hai.
Yehi areas auditor ke liye Key Audit Matters (KAM) ban jaate hain.
Applicable To:
Listed Companies Only
📍 Private companies mein shareholders aur users kam hote hain, isliye KAM ki requirement nahi hoti.
⚙️ Why KAM Is Important?
Listed companies mein public ka paisa involve hota hai.
Auditor jab 2 mahine ka detailed audit karta hai to kuch areas mein zyada involvement hoti hai.
Jahan auditor ka zyada focus ho, wohi KAM banta hai.
Example Given:
Auditor ne bank audit kiya.
Operating country mein inflation high thi.
Iss wajah se loans, credit quality, aur customer repayments risky ho gaye.
Control systems bhi ineffective the.
➡️ Auditor ko yeh sab cheezein assess karni padti hain aur jahan zyada kaam kiya ho, usay KAM consider kiya jata hai.
📘 KAM Definition (as per standard)
“KAM are those matters that, in the auditor’s professional judgment, were of most significance in the audit of the financial statements.”
🧪 Identification Criteria of KAM
Significant Auditor Attention:
Jahan auditor ne zyada time spend kiya ho.
Auditor ne jahan zyada procedures apply kiye ho.
Complexity Involved:
High risk areas.
Complex calculations or judgments.
Subjective Judgments:
Auditor ko significant judgment apply karna para ho.
🔍 Topic: Auditor ke kaam ka relation with KAM (Key Audit Matters)
📌 Example: PTCL Revenue System
PTCL ka revenue system complex hai, kyunke:
Multiple sources of income hain: landline, mobile, internet, leased lines waghera.
Har ek ka separate data system hai.
Revenue estimation complicated hai due to prepaid/postpaid models.
👨‍💼 Auditor ka Kaam:
Auditor ne PTCL ka revenue system audit karte waqt:
Controls ko test kiya.
Reasonableness of estimates check ki.
System-generated reports analyze ki.
Data analytics apply ki to verify trends aur mismatches.
🚨 Risk:
Revenue recognition mein material misstatement ka risk high tha.
Complex systems aur estimation uncertainty ki wajah se fraud ya error ka chance barh gaya tha.
🔑 KAM Banna:
Auditor ne conclude kiya ke yeh area (PTCL revenue) KAM hai, kyunke:
High estimation uncertainty.
Complex systems.
Significant auditor efforts required.
Frequent system changes aur multiple billing models involved thay.
📢 Communication with Those Charged with Governance (TCWG)
Kabhi-kabhi auditor ko client se response ya cooperation nahi milti.
Jaise: “6 baje tak banda aya hi nahi meeting mein.”
Auditor phir issue ko TCWG (e.g. Board) ke sath raise karta hai.
Auditor ne communicate kiya:
Yeh issue sirf report mein nahi likhna hota.
Auditor batata hai: “Hum ne yeh matter observe kiya.”
🎯 Key Audit Matter Banane Ki Wajah
KAM sirf is wajah se nahi banta ke error mila.
KAM tab banta hai jab:
Auditor ko zyada effort laga.
Judgement involved thi.
Users ke liye matter fundamental tha.
Important Point:
KAM mein auditor yeh nahi likhta ke unhone kya findings ki.
Sirf yeh likhta hai ke auditor ne kis area pe kaam zyada kiya aur kyun.
⚡ Case Example: Electricity Company & NTDC
🌀 Simple Setup:
Ek company electricity produce karti hai.
Sirf ek customer hai: NTDC (Transmission Company)
Electricity generate hoti hai → meter record karta hai → NTDC ko deliver.
🧮 Complexity Added:
Agreement ke hisaab se agar:
Electricity waste hoti hai, toh uska % bear karna hota hai.
Plant band hota hai, phir bhi paisay miltay hain (as per contract).
Peak/Non-Peak hours ke rates alag hain.
📊 Auditor’s Work:
Auditor ne:
Agreement ke parameters samjhe.
Whole-year data collect kiya.
Plant shutdown ki timings verify ki.
Complex revenue calculation test ki.
🧠 Key Insight:
Agreement + plant shutdown + waste electricity = complex revenue model
Iss wajah se auditor ka kaam bada → KAM ban gaya.
🧾 Audit Judgment vs Standard Cases
Simple revenue model = low risk = no KAM
Complex contract + estimation + control dependency = High risk → KAM
🔄 Controls Achay Hon Tab Bhi Kaam Karna Parega
Controls achay hain to bhi auditor ka kaam eliminate nahi hota.
Auditor phir bhi focus karta hai aur test karta hai.
⚖️ Audit Certificate vs Audit Opinion
Certificate: Specific confirmation e.g., tax, loan certs
Opinion: Overall view on FS (financial statements)
Dono alag concepts hain – confuse nahi karna.
📌 Conclusion: Jab revenue ya koi aur area complex ho, agreements tricky hon, estimation heavy ho, ya client dependent data ho → auditor ka kaam barhta hai. Yehi cheezein KAM ko define karti hain. Error milna zaroori nahi — zyada judgment aur user impact ka hona zaroori hai.

✍️ Concept Breakdown:
“Other Information” kya hoti hai?
Woh tamam information jo financial statements ke ilawa hoti hai, lekin annual report ka part hoti hai — e.g. Directors’ Report.
Ye information auditor ne audit nahi ki hoti, lekin usay read karna zaroori hota hai aur agar koi inconsistency ho FS ke comparison me, to usay identify karna hota hai.
⭐ Important Lines:
“Other Information ka matlab: financial statements ke ilawa wali published information (e.g. directors’ report).”
“Auditor ka kaam: read karna, match karna FS se, agar inconsistency ho to highlight karna.”
“Opinion nahi dete Other Information par.”
“Agar sab kuch theek ho to: ‘We have nothing to report.’ likha jata hai.”
⚠️ Risk (Mentioned):
Agar FS aur other information (e.g. revenue growth) me contradiction ho jaye — to auditor ko dekhna hoga kahin FS hi galat to nahi?
📒 Example :
“PTCL example”: Revenue growth directors report me kuch aur likhi hai, FS me kuch aur. Auditor ne comparison kiya, phir confirmation ke liye pricing evidence bhi check ki.
🧰 Additional Key Points:
Other information kab applicable hoti hai? Jab company legally annual report publish karti hai.
Agar company annual report banati hi nahi — to yeh part auditor ke liye applicable nahi hota.
Auditor ne likhna hota hai ke:
“We have read other information.”
“No material inconsistencies found.”
“We do not express an opinion on it.”
Fundamental for understanding FS: Agar koi info user ke liye zaroori hai FS samajhne ke liye, to usay bhi cover karna hota hai.

🧾 Responsibility Clarification in Audit Report
🔹 Auditor’s Signature & Shareholder Misunderstanding
Auditor apni report me shareholder ko clear karta hai:
“Yeh mat samajhna ke financial statements maine banaye hain.”
“Yeh mat samajhna ke internal controls maine lagaye hain.”
Clarification: Yeh sab responsibility management ki hoti hai.
🔹 Going Concern Assessment
Auditor directly yeh nahi kehta ke company 12 months tak chalegi ya nahi.
Going concern assess karna management ki zimmedari hai.
Auditor sirf yeh check karta hai ke:
Management ne assessment ki ya nahi.
Assessment realistic hai ya bas hawa me ki gayi hai.
Agar koi signal mile (e.g. ek hi customer hai, uska contract khatam hone wala hai), auditor sawal uthata hai.
💬 Example:
Ek company ka sirf 1 customer tha.
Uska contract renew nahi hua tha.
Auditor ne management se poocha:
“Aapne going concern assess kiya?”
Management: “Renewal ho jayega.”
Auditor: “Abhi tak hua to nahi!”
🔎 Auditor ka kaam: Situations ko dekh kar signals uthana, aur management se poochhna ke unhone assessment ki ya nahi. Auditor khud judgement nahi deta — warna self-review ho jata.
🏢 EasyPaisa & Telenor Network Example
EasyPaisa ka apna retail network nahi → Telenor ka network lease par use karta hai.
Auditor ne poocha: “Agar Telenor network chhod de, to EasyPaisa kya karega?”
Business continuity ka risk → Going Concern Issue?
Later pata chala agreement 10 saal ke liye renew ho chuka hai ✅
Risk khatam ho gaya.
🧠 Auditor’s Objective
🔹 Definition
Audit ka objective: “To obtain reasonable assurance that FS are free from material misstatement”
🔹 Assurance Kese Milega?
Audit Evidence
All opinion ka base = Audit evidence
Without evidence, opinion nahi di ja sakti.
Sampling Basis
Auditor pura data nahi check karta — sampling basis pe kaam hota hai.
Reason: Time limitation, cost, practicality
Multiple Judgments Involved
Audit is not black-and-white
Har jagah judgment hoti hai — jese architecture ka kaam, har jagah suitability check karni padti hai
🧱 Example for Judgment:
Ek banda building banwana chahta hai, kisi local mason se karwa deta hai bina qualification ke
Bad me architect aata hai, check karta hai space, safety, suitability
Same auditor: Har cheez ko inspect karne se pehle assess karta hai kahan detailed checking chahiye
🛠️ Audit Timeframe & Practicality
🔹 Constraints:
Pura mahina ka data 24 ghante me check nahi ho sakta
Millions of transactions (e.g. PTCL) → auditor ko monthly ya selective period basis pe dekhna padta hai
📊 Data Connectivity & Audit Evidence
🔹 Real Case: Submarine Cables & Data Transfer
Client claim: System auto-shows where data is transferred (e.g., from Pakistan to USA via submarine cable).
Auditor: “How can I verify this?”
Answer: System evidence (connectivity logs, data paths) → ✅ reliable if secure & verifiable.
🔹 Key Concept
Auditor cannot verify each transaction daily (time/volume constraint)
Hence, system-based evidence can be used where practical
🧠 Judgments in Financial Reporting
🔹 Judgments Start With Management
Financial statements require management judgment at many levels:
Revenue recognition
Allocation of transaction price
Future assumptions (e.g., inflation, demand)
🔹 Example: Ufone Super Card Revenue Allocation
1 card = services (voice, SMS, data)
How much revenue to allocate to each service?
No paper trail → needs reasonable estimation
Based on usage data, historical patterns
🔹 Example: Shift in Usage Pattern
Last year: voice revenue > data
This year: data usage ↑, voice ↓
Need to revise revenue policy accordingly
Auditor checks if management adjusted based on realistic patterns
📌 Link to Audit Standards – ISA 200
🔹 What ISA 200 Says
Audit has inherent limitations
Auditor gives reasonable assurance, not absolute
Auditor doesn’t re-perform management’s work — he evaluates
📉 Misuse of Judgment Risk
🔹 Manipulating Financial Results
If company wants to look good before IPO, they may:
Overstate revenue
Understate liabilities
🔹 Auditor’s Role
Evaluate if judgments are:
Based on logical, consistent methodology
Supported by data or market trends
Not just for window dressing
🔹 Judgment Indicators
Look at industry trends
Compare past vs. present data
Analyze usage patterns

Main Concept: Judgment & Materiality in Audit ✍️📘🧠
1. Auditor’s Responsibility & Judgment: ✏️📌🧐
Auditor ki judgment har jagah audit mein involve hoti hai.
Audit ke decisions fixed nahi hote; circumstances ke according change ho sakte hain.
Auditor absolute assurance nahi deta — sirf reasonable assurance.
Judgment kabhi absolute proof nahi hoti, sirf evidence base pe evaluate ki jati hai.
2. Example of System Change: 🖥️🔄📂
Ek company ne new IT system implement kiya.
Purana data naye system mein migrate hua.
Auditor ne multiple checks kiye:
Data demand par check kiya.
Past records cross-check kiye.
Industry data ko bhi analyze kiya.
Phir bhi 100% accuracy ka assurance nahi diya ja sakta.
3. Auditor’s Conclusion & Management Representation: 📄✍️✅
Audit ke end pe auditor management (CFO, CEO) se written representation letter sign karwata hai.
Statement ye hoti hai: “FS humari responsibility hai.”
Auditor ye letter future mein evidence ke liye use karta hai.
Example: CEO agar baad mein mana kare to auditor ke paas yeh letter hota hai jo proof deta hai.
4. Materiality ka Role: 💰📊⚖️
Audit mein materiality judgment based hoti hai:
Ek item kisi shareholder ke liye material ho sakti hai, dusre ke liye nahi.
Director agar close ho matter se, to usko value ka estimate hota hai.
Auditor ko yeh dekhna hota hai ke:
Kya amount material hai?
Agar hai, to kya isse FS modify karni chahiye?
5. Re-checking Based on Circumstances: 🔁🔍📝
Kabhi kabhi audit ke baad bhi kuch errors milti hain:
Auditor phir se test karta hai.
Previous threshold revise karta hai.
Additional evidence gather karta hai.
Isay “Re-evaluation due to triggering events” kehte hain.
6. Audit Planning & Capability: 🧾📈👥
Jab audit plan hota hai:
Client ka size dekha jata hai.
Risk zyada ho to zyada log assign kiye jate hain.
Team ki capability matter karti hai:
Agar firm ke paas qualified log nahi hain, to audit ka objective achieve nahi hoga.
–> Example:
Ek firm ke paas 500 employees hain but time nahi — client serve nahi ho raha.
What is Materiality? 🧠💡✅
Materiality = Any information that can influence the economic decision of a reasonable user.
1. Materiality Example: PTCL Case 🏢📊💬
PTCL ka revenue 115 billion hai. Agar apne is client ko sirf 5 lakh ki audit fee di hai, to yeh disproportionate lagta hai. 🤨📉⚠️ Auditor ko kaam ki quality bhi deliver karni hai — aur uske liye paisa bhi quality hona chahiye. Jab client bada ho, to kaam bhi zyada hota hai — iska matlab zyada resources aur zyada staff chahiye hote hain. Yeh sab planning phase mein assess hona chahiye.
2. Material Difference & Modified Opinion 📈✍️❗
Agar FS mein 5% se zyada ka difference ho (profit before tax se compare), to yeh material misstatement ho sakta hai. Is case mein auditor ko:
CFO ki sign leni hoti hai ✅
CEO ko inform karna padta hai 📢
Zarurat ho to modified opinion bhi deni padti hai 📃
Agar management sath nahi de rahi, ya document sign nahi kar rahi, to yeh bhi risk ka indication hai.
3. Balance Sheet Specific Line Item Check 📊🔍🧾
Agar kisi ek line item (e.g. inventory) mein unusual balance ho, to auditor usko separately check kar sakta hai. Is process ko bolte hain specific item focus. Auditor ye dekhta hai:
Internal controls effective hain ya nahi
Agar controls weak hain, to checking zyada hoti hai
Yani jahan risk ho, wahan checking bhi zyada hoti hai.
4. What is Material to User? 👨‍💼💭📉
Materiality ka base hota hai user ka focus. 🤔🔎📈 Har user ka focus alag hota hai — NGO ka user expenses pe focus karega, investor profit pe.
Example: NGO ke case mein, har choti cheez bhi material ho sakti hai kyunke unka scale chhota hota hai. Is liye auditor ko yeh dekhna hota hai ke:
User ka concern kya hai
Kis cheez se unka decision influence ho sakta hai
5. Changing Threshold for Testing 📉🔁🔬
Agar aap threshold kam kar dete ho (e.g. 5 lakh se 4 lakh), to sample size badh jata hai.
Zyada invoices check hoti hain
Zyada misstatements detect hone ka chance hota hai
Yeh sample size change hota hai risk ke base pe, aur auditor ki judgement pe depend karta hai.
6. Auditor’s Objective in Materiality 🎯📃🧠
Auditor ka core objective hota hai “to obtain reasonable assurance that FS are free from material misstatement.” Yeh definition practical knowledge ke through samjhni hoti hai:
Audit ek judgement-based process hai
User ke liye kya material hai, wo samjhna auditor ki zimmedari hai
Agar koi misstatement user ke decision ko influence kare — to wo material hai.
7. Material Misstatement Examples 🧾📌🚨
Example 1: Ek employee ne company pe case kiya for 2 lakh salary deduction. Lekin agar company ka turnover billion ka hai, to yeh material nahi hoga.
Example 2: PTCL ka pension case — agar 80 billion ka impact ho sakta hai aur disclose nahi kiya gaya, to yeh material hai.
Yani auditor yeh assess karta hai:
Kya disclosure hona chahiye tha?
Agar nahi hua, to kya user ka decision change hota?
Main Concept: Material Misstatement & User Decision Impact 🧠📊📉
1. Misstatement Material Kab Hoti Hai? 📌⚠️💬
Agar koi galti aisi ho jo user ke economic decision ko impact kare, toh wo misstatement material kehlati hai.
Sirf yeh kehna ke galti choti thi kafi nahi — user point of view matter karta hai.
Example: Agar State Bank ko galat picture dikh rahi ho, toh wo material hai.
2. Practical Example - AGF & Reporting Conflict: 🧾🤝📄
Auditor ne AGF mein audit kiya, proper standards ke according.
Lekin client ne report reject kar di: “Tum kaun hote ho report karne wale?”
Agreement mein likha tha ke auditor harmful statement nahi change karega.
Auditor ka kaam hai material errors identify karna, not every minor mistake.
3. Materiality Statement Explanation: 💡🧾🧐
Auditor yeh keh sakta hai: “Ho sakta hai choti moti errors hoon, lekin wo material nahi hongi.”
Financial Statements mein minor errors allowed hain, lekin threshold cross hone par opinion modify hoga.
Numerical Materiality Threshold Calculation: 🧮📈📊
8. General Rule:
Auditor Profit Before Tax ka 5% threshold rakhta hai materiality judge karne ke liye.
Example: Agar PBT = 1 million, toh 5% = 50,000.
Is limit se zyada ka impact material hoga.
9. Application on Audit Areas: 📊📋🔍
Balance Sheet check karte waqt: PP&E, Inventory, Cash waghera.
Example: Agar PP&E mein 500 laptops purchase kiye gaye worth 5 lakh — each invoice verify ki jati hai.
Jo bhi head 5 lakh se upar hai, testing mein zaroori hota hai.
10. Allowable Error & Audit Testing: ⚙️📉✅
5 lakh tak ka error — tolerable.
Us se upar — verify & reportable.
Jitne bhi individual assets 5 lakh se upar hain — 100% testing.
11. Relevance of Sampling: 🎯🧪📊
Neeche wale amounts sampling se check honge.
Upar wale amounts full verification ke under aayenge.
Main Concept: Inventory, Sampling & Misstatement Impact 📦📑📊
1. High-Value Inventory Verification: 🏷️🔍📈
Example: Inventory ka balance 300 million hai.
50 invoices aisi hain jo 5 lakh se upar hain.
In invoices mein se ek bhi galat hui, toh wo misstatement material level tak ja sakti hai.
Is liye, high-value items ko individually verify karna hota hai.
2. Low-Value Items & Equal Sampling Chance: 🎲🧾📉
Jo 5 lakh se neeche hain unka selection sampling basis pe hoga.
Har item ka selection ka chance equal hota hai.
Sampling ka objective yeh hai ke errors ko detect kiya jaye bina har choti item ko check kiye.
3. Sampling & Risk Balance: ⚖️📋📌
High risk ya zyada value ke items ko full testing.
Low risk items pe sampling apply hoti hai.
Auditor ko logical reasoning se decide karna hota hai kitni testing required hai.
4. Accumulated Misstatements Summary: 📚➕🧠
Har galti jo detect hoti hai (choti ya badi), auditor usay accumulate karta hai.
Iska naam hai: “Summary of Uncorrected Misstatements.”
Baad mein yeh summary check hoti hai — kya yeh total milkar material threshold cross kar rahi hai?

Main Concept: Written Representation from Management
🔶 Auditor management se written representation leta hai:
Ke kisi bhi material misstatement ka unko pata nahi.
Agar koi galti hui bhi thi, to usay rectify kar diya gaya hai.
Yeh bhi likhwa leta hai: “Agar koi aur material misstatement hoti jo sahi honi chahiye thi, wo bhi batayein.”
📌 Must Remember: Written representation ka matlab yeh nahi ke auditor ka kaam khatam.
Important Line:
“Written representation is not a sole evidence, it is a supporting evidence.”
Example (Bank Audit Case):
🔎 Auditor ne MIS data me dekha:
Loan discuss hua tha 15 Jan ko
Agreement dikhaya gaya 5 Jan ka
Interest charge hua extra (5 Jan se)
📌 Yeh situation auditor ko doubt me dalti hai ke management ne material information chhupai ho sakti hai.
Main Concept: Power of Written Representation
🔶 Jab koi banda written me sign karta hai, to us par zimmedari aa jati hai:
Future me agar koi issue aata hai to uska accountable wo sign karne wala hoga.
Auditor ye representation le kar apni zimmedari limit kar leta hai.
📌 Written representation lena auditor ki risk management strategy ka hissa hai.
Concept Clarification:
Auditor management ke hidden collusion tak nahi pohanch sakta.
Is liye jo bhi us ke control me ho, wo karta hai: email check, confirmations, written representation lena.
Agar written na le to future me blame auditor pe aa sakta hai.
Example (Collusion & Evidence Support):
Auditor ne ek area audit kiya, client keh raha tha: “hum kuch nahi chhupa rahe.”
Auditor ne sirf itna likhwa liya: “iske ilawa koi aur email nahi hai.”
Jab likhne se mana kya gaya, auditor ko doubt hua — yahan kuch toh chhupaya ja raha hai.

Jab auditor keh raha hota hai ke FS “true and fair” hain, iska matlab:
FS facts ke mutabiq hain (true)

FS mein ek building ki value likhi hai Rs. 10 crore

IFRS (International)
Companies Act 2017 (Pakistan specific)

Topic: Other Matter Paragraph (OMP)
Other Matter Paragraph ka kaam:
Jab auditor ko lagta hai ke user ki understanding ke liye koi extra baat batana zaroori hai jo FS se directly related nahi hai, lekin audit report se related hai.
Example: FS special purpose framework pe bane hain, ya auditor ne koi aur audit report issue ki hai jo is se mix na ki jaye.
📌 OMP dene ke main points:
Going Concern / Incident Related
Agar pehle hi going concern ke liye paragraph diya hai to dobara OMP ki zarurat nahi hoti — unless koi aur alag cheez ho.
Material
OMP tab hi dena jab matter fundamental ho user ki FS understanding ke liye.
Agar matter fundamental nahi → OMP nahi dena.
Auditor’s Judgment
Auditor apni judgment use karega decide karne ke liye ke OMP dena hai ya nahi.
Sirf wohi baat include kare jo user ki audit report samajhne ke liye zaroori ho.
🎯 Example Scenarios:
Special Purpose FS
FS general purpose ke bajaye special purpose pe bane hue hain.
User ko lag sakta hai yeh normal FS hain → confusion door karne ke liye OMP me bataya jata hai.
Wording: batana ke yeh FS special purpose ke liye bane hain, normal nahi hain.
Another Audit Report Issued
Auditor ne ek aur audit report issue ki hai (general purpose FS pe).
OMP me clearly likhna:
Wo alag report hai.
Uski date mention.
Dono ko mix na karein.
📌 Special Purpose vs General Purpose Reports
Agar Special Purpose report General Purpose ke baad issue hota hai, EOM me sirf tab reference dena jab user ki FS understanding ke liye essential ho.
EOM me clarify karna ke:
FS special purpose ke liye bane hain.
Additional audit report confusion door karne ke liye hai.
Agar dono reports same FS ke liye hain → mention justified.
📌 Referencing Other Audit Reports
Dusre auditor ki report ka reference tab tak na do jab tak standards allow na karein.
Har report apne scope, date, aur responsibility ke sath independent hoti hai.
GPFS audit aur SPFS audit alag auditors ne ki ho to cross-reference nahi karna unless standards permit.
📌 Event Dates and Reporting
Responsibility Period: FS date → Report date.
FS date ke baad aur report date se pehle koi event ho to auditor ko evaluate karna hoga.
Example:
FS Date: 31 Dec 2023
Event: 28 Mar 2024 → Agar FS relevant ho to address karo.
📌 After Report Date
Report date ke baad auditor ka responsibility nahi hota, except agar standards ke mutabiq reissue karna pade.
Prior year ka audit prior auditor ki responsibility hota hai.
Example: 2023 FS audited by KPMG (report date 15 Mar 2024) → Current auditor ka responsibility is date se pehle ke events tak nahi.
📌 Practical Cases
Case 1: SP audit after GP audit → Mention in EOM if crucial.
Case 2: Prior year different auditor → Mention date & opinion.
Case 3: Event after FS date, before report date → Address karo.
📌 Report Date vs Signature Date
Report Date: Jab audit kaam complete hua.
Signature Date: Jab report sign hui (aksar same hoti hai).
Responsibility report date tak hoti hai, uske baad nahi.
📌 Auditor ka Review Period
Auditor ka kaam: FS date (e.g., 31 Dec) se report sign hone tak ke events ka review.
Sign karne se pehle ensure karo ke:
Saare adjusting entries record ho chuke hain.
Disclosure ki requirement puri ho chuki hai.
Agar koi material subsequent event mile → pehle adjust/disclose karvao, phir sign karo.

FS date: 31 Dec, inventory recorded @ Rs. 25/unit.
5 Jan: Sale hua @ Rs. 18/unit (loss evidence).
Auditor ka action:
Material impact → adjustment required.
Management agar refuse kare → report modify karo.
📌 Example 2 – Post Year-End Flood
31 Dec: Inventory perfect condition.
Jan: Flood damage → non-adjusting event (event occurred after year-end).
Impact:
Agar material hai → disclosure required.
No adjustment to 31 Dec figures.
📌 Example 3 – Debtor Insolvency
FS date: 31 Dec.
Jan: Major customer bankrupt, but condition existed at year-end.
Impact:
Evidence existed before year-end → adjusting event.
Provision for bad debts required.
📌 Practical Notes
Auditor should perform specific procedures before signing:
Legal confirmations.
Management inquiries.
Reviewing subsequent transactions.
Partner involvement critical for material cases.
Report date fix tab hoti hai jab saara audit evidence mil jata hai.
📌 Key Takeaways
IAS 10 guide karta hai:
Adjusting → condition existed at FS date.
Non-adjusting → condition arose after FS date.
Disclosure ka decision materiality pe depend karta hai.
📌 Example 4 – Bank Collapse After Year-End
FS date: 31 Dec.
Entity ne Rs. 1.2 billion ka loan diya tha bank ko.
Audit partner ko report sign se pehle pata chala bank bankrupt ho gaya.
Condition: Financial trouble existed pre year-end → Adjusting event.
Action:
Advance ko impair karo (bad debt provision).
Sign tab karo jab adjustment ho jaye.
📌 Example 5 – Major Lawsuit Settlement
FS date: 31 Dec.
Jan me lawsuit settle hua, aur outcome confirm hua ke obligation year-end pe exist karta tha.
Impact:
Adjusting event → liability recognize karo.
📌 Example 6 – Post Year-End Natural Disaster
Event hua Feb me, inventory destroy ho gayi.
Condition: Event after year-end, unrelated to prior conditions.
Action:
Non-adjusting event.
Agar material → disclose in notes.
📌 Auditor Procedures Before Report Date
Legal letters obtain karna.
Management se updated info lena.
Significant subsequent transactions review karna.
Partner ka specific queries list banana (material cases me).
Reminder: Report date wahi hogi jab saara audit evidence complete ho, aur partner satisfied ho ke sab material subsequent events handle ho gaye.

Auditor ki responsibility sirf report date tak ke subsequent events cover karti hai.

📌 Main Concepts
Misstatement & Qualified Opinion
Agar error material hai lekin pervasive nahi → Qualified opinion.
Qualified ka matlab: “Humari opinion me, except for this issue, baaki sab theek hai”.
Misstatement ka example: COGS ya Balance Sheet wrong book hui ho.
Pervasive Effect
Jab ek se zyada jagah problem ho aur uska impact poore FS pe ho → Pervasive.
Materiality amount bahut zyada ho → Materiality se upar chala jaye to bhi pervasive ho sakta hai.
Scope Limitation
Auditor ko required audit evidence nahi mil raha.
Confined → ek jagah ka issue.
Not confined → multiple jagah effect.
Agar missing evidence ka impact material hai → Qualified opinion.
Agar missing evidence ka impact material + pervasive hai → Disclaimer of opinion.
Disclaimer of Opinion
Jab auditor ko itna significant scope limitation ho ki opinion dena possible nahi.
Example: PTCL ka 60% balance sheet portion verify nahi ho paya.
Heading change hoti hai: “Opinion” → “Disclaimer of Opinion”.
“Basis for Opinion” → “Basis for Disclaimer of Opinion”.
Modified Opinion Types Recap
Qualified → Material misstatement OR scope limitation (material, not pervasive).
Adverse → Material misstatement + pervasive.
Disclaimer → Scope limitation + material + pervasive.
Examples
Inventory ka kuch hissa verify nahi ho paya (material, not pervasive) → Qualified.
PTCL case jahan 60% portion ka evidence missing → Disclaimer.
📝 Flow Reminder
Identify issue → Misstatement ya Scope limitation?
Measure materiality.
Check pervasive hai ya nahi.
Decide opinion type accordingly → Qualified, Adverse, ya Disclaimer.
📌 Main Concepts
Going Concern
Auditor assess karega ki entity 12 months tak operations continue kar paayegi ya nahi.
Agar substantial doubt hai → Management se plans pooche jaate hain.
Evidence kaafi nahi mila aur doubt material + pervasive hai → Disclaimer ho sakta hai.
Example – Bank Confirmation Missing
Auditor bank se confirmation lena chahta tha, lekin company ne allow nahi kiya.
Ye scope limitation hai.
Agar bank balance material + pervasive → Disclaimer.
Agar material lekin pervasive nahi → Qualified opinion.
Difference Recap
Qualified: Material misstatement ya scope limitation (not pervasive).
Adverse: Material misstatement + pervasive.
Disclaimer: Scope limitation + material + pervasive.
Report Wording Changes
Disclaimer case me opinion paragraph ka title change ho jata hai.
“Basis for Opinion” → “Basis for Disclaimer of Opinion”.
Wording me opinion express nahi ki jaati.
🔹 Going Concern Assessment
Main issue: Management ne Going Concern ka evidence provide nahi kiya.
Scenario:
Management ne apni ability to continue operations ka proof nahi diya.
Loan repayment plans ka koi saboot nahi.
Yeh scope limitation create karta hai → jo le jata hai Disclaimer of Opinion par.
🔹 Correct Approach
Issue identify karein → jaise going concern ke evidence ki kami.
Impact link karein → assessment possible nahi → scope limitation.
Opinion → Disclaimer of opinion.
🔹 Common Mistakes to Avoid
“Material uncertainty” likhne se pehle proof hona chahiye.
Irrelevant standards (jaise disclosure) ke sath link na karein.
“Payment missed” tabhi likhein jab verify ho.
🔹 Ideal Answer Flow
Events & conditions jo going concern pe doubt create karte hain unko state karein.
Management ka evidence provide na karna mention karein.
Conclude karein → scope limitation → disclaimer of opinion.
🔹 Examiner’s Expectation
Events & conditions clear likhe hon.
Logical link ho issue → assessment → opinion.
Simple aur precise language use karein.
✅ Final Opinion in Scenario: Disclaimer of opinion due to inability to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence (scope limitation).

Main Concept:
Materiality ka concept — quantitative vs qualitative — aur kaise chhote se chhote misstatements bhi material ho sakte hain agar wo users ke economic decision ko influence karein. 💡
Example – Performance & Bonus Link
Supervisor: 3 adjustments accept, 2 reject.
Question: 2 kyun reject? 🤔
Investigation:
Performance Plan tha — profit certain level pe hota to bonus milta.
Agar 2 adjustments accept hoti → profit kam hota → bonus nahi milta.
Management ne apne bonus bachane ke liye adjustments reject ki. ⚠️
Materiality Analysis
Quantitative materiality = 75 million.
Total misstatements = 150 million → clearly material.
Lekin sir ne bataya: Agar sirf 10 million ka effect hota lekin bonus trigger change hota → ye bhi material hai (qualitative reason).
Kyun? Kyunki yeh users ke economic decision (bonus dena/na dena) ko affect karta hai. 📈
Key Point ⭐
“Even if quantitative threshold se neeche hai, agar wo users ke decision ko impact kare, to wo material hai.”
Audit Perspective
Auditor ko dekhna hoga:
Sirf numbers nahi, context bhi.
Bonus, covenants, contracts ka link.
Qualitative factors ke basis pe bhi misstatement material ho sakta hai.
Entity ki understanding weak ho → important issues miss ho sakte hain → regulator penalty.
Real-life Impact
Example: 3,500 employees ko 4× salary ka bonus.
Small adjustment se bonus eligibility badal sakti hai → huge monetary impact.
Main Concepts Covered
Material Misstatement Types
Quantitative (Amount-based) → e.g., misstatement in figures.
Qualitative (Nature-based) → even small amount but impacts regulatory ratios, licenses, covenants, etc.
When Misstatement is Material
Agar Material Representation ka correction management refuse kare → Opinion Modify karna.
Agar clear ho jaye ke misstatement immaterial hai → no impact on opinion.
Key Example – Bank Audit & Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)
CAR requirement = 15% minimum.
Case:
Total Capital Adequacy after error correction → 14.5% hota.
Error = 100 million misstatement.
Agar record hota → ratio meet hota. Agar na hota → ratio fail hota → license condition breach.
Conclusion: Small amount qualitatively material because it impacts license compliance.
Link to Going Concern
Agar ratio breach ho jaye → Regulatory authority (State Bank) can suspend/restrict license.
Management ke future plans & actions evaluate karne hote hain to assess Going Concern.
Qualitative Materiality ka Core
Amount chhota ho sakta hai but agar uska impact user ke economic decision change kare → Material considered.

Going Concern – Assessment Process 🏢
Concept:
Auditor ka kaam hota hai check karna ke company Going Concern hai ya nahi.
Agar financial statements by default going concern assume kar ke banayi gayi hain, auditor ko assess karna hoga ke yeh sahi hai ya nahi.
Assessment ke liye management se poochna hota hai: “Kya aapne Going Concern assessment ki hai?”
Agar haan ➡ plan aur evidence verify karo.
Agar nahi ➡ disclaimer of opinion de sakte ho.
Indicators ke example:
Cash ki kami / bank payments due but unable to pay.
Customer contracts na milna.
Bank se additional financing ka arrangement chal raha ho.
Case Example:
Management ka plan: Dusre bank se negotiation chal rahi hai paisa arrange karne ke liye.
Auditor ka kaam:
Inspect kare bank ka agreement / terms.
Direct confirmation le new bank se – likh kar poochna ke loan approve hoga ya nahi.
Check quality of evidence – sirf photocopy of agreement quality evidence nahi hota; direct bank reply zyada reliable hai.
Board of Directors ke meeting minutes review karna – approve hua hai ya nahi.
Material Uncertainty point:
Agar paisa receive hone ka agreement hai aur payment due se pehle paisa aajata hai ➡ material uncertainty nahi.
Agar bank ne conditions rakhi hain jo fulfill karni mushkil hain ➡ material uncertainty ho sakti hai.
Auditor ka next step:
Cash flow projections verify karna:
Monthly sales inflow estimate
Monthly expenses estimate
Net cash balance calculate karke dekho ke loan instalment pay ho sakti hai ya nahi.
Projection Verification:
Management se projected cash flows lo.
Check karo:
Sales forecast realistic hai?
Expenses understate toh nahi kiye?
Bank statement aur agreements se reconcile karo.
Going Concern – Sales-Based Cash Flow Support 📊
Scenario:
Client kehta hai: “Bank se loan nahi lenge, hum apni sales generate karke cash flows banayenge”.
Auditor ka kaam: is self-generated cash flow plan ko verify karna.
Step 1 – Quantity-based Sales Projection Test
Management ka plan sales quantity par based hai.
Auditor ne:
Historical quantity data check kiya – pichle saal XYZ customers ko kitna quantity supply hua.
Us growth ko compare kiya budgeted/projected quantity growth se.
Dekhna hai kya projection mein jo growth hai wo historical trend ke mutabiq hai ya unrealistic hai.
Example:
Agar last year se 5% growth thi, lekin projection me 30% growth dikhai gai hai ➡ reason mangna hoga.
Step 2 – New Customers Impact
Management ka kehna: “Is saal naye orders aur naye customers onboard hue hain”.
Auditor ka kaam:
New orders ka documentary evidence lena.
Verify karna ke ye orders budget mein include hue hain.
Agar nahi, toh separately consider karna.
Step 3 – Production Capacity Limit Check ⚙️
Auditor ne projected quantities ko company ki actual production capacity se compare kiya.
Agar projection capacity se zyada hai ➡ plan unrealistic.
Example:
Company ki max capacity: 15 million meters.
Projection: 18 million meters ➡ not feasible.
Step 4 – Internal Consistency of Plan
Projection ke andar logical alignment check karna:
Sales quantity feasible hai?
Production capacity ke andar hai?
New orders documented hain?
Step 5 – Auditor ka Conclusion Process
Har projection element verify karna (historical comparison, capacity limit, new orders evidence).
Management ka plan realistic hai ya nahi decide karna.
Agar unrealistic ➡ material uncertainty on going concern ho sakti hai.
Going Concern – Capacity & Feasibility Check 🏭
Scenario Extension:
Ab auditor capacity ke limitation aur internal constraints ka detailed review kar raha hai.
Focus: Quantity-based projections realistic hain ya nahi.
Step 6 – Maximum Capacity Constraint
Compare budgeted quantities with maximum achievable production capacity.
Example:
Capacity = 15 million meters
Budget projection = 18 million meters ❌
If projection > capacity → Unrealistic unless management proves capacity expansion.
Step 7 – Internal & External Constraints
Internal factors:
Raw material availability
Workforce limits
Machine utilization rates
External factors:
Market demand
Regulatory restrictions
Auditor needs to verify both before accepting projections.
Step 8 – Management’s Justification
Management claim: “Naye customers onboard hue hain” ✅
Auditor action:
Verify contracts/orders.
Check inclusion in budget.
Separate new customers from recurring customer base.
Step 9 – Linking Capacity to Cash Flow
Quantity projection → Sales revenue → Cash inflow.
If capacity limit breached → Cash flow projection unreliable.
Example: Overstated quantity inflates revenue & hides going concern risks.
Step 10 – Final Auditor Evaluation
Align quantity projections with:
Historical performance 📈
Capacity constraints ⚙️
Documented new orders 📄
If any mismatch → raise as audit concern under going concern assessment.

Example: Insurance claims, jo bahut complex ho sakte hain, unka valuation.
Case study: Insurance claim valuation:

IBNR aur Claims Ki Detailed Discussion 🎯
➡️ IBNR kya hota hai?
Matlab “Incurred But Not Reported” yani wo claims jo abhi tak report nahi huay, magar honay wale hain.
Ye insurance companies ke liye bohat important hota hai, kyunki pata hona chahiye kitne claims future mein aane hain.
➡️ Claim reporting ka timing issue:
Events kab hote hain, aur claims kab report hote hain, dono alag alag waqt pe hotay hain.
Example: 2020 mein event hua, magar claim 2021 mein report hua.
Is delay ko samajhna zaroori hai taake estimate sahi bane.
➡️ Chain Ladder Method:
Ye ek mathematical method hai jisme pichlay saalon ke claims data ko analyze karke future claims estimate kiye jate hain.
Har saal ke reported claims ko use karke ratio banate hain, phir us ratio se estimate nikala jata hai.
Yeh method claims ka overall trend samajhne mein help karta hai.
➡️ Historical data ki importance:
Pichlay saalon ke data ko benchmark ke taur pe use karte hain.
Agar koi saal mein accidental claim rate 5% se zyada increase ho jaye, to wo ek red flag hota hai.
➡️ Calculation ka overview:
Example: 2021 mein 50 million claims, 2022 mein 20 million, phir 2023 mein data analyze karte hain.
Ye data use karke hum pata lagate hain ke kitne claims abhi tak report nahi huay.
➡️ Claims ki reporting chain:
Claims report hone ki ek chain hoti hai — jisme har saal thoda-thoda claims report hote hain.
Hum is chain ko samajh ke claims ki accurate estimate nikalte hain.

📝 Case Discussion: Legal Case & Key Audit Matter (KAM)
Scenario: Ek legal case company k against, jo kaafi lamba chal raha tha.
Sawal: Agar yeh Key Audit Matter (KAM) hai, toh audit report me kaise likhna?
Steps:
Notice ka reference & relevant details likhni.
Draft example: “Fully disposal statement – legal case instituted against the company by competitor for corruption of matter & refresh whether matter has been adequately disposed.”
⚖️ Judgements is case me:
Applicability – Kya reporting date pe yeh matter applicable hai?
Impact – Auditor/client ko paisay denay parenge? Agar haan, kitne?
Outcome – Case jeetne ya harne ka chance.
📋 Writing ka tariqa:
Agar clearly KAM diya hua hai → likh do.
Apni personal opinion na do jaise “mere hisaab se KAM nahi hai” agar question ne bola hai ke hai.
Relevant factors include karo:
High significance.
Material to FS.
Significant event/transaction.
💡 Example points:
Judgement/Estimates → Legal applicability, amount ka impact, probability of loss.
Events/Transactions → Example: sudden data transfer by company → reconsider matter.
Materiality → Agar FS me material hai, toh KAM ban sakta hai.
📚 Practical Examples:
EFU General Insurance case → high FS materiality ki wajah se KAM.
Depreciation method change (SL → WDV) → assess karo agar KAM parameters meet karta ho.
📝 High Estimates & Complex Judgements
Sir ne IDNR example di to explain high estimates.
High estimate ka matlab: jab normal historical data + accidental rates + environmental rates sab combine ho, multiple estimates involved.
Complex judgement tab hota hai jab ek se zyada significant estimates involved hon.
Example: Projected crop price, yield, market rate — sab estimates.
Agar ek bhi galat hua → overall calculation impact.
Har estimate change = KAM nahi hota.
Example: Building cost change from 50 to another rate → normally expert ki zarurat nahi.
⚖️ Expert Involvement
Expert tab hire hoga jab matter company ke knowledge se beyond ho.
Example:
Tax case: Import par FTR apply kiya gaya.
PTCL ne challenge kiya → kaafi complex judgement matter.
Auditor ne taxation expert hire kiya to conclude sahi/galat.
Conclusion: Expert only where own expertise insufficient.
📋 Emphasis of Matter (EoM) Paragraph
Auditor ki judgement me agar koi matter fundamental to FS users’ understanding ho → EoM.
ISA 706 ke mutabiq:
EoM = Additional communication.
Purpose: Draw user attention to specific disclosed matter.
Disclosure vs Accounting:
Agar accounting honi chahiye lekin sirf disclosure di ja rahi hai → pehle accounting correct karo.
📚 Practical Examples from Lecture
PTCL pension case:
Litigation matter, fundamental to user understanding.
EoM paragraph add kiya.
Event after reporting date:
Example: 31 Dec FS, 28 Jan flood → significant inventory loss.
Auditor may refer via EoM if relevant & fundamental.
📆 Events After Reporting Date (EARD)
Definition: Jo events FS date (Financial Statements ki date) aur auditor’s report date ke beech hotay hain.
Types:
Adjusting Events – evidence dete hain ke FS date pe condition exist karti thi → FS me adjustment zaroori.
Non-adjusting Events – FS date ke baad ka condition show karte hain → sirf disclosure ka requirement ho sakta hai.
Example (Sir ka):
FS date: 31 Dec 2024.
28 Jan 2025: Flood ne kaafi inventory destroy kar di.
Auditor ka kaam: Check karo adjusting hai ya non-adjusting → zyada chance non-adjusting, lekin agar fundamental hai toh EoM lag sakta hai.
📌 Auditor’s Responsibility for EARD
Report date tak events monitor karo.
Management ke saath discuss karo.
Ensure karo ke accounting/disclosure sahi kiya gaya hai.
Agar management adjust/disclose karne se refuse kare jab zaroor ho → opinion modify karo.
🛠 Practical Application
Case: Auditor ko ek material litigation settlement ka pata FS date ke baad chalta hai.
Agar ye settlement ka link pre-year-end conditions se hai → adjusting event.
Agar ye sirf post-year-end originate hua hai → non-adjusting.
Judgement important hai: Agar material & fundamental hai → EoM consider karo.
⚖️ Link with ISA Standards
ISA 560 → Events after reporting date ka poora framework deta hai.
ISA 706 → Agar koi non-adjusting event properly disclose kiya gaya hai aur fundamental hai, toh EoM paragraph add kiya ja sakta hai.
🏭 Insurance Claims & Revenue Recognition Logic
Jo company motor insurance ka kaam karti hai, uske claim history dekho → Expected claims ko pehle se book kar lo, wait mat karo actual claim aane ka.
Past situation: Pehle revenue ko “night basis” pe book karte the jab policy start hoti thi.
Ab new method: Revenue se agent ka commission minus karo (jo policy laane ka kaam karta hai).
Net bacha → Net Service Revenue.
📅 Policy Term & Future Cash Flows
Dekho policy kitne saal chalegi (1, 2, 10 years).
Future cash flows ki present value nikalo.
Agar policy ke andar financing component hai → Interest income alag record karo (IFRS 15 ka concept).
Financing component ko revenue se hata ke separate treat karna hai.
🔄 Early Application of Standards
IAS/IFRS ka standard agar apni effective date se pehle adopt karein → Iska impact comparative FS pe bhi aata hai.
Comparability maintain karne ke liye pichle saalon ka data bhi restate karna padega.
Fundamental change hai to auditor ko user attention draw karni chahiye.
Early application = judgement call. Kabhi beneficial hoti hai taake market me pehle adapt ho jao.
⚠️ Loss-Making Contracts
New standards kehta hai: Agar contract first day se hi loss-making hai → Loss pehle din book karo (onerous contract treatment).
Insurance segment (e.g., motorcycle policies) agar loss me ho → Immediate recognition.
Standard ka aim: Future me surprises avoid karna.
📝 kAM (Key Audit Matters) vs EoM (Emphasis of Matter)
Agar matter already kAM hai to EoM me repeat nahi karna.
Early application agar fundamental hai → FS understanding ke liye EoM me mention ho sakta hai.
📆 Events After Reporting Date (Revisit)
FS date se report date tak auditor ki responsibility hoti hai events monitor karne ki.
Example:
FS date: 31 Dec 2024
Report sign: 15 Mar 2025 → Auditor ne is date tak ke events consider kiye.
15 Mar ke baad auditor ki responsibility normally khatam.
AGM notices ka timeline bhi calculate hota hai (e.g., 21 days rule for dispatch before AGM).
🏛 Post-Report Date – Facts Become Known to Auditor
Scenario: FS + Audit Report already issued to shareholders.
Key Point: Auditor ka role us waqt activate hota hai jab fact (material) auditor ko pata chale after report sign & FS issue.
Example from lecture:
Report sign: 6 March.
FS dispatched & available.
Baad me pata chala: Case ka decision aaya tha before report sign → management ne account for nahi kiya tha.
Agar pata hota at sign date → opinion modify hoti.
🛠 Auditor’s Required Action
Discuss with Management:
Event explain karao.
Management ko bolo: FS revise karo.
Sab stakeholders ko inform karo (jin ko FS mila).
SECP ya relevant regulator ko notify karo.
Revised FS & Audit Report:
Revised FS me note add karo: > “FS revised due to court case decision prior to report date which was not accounted earlier.”
New audit report issue karo (modified or updated as per new facts).
If Management Refuses:
Auditor ka duty: Governance ko inform karo.
Legal framework me escalate karo.
⚖️ ISA 560 Link
Yeh case Fact Becomes Known to Auditor After Date of Auditor’s Report ka classic example hai.
Agar fact ka effect FS pe fundamental hai → revision & re-issuance zaroori.
Agar fact non-fundamental hai → sirf disclosure / EoM ka option.
🚦 Special Notes from Lecture Discussion
Agar report abhi sign nahi hui → simple: adjust FS before issue.
Agar sign ho chuki & dispatch pending → revise report before dispatch.
“Once issued to users” → auditor ki responsibility only when fact is significant & impacts FS understanding.

📜 Scenario Overview: Yahan baat ho rahi hai ke auditor ko audit engagement accept karna chahiye ya nahi jab Financial Reporting Framework (FRF) acceptable na ho — chahe regulator ne audit karne ka keh diya ho. Sir ne clear kiya ke FRF ki acceptability pehle confirm karna zaroori hai, chahe wo general purpose ho ya special purpose framework ✅.
📌 Key Points:
FRF Acceptability Sabse Pehle:
Audit accept karne se pehle check karo ke FRF acceptable hai ya nahi.
Ye rule dono frameworks (general & special purpose) pe apply hota hai 📊.
Agar FRF acceptable nahi → audit accept nahi karna 🚫.
Regulator ka Kehna ≠ Standards Override:
Agar regulator kahe audit karo, lekin FRF acceptable nahi → standards ke mutabiq reject karo.
Auditor ko apni reputation protect karni hoti hai aur misleading FS se naam associate nahi karna ⚠️.
Agar Force Kar Diya Jaye (Regulatory Mandate):
Engagement letter me likho ke report me koi wording FRF ko acceptable nahi batayegi 📝.
Ek extra disclosure add karo jo bataye ke agar acceptable FRF hota to FS kaise hoti 📑.
Ye transparency maintain karne ke liye hota hai.
Impact on Opinion:
FRF ke flaws ki wajah se opinion modified hoga ✏️.
Basis for modification clearly explain karna hoga.
Sirf disclosure de dena opinion ko normal nahi banata.
Disclosure vs Modification:
Sirf batana ke sahi figures kya hote ≠ FRF ka issue solve karna.
Modification ka base hota hai framework ka flawed hona, sirf numbers ka galat hona nahi.
Professional Judgment & Ethics:
Agar management FRF fix na kare, auditor withdraw bhi kar sakta hai 🚪.
Standards pe compromise nahi karna, chahe regulator ya client pressurize kare 💪.
📜 Scenario Overview: Is part me focus hai ke auditor jab Special Purpose Framework (SPF) ke saath kaam kare to acceptance ke rules kaise apply hote hain. Sir ne highlight kiya ke SPF bhi acceptable hona chahiye.
📌 Key Points:
Special Purpose Framework ka Nature:
SPF ek specific audience ya purpose ke liye banta hai (e.g., tax return, regulatory reporting).
Example: Bank ke regulator ke liye banaya gaya FS jo IFRS se different hai 📊.
Acceptability Criteria SPF ke liye:
SPF acceptable tab hai jab:
Intended users ko pata ho ke SPF use ho raha hai.
SPF unke liye relevant ho.
SPF ki requirements transparent aur understandable ho 📑.
Agar SPF Acceptable Nahi:
Audit accept mat karo 🚫.
Agar regulator force kare → Part 2 jaisa approach follow karo:
Engagement letter me safeguards.
Modified opinion ✏️.
Extra disclosure.
SPF Me Opinion Wording:
Opinion me clearly mention karo ke framework special purpose hai.
Example phrase: “prepared in accordance with the special purpose framework of XYZ Regulator.”
Risk of Misunderstanding:
Agar FS public distribute honi hain to SPF ka use users ko confuse kar sakta hai ⚠️.
Auditor ka kaam hai clarity dena — taake koi ye na soche ke FS general purpose ke liye hain.
Professional Ethics SPF Me:
SPF audit bhi same ethical requirements follow karta hai jo general purpose me hoti hain 💪.
Auditor ko independence, objectivity, aur transparency maintain karni hoti hai.
📜 Scenario Overview: Is section me sir ne detail me bataya ke SPF audit report me kya kya extra cheezen highlight karni hoti hain, wording kaise change hoti hai aur kaunse safeguards aur disclosures zaroori hote hain ✅.

Report Ka Structure SPF Me:

Restriction on Use:
