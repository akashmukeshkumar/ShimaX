Lecture- 1
📚 Reference: Book Pages -
📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🌟 Main Concept:
Audit documentation ka matlab hai jo bhi auditor kaam perform karega, uska proper written proof maintain karna. Documentation ka main purpose yeh hai ke:
Evidence available ho ke auditor ne required procedures perform kiye.
Quality control review ke time proof mile.
Auditor apne professional responsibility justify kar sake.
🔑 Important Points:
1. Client Acceptance / Continuance Procedures
Before engagement start → Client ka background check kiya jata hai.
Owners
Directors
Senior management
Purpose → Auditor apko kisi aise entity ke sath associate nahi karna jo unethical practices me involve ho.
Yeh requirement Code of Ethics ki hai.
Agar unethical client ke sath associate karte ho → breach of professional principles (Integrity, Professional behavior).
📌 Assertion Hit: Auditor must not associate with misleading or false information.
2. Understanding the Entity & Its Environment
Entity ke business operations samajhne hote hain:
Imports / Exports
Financing arrangements
External environment → laws, regulations, exchange rate impact, etc.
Going concern issues assess karne hote hain.
Internal controls samajhne hote hain:
Sales cycle
Purchase cycle
Payroll cycle
Example: Agar credit limit approve nahi hoti → risk exist karta hai.
Yeh requirement ISA 315 / ISA 350 se linked hai.
📌 Assertion Hit: Understanding controls helps in risk identification.
3. Link with ICAP & Quality Control Review
ICAP official jab quality control review karega to yeh check karega:
Kya client acceptance procedures perform kiye gaye?
Kya background investigation documented hai?
Sirf keh dena “maine kiya tha” kaafi nahi hoga.
Proof → documented evidence hona chahiye.
Example: Owners ke LinkedIn screenshots, website checks, contracts attached in audit file.
📌 Risk Identified: If not documented → It will be assumed not performed.
🌟 Main Concept:
Audit documentation ka main maksad yeh hai ke auditor ke kaam ka evidence available ho. Agar documentation nahi hai to maana jata hai ke kaam perform hi nahi hua. Documentation auditor ko accountable banata hai aur ensures compliance with ISAs aur regulatory requirements.
🔑 Important Points:
1. Understanding the Business & Regulations
Auditor ko business operations, environment aur applicable laws samajhne hote hain.
Yeh samajhna zaroori hai kyunki:
Laws & regulations me change → financial statements par direct impact.
Example: Insurance company → agar tax rate change ho jaye → profit reduced.
Auditor ke paas iska documented proof hona chahiye ke usne:
Business operations samjhe.
Applicable regulations identify kiye.
Financial implications analyze kiye.
📌 Assertion Hit: Understanding & documenting laws/regulations ensures correct financial reporting.
2. Going Concern Documentation
Audit software (Canvas) auditor ko specific questions poochta hai:
Kya loans available hain?
Kya debt covenants exist karte hain?
Auditor procedure:
Debt covenants ko loan agreements se verify karna.
Amounts recalculate karna.
Ratios calculate karna (e.g., Interest coverage, Debt-to-equity).
Conclusion document karna → Not breached / Breached.
Yeh sab steps agar documented hain tabhi quality control reviewer maanega.
📌 Risk Identified: Agar recalculation aur ratios document na hon to conclusion unreliable hoga.
3. Accountability in Documentation
Har working paper me likhna zaroori hai:
Kis ne prepare kiya (Prepared by)
Kis ne review kiya (Reviewed by)
Example:
Excel sheet banayi → “Going Concern Sheet”.
Automated audit file → jese hi sheet attach hoti hai, preparer/reviewer initials visible hote hain.
Yeh ensure karta hai ke engagement team accountable hai.
📌 Assertion Hit: Prepared by & reviewed by ensures proper accountability.
4. Primary Purposes of Audit Documentation
Evidence ke liye → Audit procedures performed & conclusions reached ka proof.
Support for auditor’s report → Audit opinion ki base banta hai.
Assist in planning & performing audit → Engagement team ke liye reference.
Accountability → Auditor responsible hoga apne work ka.
Lecture- 2
📚 Reference: Book Pages -
📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔹 Concept Breakdown
Audit Documentation ka Purpose:
Evidence collect karna → jis base par auditor apni report deta hai.
Accountability → kis ne kaam kiya, kis ne review kiya, sab record hota hai.
Reference for future → independent reviewer easily samajh sake.
Materiality Documentation:
Materiality decide karne k liye 2 cheezein chahiye hoti hain:
Benchmark (basis of calculation)
Rate of materiality
Benchmark selection:
Depend karta hai users ke focus pe.
Agar listed profitable company hai → Profit Before Tax benchmark.
(Example: Listed Company → Profit Before Tax)
Agar NGO ya NPO hai → Expenses benchmark.
(Example: NGO → Total Expenses)
Documentation me clearly likhna hoga kyun ye benchmark choose kiya.
Risk: Agar benchmark galat select hua toh materiality misjudge ho sakti hai.
Assertion: Benchmark selection → overall assertions (accuracy, completeness, presentation) pe impact karta hai.
Rate of Materiality:
Depend karta hai Risk Assessment par.
Pehle audit year → Risk zyada hota hai → rate higher select hota hai.
(Example: First year audit → Higher % rate use hota hai)
Agar risk low hai toh rate low hoga.
Har rate ki justification documentation me required hai.
Risk: Agar rate sahi justify na ho → misstatement detect na hone ka chance.
Assertion: Mainly accuracy aur completeness assertions pe effect.
Documentation ka Rule:
Self-explanatory honi chahiye.
Kisi bhi independent third party (jo audit team ka part na ho) samajh sake.
Changes in materiality during audit → document with reasons.
Related Parties Documentation:
Auditor ko document karna hota hai:
Related party relationships
Transactions with related parties
Objective: ensure ki substance of transaction reflect ho financial statements me.
Risk: Agar related parties document na ho → disclosure misstatement ho sakta hai.
Assertion: Presentation & Disclosure assertion hit hota hai.
Main Concept: Audit Documentation (What to Document & Why)
🔹 What to Document
Evidence → jo auditor ko mila hai.
Procedure performed → jo kaam auditor ne kiya.
Conclusion → jo auditor ne evidence ke base par conclude kiya.
👉 Teeno cheezein (Procedure – Evidence – Conclusion) apas me match honi chahiye. Agar mismatch hua → accountability issue.
🔹 Example Flow
Auditor ne Interest Recalculation ki (Procedure).
Us se evidence mila → calculation theek hai ya difference hai.
Conclusion likha → “Interest amount FS me correct hai.”
Agar evidence aur conclusion match nahi karte → auditor ka kaam flawed hoga.
🔹 Benefits of Documentation
Accountability increase hoti hai.
Review & supervision easy ho jata hai.
Future reference ke liye kaam ata hai.
Easy for supervisor to check juniors’ work.
🔹 Example: Investment Audit
Step 1: Obtain Investment Register.
Company name
Quantity
Price
Total Value
Step 2: Mathematical accuracy check.
Step 3: Trace to GL → TB → FS.
Step 4: Use Audit ticks/legends (✔ = traced, ∑ = recalculated, etc.).
Step 5: Document procedure performed + attach evidence.
🔹 Example: Legal Case (Disclosure in FS)
Perform: Read legal correspondence.
Evidence: Emails, letters, call records.
Documentation: Attach PDF/email in audit file.
Conclusion: “Based on procedures performed, case is disclosed in FS.”
🔹 Confirmation Example
Confirmation bhejne ka proof document karna (Courier number, email copy).
Received reply bhi attach karna.
Without this → auditor ka kaam incomplete lagega.
🔹 Characteristics of Good Documentation
Facilitates Review → Supervisor ko easily samajh aye.
Helps Planning & Performing Audit → Risk assessment aur procedures clear hoti hain.
Supports Supervision → Evidence hota hai kaam hua.
Ensures Accountability → Team member ka kaam traceable hota hai.
Permanent Record → MOA, AOA, Loan agreements, etc. future ke liye rakhne hote hain.
Lecture- 3
📚 Reference: Book Pages -
📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
1. Audit Documentation ka Purpose
Audit documentation isliye zaroori hai kyonke agar koi review (ICAP ya koi third party) kare, to usay clear evidence milna chahiye ke auditor ne apna conclusion kis basis pe draw kiya.
Ye sirf accounting records ka part nahi hoti, balki independent audit ka apna record hota hai.
2. Audit Documentation ≠ Accounting Records
Example: Agar auditor ne loan verify karna hai → wo loan agreement lega, uska screenshot / copy apne working paper me attach karega.
Lekin auditor client ki banayi hui financial statements ko simply attach nahi karega as audit documentation.
Matlab: Audit documentation apni independent evidence hoti hai, accounting records ka substitute nahi.
Accounting records banane ki zimmedari management ki hai, auditor ki nahi.
3. Ownership, Custody & Confidentiality
Audit documentation ka ownership auditor ke paas hota hai.
Ye client ke accounting records ka part nahi banti.
Auditor bound hota hai confidentiality agreement se:
Koi personal ya third-party fayde ke liye confidential audit info use nahi karni.
Audit firm ka system (portal) aisa hota hai ke sirf relevant team members hi files dekh sakte hain.
Example: EY ne 2022 me ABC company ka audit kiya, agle saal KPMG auditor bana → KPMG ne professional clearance letter likha aur working papers dekhne ki request ki. Lekin auditor bina client ki permission ke working papers share nahi kar sakta.
4. Retention Period (Kitne Time Tak Rakhnay Hain)
Minimum retention period = 5 years (standard requirement).
Lekin firm ki apni policy longer ho sakti hai.
Matlab: Auditor ko decide karna hai ke working papers kitni der tak confidential rakhega.
5. Examples of Audit Documentation
Loan agreement copies.
PPE ki valuation aur disposal ki calculation evidence.
Impairment models (cash flow projections, assumptions, judgments).
Board approvals aur restructuring plans ke supporting documents.
6. Assembly of Audit File
Jab audit report sign hoti hai (e.g., 28th date), uske baad audit file assemble karni hoti hai.
File properly organize, safe aur secure honi chahiye.
1. Assembly of Audit Documentation
Jab audit complete ho jata hai → sab team members ke working papers collect karke assemble karna hota hai.
Assembly ka matlab: sirf organize karna, format proper karna, numbering dena (e.g., P1 = PPE, R1 = Receivables, I1 = Inventory).
Important: Assembly ke dauran koi signature ya date change nahi honi chahiye.
Draft/Incomplete papers assembly me allow nahi hote.
2. Working Paper Structure
Har working paper ka ek proper structure hota hai:
Lead sheet → FS amount (BS/PL figures).
Breakup schedules → details (e.g., Receivables list, PPE breakdown).
Confirmation control sheet → kis party ko confirmation bheji, date of request, date of response, balance confirmation.
Exceptions/Deficiencies → agar koi difference ya inquiry raise hui ho.
Purpose: koi bhi third party auditor easily samajh sake ke kaam kaise hua.
3. Timeline for Assembly
Audit file report signing date ke 60 days ke andar assemble karni hoti hai.
Is time ke baad assembly changes allowed nahi.
4. Retention & Finalization
Assembly complete hone ke baad → file final ho jati hai.
Auditor ko minimum 5 years tak audit documentation retain karna hota hai.
Once finalized → delete, discard ya modify nahi kar sakte, except specific circumstances.
5. Modification of Audit Documentation (Special Case)
Normally, post-report modification allowed nahi hota.
Exception: agar koi information ya document report sign ke baad discover hota hai (e.g., misstatement ya missing expert report).
Conditions for modification:
Clearly document: When (date), Who (auditor/partner), Why (reason).
Partner approval required.
Original report ke pehle date ka evidence attach karna (emails, expert report, etc.).
Example: EY case → Expert valuation report attach reh gaya tha, later added with partner approval aur justification.
6. Risk with Modification
Audit opinion distort na ho.
Agar nayi information financial statements ko materially impact karti hai → management se adjust karwana zaroori hai.
Example: Debtor misstatement discovered after report date → management ko revise FS karni hongi, auditor ko disclose karna hoga.
