📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔹 Intro to Materiality
Sir ne start kiya with: Materiality ek judgmental concept hai. ⚖️
Auditor decide karta hai kaunsa information itna important hai ke uska FS users ke decisions pe effect ho sakta hai. 🧠
Matlab: “Koi bhi chhoti chhoti galti matter nahi karti jab tak wo users ke decision ko affect na kare.”
📖 Definition (ISA 320)
Misstatements material hain agar wo FS users ke economic decisions ko influence karen jo wo FS pe rely karke lete hain. 💹
🏷️ Key Points Sir ne Highlight kiye
Materiality = Relative concept (depends on size & nature of misstatement). 📊
Ek misstatement chhoti ho sakti hai lekin agar wo law/regulation break kare → material ban jaati hai. ⚠️
Judgment ka role bohot zyada hai (auditor ki experience pe depend karta hai). 👨‍💼

🔹 Types of Materiality
Sir ne teen main types discuss ki:
Overall Materiality (Financial Statements level)
FS ke liye ek overall threshold set hota hai. 📊
Agar misstatements is threshold se zyada ho → FS unreliable ho jati hain. 🚨
Performance Materiality(tolerable error)
Overall materiality se lower amount rakha jata hai.
Kyun? Taake chance kam ho ke aggregate misstatements overall threshold cross kar jayein. ⚖️
Example: Agar overall materiality = 10 million → performance materiality ≈ 7 million.
Specific Materiality
Jab particular area/accounts zyada sensitive ho (e.g. related party transactions, directors’ remuneration). 💼
Unke liye specific materiality set karte hain (jo overall se alag hoti hai).
⚠️ Sir ka Note
Materiality fix number nahi hota, circumstances change hoti hain to adjust karna parta hai. 🔄
Example: Company ka profit decline kare → materiality ka level bhi adjust karna parta hai.

🔹 Factors Affecting Materiality
Sir ne bataya ke auditor materiality decide karte waqt kuch factors consider karta hai:
Size of Entity (Quantitative factor)
Badi company → higher materiality
Chhoti company → lower materiality
Example: 5 lakh ka misstatement ek small company ke liye huge hai, lekin ek multinational ke liye negligible. 📊
Nature of Item (Qualitative factor)
Kuch cheezein chhoti amount ke bawajood material ho sakti hain.
Eg: Fraud, related party transactions, law/regulation violations. 🚨
Circumstances of Entity
Agar company loss me hai → materiality zyada strict rakhi jati hai. ⚖️
Agar stable aur profitable hai → materiality relatively higher ho sakti hai.
Needs of FS Users
Users ko sabse zyada kahan interest hai (profit, revenue, assets, etc.) auditor ye bhi dekhta hai. 👀
📝 Sir ka Example
Agar ek small NGO ka audit ho raha hai → even small misstatements material ho sakte hain kyunke public trust ka issue hai. 🙌
🔹 How to Determine Materiality (Methods)
Sir ne bataya ke materiality mostly benchmarks pe based hoti hai:
Profit Before Tax (PBT)
Common benchmark → % of PBT.
Usually 5% – 10% of PBT liya jata hai. 💰
Revenue
Jab profit stable nahi hota (loss-making co.) → % of revenue use karte hain.
Normally 0.5% – 1% of revenue. 📈
Total Assets
Specially asset-based companies (e.g. investment co., banks).
1% – 2% of total assets. 🏦
Equity
Sometimes materiality set hoti hai as a % of equity.
Example: 1% – 2% of equity. 📊
📝 Sir ka Note
Koi fixed rule nahi hai. Auditor apni judgment + firm policy use karta hai. ⚖️
Risk zyada ho → conservative (lower) materiality set karte hain.
