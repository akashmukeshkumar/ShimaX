Lecture- 1

📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
**Concept Breakdown **
ISA 210 → Relates to Audit Engagement Letter.
Engagement Letter = Agreement before starting audit.
It defines Terms of Engagement = kis base pe audit hoga.
Engagement type = Audit Engagement.
Why Engagement Letter is Important?
Accountability → Responsibility fix ho jati hai (auditor + management dono ki).
Responsibilities Clear → Auditor ka role = Audit karna, Management ka role = FS prepare karna.
Dispute Resolution → Agar koi disagreement aaye to resolve karne ka tareeqa likha hota hai.
Scope Limitation Avoid → Agar management mana kare records provide karne se → Audit start hi nahi hoga.
Signing Time: Before Start of Audit (not after).
Key Points in Engagement Letter
Management will prepare Financial Statements (FS).
Management will implement internal controls.
Management will provide records & data.
Management will give access to legal counsel / staff if required.
Auditor will perform audit only, not prepare FS (independence maintained).
Ethical Aspect (Code of Ethics – Self Review Threat)
Auditor should not prepare FS, warna self-review threat.
Isiliye engagement letter mein likha jata hai:
FS → Management banayegi
Audit → Auditor karega
Scope Limitation Example
Agar management bole: “Hum FS nahi banayenge ya records nahi denge” → Audit possible hi nahi.
Ye situation Scope Limitation before audit kehlati hai.
Is case mein auditor → Withdraw consider karega.
Practical Example (Sir ka Example)
Marriage analogy: Nikkah sign hi nahi hua aur banda keh raha “main talaq de raha hoon” → Invalid.
Same way, agar Engagement Letter sign nahi hua aur management cooperate nahi karti → Audit proceed nahi karega.
Client Acceptance vs Engagement Letter
Client Acceptance Stage → Background check hota hai (criminal activity, fraud risk, etc.).
Agar client suspicious ho → Auditor engagement letter tak hi nahi jata.
Acceptance Cycle (High Level)
Client Acceptance Procedures → Background check, ethics check, risk assessment.
Engagement Letter → Formal agreement of terms.
Audit Engagement → Work starts jab terms accept ho jayein.
Audit Engagement = Foundation
Audit start hi ni hota jab tak terms pe agreement na ho.
Engagement Letter audit ka base/pemāish hai.
Agar yeh na ho → scope limitation aur audit invalid.
Management’s Responsibilities (Engagement Letter me agree hoti hain)
A. Prepare Financial Statements (FS). B. Implement internal controls. C. Provide data aur records. D. Access dena to people (legal counsel, staff, etc.).
Scope Limitation Before Audit
Agar management refuse kare provide karne se:
FS,
Records,
Ya access → to Scope Limitation hota hai.
Aise case me auditor ko withdraw karna chahiye before audit start.
Framework Agreement (Sabse Critical Point)
Framework = FS preparation ka base.
Agar framework acceptable ni hai → poore FS unreliable.
Auditor ko ensure karna hota hai ke framework acceptable ho before engagement.
Kyun Important hai?
Audit opinion FS ko compare karta hai vs acceptable framework.
Agar framework hi na ho → valid opinion possible ni.
How to Check if Framework is Acceptable?
Auditor 3 cheezen check karta hai:
Nature of Business
Example: Bank, Insurance, Manufacturing.
Har business pe apni laws/regulations apply hoti hain (e.g., Insurance Rules 2017 Pakistan me).
Information Needs of Users
General users (public/shareholders) → IFRS/Companies Act framework.
Specific users (tax authorities, regulators) → Special purpose framework ho sakta hai.
Law & Regulation
Agar law ne specific reporting prescribe ki ho → framework comply kare.
Example: Pakistan me Company FS under Companies Act + IFRS.
Special Purpose Frameworks
Jab FS sirf specific users ke liye banaye jate hain (e.g., FBR, lenders).
Yeh engagement general purpose audit se different hota hai.
Auditor ko report me disclose karna hota hai ke framework special purpose hai.
Lecture- 2
📚 Reference: Book Pages -
📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
Background & Importance
Engagement letter ** لازمی hota hai** before audit start.
Ye accountability create karta hai → auditor aur client ki responsibilities clear hoti hain.
Agar koi dispute aaye toh iska resolution letter me likha hota hai.
Ye basically ek written agreement hota hai jo audit start hone se pehle sign hota hai.
Why Engagement Letter is Essential?
Responsibilities clear karna:
Client banayega financial statements (FS), auditor nahi.
Client karega controls implement against fraud/error.
Client provide karega information & records access.
Clarity of duties aajati hai → confusion avoid hota hai.
Auditor ko legal protection milta hai in case of conflict.
Acceptability of Financial Reporting Framework
Audit lene se pehle auditor check karega ke framework acceptable hai ya nahi. Factors:
Entity ka nature & business
Objective of FS
Users ki needs
Applicable law & regulations
Examples:
Company IFRS follow kar rahi hai → acceptable.
Agar sirf taxation authority ke liye FS bane aur IFRS compliant na ho → not acceptable.
Insurance co. → unka apna regulatory framework hota hai jo follow karna parta hai.
👉 Agar framework not acceptable ho toh auditor engagement accept nahi karega (except agar law force kare). Us case me auditor additional disclaimers likhta hai.
Preconditions of Audit
Ye wo minimum conditions hain jo audit accept karne se pehle honi chahiye.
Agar ye preconditions na hon toh audit start hi nahi hoga.
Examples:
Client accept kare responsibility FS banane ki.
Client agree kare necessary records dene ke liye.
Reporting framework acceptable ho.
Contents of Engagement Letter
Objective & Scope of Audit
Objective → auditor ka purpose hai opinion dena ke FS material misstatement se free hain ya nahi.
Scope → sirf FS tak limited hota hai.
Clear kar diya jata hai ke auditor:
Internal controls pe opinion nahi dega.
Director’s report pe opinion nahi dega.
Auditor kaam karega professional judgement se, lekin har misstatement detect karna possible nahi hota (inherent limitations of audit).
⚠️ Important: Audit dete hai reasonable assurance, absolute assurance nahi.
Applicable Financial Reporting Framework
Letter me framework specify hota hai (e.g., IFRS, Companies Act Pakistan).
Misstatements usi framework ke against judge hongi.
If Framework Not Acceptable
Auditor karega:
Disclaimers add
Alternative treatments suggest
Warn karega agar FS misleading ban rahi ho
Fundamental Understanding
Engagement letter ka content user ki understanding ke liye fundamental hota hai.
Auditor kabhi aise contradictory words use nahi karega jaise:
Ek jaga disclosure dal raha hai taake misleading na ho.
Aur dusri jaga “true and fair view” likh raha hai.
Yeh contradictory ban jata hai – isliye auditor ko clarity chahiye.
Legal / Regulatory Requirement
Agar law / regulation force kare ke “true and fair view” likhna hi hoga → phir auditor majboor hoga likhne ke liye.
Auditor apni protection ke liye disclosure / emphasis dalta hai taake clear ho ke uska association misleading FS se na ho.
Lekin agar law require kare → phir likhna compulsory hai, chahe auditor ki preference kuch aur ho.
Example (Insurance Case)
Insurance company ka audit tha jahan regulator keh raha tha:
Batao kitna claim future me hoga.
Auditor limitation me hai kyunke:
Usko nahi pata kaunsa accident hoga.
Kaunsa engine kharab hoga.
Ya building collapse hogi ya nahi.
Auditor sirf estimates based on historical trends bata sakta hai, future ka exact nahi.
Yeh scope limitation hai, isliye “true and fair view” ka lafz problematic ho sakta hai, jab tak law enforce na kare.
Content so far in Engagement Letter
Objective & Scope of Audit
Auditor ka objective: opinion dena FS par.
Scope limited hai to FS, not beyond (e.g., internal control opinion nahi).
Applicable Financial Reporting Framework
Pehle conclude karna zaroori hai ke framework acceptable hai ya nahi.
Agar acceptable nahi → auditor additional disclaimers dalta hai.
Agar law/regulation force kare → phir auditor comply karega.
Responsibilities of Auditor
Auditor ki responsibility hoti hai:
Sufficient & appropriate audit evidence gather karna.
Estimates check karna (reasonable hain ya nahi).
Policies properly applied hain ya nahi.
FS presentation fair hai ya nahi.
Opinion dena based on evidence.
Auditor ka role = verify, examine, and express opinion, not prepare FS.
Responsibilities of Management
FS prepare karna using applicable framework.
Internal controls design & implement karna.
Going concern ka assessment karna.
Provide all access to info, docs, persons (e.g., CEO, CFO, directors, staff).
Ensure disclosures complete hain.
🔹 Reporting & Reference
Auditor client ko yeh clear karta hai ke report ka format ISA 700 ke mutabiq hoga 📑.
Lekin ⚠️ agar situation change ho jaye to report modify bhi ho sakti hai (jaise: EOM, Other Matter, KAMs).
Auditor yeh reference deta hai ke “report will be as per applicable framework” ✅.
Matlab: standard format follow hoga, lekin special circumstances mey changes aa sakti hain 🔄.
🔹 Minimum Content
Objective 🎯
Scope 📌
Applicable Financial Reporting Framework 📊
Responsibilities of Auditor & Management 👨‍💼👨‍💻
Reference to Report format (ISA 700) 📑
Ye sab minimum content hamesha hota hai aur compulsory hota hai ✔️.
🔹 Additional Content (Optional, situation based)
Audit logistics ka arrangement 🏢 (branch visits, access controls, sitting arrangement).
Experts ka involvement 👨‍🔬 (e.g. fair value of investment property → external valuer use karna).
Subsequent Events info 📅 (management auditor ko inform karegi agar FS date ke baad but authorization se pehle koi event ho).
Communication with Previous Auditor 📞 (first year audit mey → client se permission leni hoti hai predecessor auditor ko contact karne ke liye).
Mode of Communication 💬 (email, meetings, written letters → clear channel define karna).
🔹 Example (Pakistan context)
Pakistan Companies Act mey kuch additional disclosure requirements hoti hain jo ISA format mey nahi hoti 🏛️.
Auditor unko bhi include karega kyunki law/regulation hamesha override karta hai.
🔹 Auditor Requests & Information Flow
Auditor client se specific information request karta hai jaise:
Fixed Asset Register 🏭
Loan Commitment Details 💸
Loan Agreement 📑
Additional schedules & supporting documents 📂
Client mana nahi kar sakta kyunki engagement letter me likha hota hai ke management will provide all required info ✅.
Lekin engagement letter me yeh detail nahi likhi hoti ke mode of providing data kya hoga (USB, email, etc). ⚡
🔹 Modern Communication & Confidentiality
Ab auditors interactive systems use karte hain:
Client ko secure link diya jata hai 🔗.
Client jitna bhi data upload karega → directly auditor ki file me ajata hai 📥.
Data secure & confidential hota hai 🔒.
Manual USB exchange avoid hota hai 🚫💽.
System me automatic checks hote hain:
Agar client incomplete file bhejta hai → system auto reject karke email notification bhej deta hai 📧.
Isse documentation complete aur verified hoti hai ✅.
🔹 Additional Scope Wali Clauses
Auditor apne scope ko aur clarify kar sakta hai:
Internal controls ka understanding ayegi, lekin opinion issue nahi hoga ⚠️.
Limitation of audit likhna zaroori hai → auditor har error detect nahi kar sakta 🔍.
Communication levels fix karna:
Agar misstatement mile to pehle management se baat hogi 👨‍💼.
Phir governance walon se 👔.
Last step escalation protocols.
🔹 Fee & Timeliness
Auditor yeh clause add kar sakta hai ke:
Agar management data time pe provide nahi karegi → fees increase ho sakti hai 💰.
Isse auditor ki protection hoti hai against unnecessary delays ⏳.
🔹 Working Papers & Regulator Access
Auditor apne working papers kisi bhi regulator ko dikhane ka right rakhta hai 👀.
Client mana nahi kar sakta.
Confidentiality management ke sath compromise nahi hoti, lekin law/regulator demand hoga to provide karna zaroori hai ⚖️.
🔹 Liability Limitation
Engagement letter me auditor apni liability restrict kar leta hai 🛑.
Agar audit ke zariye koi adjustment aata hai aur client ka nuksan hota hai → auditor responsible nahi hoga ❌.
Yeh clause auditor ko unnecessary lawsuits se protect karta hai 🛡️.

🔹 Concept Breakdown
Recording Audit:
Matlab: Jab previous year bhi same client tha, auditor continues kar raha hota hai.
First year = Client Acceptance.
Next year = Client Continuance (not fresh acceptance).
Engagement Letter:
Drafted in English, signed each year as continuation.
Remains same unless significant change occurs.
🔹 Examples Table
🔹 Concept Breakdown
1. Client Request for Change in Scope:
Client may request: Audit → Review.
Reason check karni hai:
✅ Reasonable (e.g., Bank only needs review).
❌ Not reasonable (e.g., Fraud hide karna, evidence avoid karna).
2. Scope Limitation Risk:
Agar client audit se bachne ke liye review maange → unacceptable.
Law mein likha hai: Certain entities ko audit karwana hi hai → client choice nahi.
3. Auditor Response:
Agar request reasonable → Revise Terms of Engagement.
Agar request unreasonable (scope limitation) → Reject / Withdraw.
Agar client still refuse → Consider legal responsibility (Regulator ko inform karna etc.).
4. Use of Previous Knowledge:
Previous audit ka verified data re-use kiya ja sakta hai.
Dubara wahi kaam repeat karna zaroori nahi.
5. Pre-Conditions of Audit:
Acceptable financial reporting framework.
Management agreement to:
Prepare FS.
Implement internal controls.
Provide necessary information.
Agar yeh preconditions absent hain → Auditor cannot accept engagement.
