Lecture- 1
📚 Reference: Book Pages -
📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔹 Introduction
Code of Ethics = ICAP ka professional code jo auditors aur accountants ko guidelines deta hai kaise behave karna hai. ⚖️
Purpose: ensure integrity, objectivity, independence, and professional behavior.
🔹 Importance of Ethics
Public Trust – Audit profession ka main asset = logon ka trust. 🤝
Credibility of FS – Agar auditor ethical hai to financial statements reliable hoti hain. 📊
Reputation of Profession – Ethics follow karna = overall profession ki izzat banti hai. 🌍
🔹 Why Need for Ethics?
Sir ne bataya ke auditor bohot sensitive role play karta hai: - Auditor = Public Interest Guardian. - Agar ethical na ho to → misstatements ignore kar dega → users ka trust toot jata hai. ❌
🔹 Fundamental Principles of Ethics
Sir ne 5 core principles explain kiye jo har auditor follow karta hai:
Integrity
Hamesha honest & straightforward rehna.
Kisi ko intentionally mislead na karna. ✅
Objectivity
Bias, conflict of interest, undue influence avoid karna.
Decision-making should be fair. ⚖️
Professional Competence & Due Care
Apni knowledge update rakhna (CPD).
Kaam diligence aur care ke sath karna. 📚
Confidentiality
Client ka data unauthorized logon ko disclose na karna.
Sirf legal/professional duty ho to disclose karna. 🤐
Professional Behavior
Law aur regulations ka respect karna.
Profession ki reputation ko damage na karna. 🌍
🔹 Conceptual Framework Approach
Sir ne explain kiya ke ICAP Code ek conceptual framework approach follow karta hai. 🧩
Matlab: auditor ko har situation me ethical threats evaluate karni hain aur unke liye safeguards apply karne hain.
🔹 Ethical Threats (5 categories)
Self-interest threat – Auditor ka apna financial ya personal interest compromise kar de objectivity. 💰
Example: Client ka shares hold karna.
Self-review threat – Auditor apne hi kaam ko dubara audit kare. 🔄
Example: FS prepare karna aur phir unka audit karna.
Advocacy threat – Auditor client ke interest ko promote kare. 📢
Example: Client ke case me court me represent karna.
Familiarity threat – Client ke sath zyada closeness → independence compromise. 🤝
Example: Family member client me senior position pe ho.
Intimidation threat – Jab client pressure dalta hai auditor par. 😠
Example: "Agar qualified opinion diya to contract cancel."
🔹 Safeguards Against Threats
Sir ne bataya ke ethical threats ko handle karne ke liye 2 types of safeguards hote hain:
Safeguards Created by Profession, Law or Regulation
Education, training & CPD 📚
Corporate governance requirements ⚖️
Professional standards & monitoring 🔍
Safeguards in Work Environment
Firm ki policies & procedures 🏢
Rotation of staff 🔄
Independent quality reviews ✅
🔹 When to Decline/Withdraw
Agar safeguards apply karne ke baad bhi threat eliminate nahi hota → auditor should decline or withdraw from engagement. ❌
🔹 Independence in Audit Engagements
Sir ne focus kiya ke independence = most critical requirement for auditor. 🛡️
Types of Independence:
Independence of Mind
Ability to give opinion without bias, influence ya pressure. 🧠
Independence in Appearance
Public perception bhi matter karta hai. Auditor ko aisa lagna chahiye ke wo independent hai. 👀
🔹 Importance of Independence
Independence lost = auditor ki credibility khatam. ❌
Financial statements ka trust chala jata hai. 📉
Sir ne bola ke independence ≈ backbone of audit quality.

🔹 Integrity (Principle 1)
Integrity = honesty & straightforwardness in all professional & business relationships. ⚖️
Sir ne bola: koi misleading information ya false statement nahi honi chahiye.
Example: Client ki FS me jaan bujh kar figures misstate karna → integrity breach. ❌
🔹 Objectivity (Principle 2)
Objectivity = no bias, conflict of interest, undue influence. 🎯
Auditor ko apna judgment independent aur neutral rakhna hai.
Example: Agar auditor ka close friend client me finance manager hai → risk of bias. 🤝
🔹 Professional Competence & Due Care (Principle 3)
Competence = apni knowledge & skills ko continuously update karna. 📚
Due care = apni professional duty ko diligence & care ke sath perform karna. ⚡
Sir ne stress diya: outdated knowledge use karna bhi ethics ka violation hai.
Example:
Agar auditor IFRS ke latest changes ko ignore kare → FS me errors → due care ka breach. ❌
Sir ka Ufone Example:
Ufone ke auditor ne apni knowledge update nahi ki regarding new accounting standard → FS me misstatement → professional competence & due care ka clear violation. 📱
🔹 Confidentiality (Principle 4)
Auditor ko client ki information secret rakhni hoti hai. 🔒
Without client consent, kisi ko bhi disclose nahi karna (except legal/professional duty). ⚖️
Allowed Disclosure Cases:
Legal requirement (e.g., court order, law enforcement).
Professional duty (e.g., regulatory authority ko report karna).
Example:
Client ki fraud activity agar law enforcement ko report karna mandatory hai → allowed disclosure. ✅
🔹 Professional Behavior (Principle 5)
Requirement: Accountant ko aisa act karna chahiye jo profession ki good reputation ko maintain kare. 🌐
Har aise kaam se bachna jo bad image create kare profession ki.
Examples Sir ne diye: 1. False claims in CV ❌
- Agar accountant apni CV me aise experience ya degree mention kare jo actually nahi hai → ye unethical aur profession ki reputation ko damage karta hai.
Misleading advertisement 📢❌
Agar accountant apni services ke bare me exaggerated claims kare (jo actually provide hi nahi karta) → ye bhi professional behavior ka violation hai.

Law kehta hai: “Don’t drive above 100 km/h.”
Agar koi banda 99.9 km/h pe chala raha hai sirf loophole ka faida lene ke liye → technically legal hai, lekin ethically wrong hai.

🔹 Threats to Fundamental Principles
Sir ne bataya ke accountants ko har waqt apne decisions aur actions me in threats ka khatra hota hai:
Self-interest threat 💰
Jab personal gain ya financial interest professional judgment ko influence kare.
Example: Client company ke shares own karna jiska audit kar rahe ho.
Self-review threat 🔄
Jab accountant apna khud ka kaam review kare jo pehle kiya tha.
Example: Pehle bookkeeping ki, ab audit bhi wahi karein.
Advocacy threat 📣
Jab accountant client ka representative ban jaye in such a way ke objectivity compromise ho jaye.
Example: Court me client ka case defend karna.
Familiarity threat 👥
Jab close relationship client ke sath professional skepticism ko kam kar de.
Example: Family member ka audit karna.
Intimidation threat 😨
Jab client ya koi aur undue pressure dale aur accountant ko apna judgment compromise karna pade.
Example: Client bole “agar aisa opinion diya to contract cancel kar denge.”
🔹 Safeguards to Reduce Threats
Sir ne bola: har threat ko eliminate ya reduce karne ke liye safeguards use hote hain. Ye 2 categories me hotay hain:
Professional, Legal & Regulatory Safeguards ⚖️
Professional standards follow karna.
Laws & regulations jo accountant ki independence protect karte hain.
Professional body ke monitoring aur disciplinary procedures.
Work Environment Safeguards 🏢
Firm ke internal policies aur procedures.
Quality control systems.
Independent review by another professional.
Sir ka Example (Safeguard in action): 🔎
Agar ek client apne auditor pe pressure dale ke “ye cheez ignore kar do warna contract khatam.”
Safeguard: auditor firm ke seniors ko involve kare, ya legal requirement follow kare → taake independence aur objectivity protect ho jaye.

📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
Exam task recap: “Case ko dekh kar identify karo: kaun‑sa threat nazar aa raha hai, aur kaun‑sa fundamental principle effect hota hai. Sirf case ki lines use karo; koi extra story nahi.”
🎯 Situation (as given)
Audit team member ko audit client se job offer milti hai jab woh same client ki engagement par kaam kar raha hota hai. Offer ko le kar pressure bhi ho sakta hai (e.g., “agar compliant na hue to offer withdraw”).
Rule: Situation ko copy-accurate likho; invent mat karo.
✍️ Drafting Technique (step‑by‑step)
State situation → case ki exact wording.
Identify threat(s) → naam likho (no guessing beyond case).
Explain why → 1 line me mechanism: “mighty/likely/there is a risk that …”.
Link to principles → kaun‑sa principle affect hota hai (reason with 3–6 words).
Keep it tight → bas itna hi; extra theory nahi.
🧠 Apply on this case
1) Self‑Interest Threat ⚠️
Why arises? Audit team member job secure karne ke liye misstatements overlook/soft stance le sakta hai.
Model line:
“This creates a Self‑Interest Threat, as the audit team member might overlook misstatements in order to secure the position.”
Principles affected:
Integrity (honesty/straightforwardness compromise) ➜ “may not be fully honest,”
Objectivity (bias) ➜ “judgment may become biased due to personal benefit.”
2) Intimidation Threat 🚨
Why arises? Perceived pressure / “offer withdraw” type threat → auditor objectively act nahi kar pata.
Model line:
“This creates an Intimidation Threat, due to pressure that the offer could be withdrawn if the auditor does not comply.”
Principles affected:
Objectivity (pressure impairs unbiased judgment),
Integrity (may feel compelled to act against being straightforward).
❗ Don’t over‑claim (Familiarity note)
Familiarity Threat tabhi likho jab case explicitly close/long association, family/close relationship batae. Agar case me yeh nahi likha, mat add karo.
🧩 Ready‑to‑use mini answer (copy style)
Since an audit team member working on the audit has received an employment offer from the audit client, this creates a Self‑Interest Threat (the member might overlook misstatements to secure the position) and an Intimidation Threat (perceived pressure that the offer could be withdrawn). These threaten the principles of Integrity (not being fully honest/straightforward) and Objectivity (judgment may become biased).
🎯 Situation (as per case)
Audit team member audit client ka case me court/authority represent karta hai (client ke behalf arguments deta hai).
Rule: Sirf case ki lines; no add‑ons.
🧠 Apply on this case
Advocacy Threat 🎤
Why arises? Audit team member client ke position ko defend kar raha hai → independence/objectivity compromise hoti hai.
Model line:
“This creates an Advocacy Threat, as the auditor is representing the client’s case and may become associated with their position.”
Principles affected:
- Objectivity ➜ auditor ki judgment biased ho sakti hai.
- Integrity ➜ impartiality/straightforwardness undermine hoti hai.
🧩 Ready‑to‑use mini answer
Since an audit team member is representing the audit client in a case, this creates an Advocacy Threat, as the auditor may become associated with the client’s position. This threatens the principle of Objectivity (judgment may be biased) and Integrity (impartiality compromised).
🎯 Situation (case line)
Audit team member:
➡️ Accounting records bana raha hai for client (yaani preparing financial info)
➡️ Aur phir audit bhi wohi records ka khud karega.
🧠 Apply on this case
Self‑review Threat 🔎
Why arises? Auditor apna hi kaam review kar raha hoga → independence/objectivity seriously damage hoti hai.
Model line:
“This creates a Self‑review Threat, as the auditor would be auditing financial records which they themselves prepared.”
Principles affected:
- Objectivity ➜ unbiased judgment impossible.
- Professional competence & due care ➜ compromised (auditor ka role distort hota hai).
🧩 Ready‑to‑use mini answer
Since the auditor is preparing accounting records and then auditing the same records, this creates a Self‑review Threat, as the auditor would be reviewing their own work. This threatens Objectivity (judgment biased) and Professional competence & due care (role distortion).

Concepts Discussed:
Fee Issue (Less than Previous Auditor) 🚫💰
Rule: Kabhi bhi previous auditor se kam fee charge nahi karni chahiye.
Reason: Self-interest threat
Principle hit: Integrity & Objectivity compromise ho sakti hai.
Documentation & Control Testing 📝🔍
Review karte waqt auditor ko clearly likhna chahiye how control was tested.
Example: Billing manager report generate karta hai → Auditor should check reconciliation between system aur report.
Documentation ka maksad: clarity + support for audit evidence.
Fraud Risk Example (PTCL) ⚠️📊
Client handle karta hai millions ka sensitive data.
Agar weak controls hain → Fraud risk high hoga.
Auditor ko specific risk areas identify karna aur unko test karna zaroori hai.
Principle: Professional Competence & Due Care
Family-owned Business Controls 👨‍👩‍👦🏢
Close family members jab governance aur operations handle karte hain → Weak Control Environment.
Auditor ko skepticism rakhna hoga kyunke override of controls ho sakta hai.
Yahaan sirf auditor ki integrity apply hoti hai, client ki nahi.
Advocacy Threat ⚖️🗣️
Definition: Jab auditor client ki position ko itna promote kare ke apni objectivity compromise ho jaye.
Example: Court case me client ki taraf se defend karna → Later apni hi judgement ko challenge karna mushkil ho jata hai.
Principle hit: Objectivity
Rotation / Movement of Staff 🔄👔
Agar ek staff member client side se auditor firm join kare (ya reverse) → Risk depends on:
Uski position level
Uska time period kab tak client ke sath tha
Iska label hamesha depend karta hai position + timing par.
Inadvertent Error (Minor Mistakes) ✏️❌
Kabhi kabhi auditor ki taraf se choti mistakes ho jati hain (like calculation ya overlook).
Yeh integrity compromise nahi karta, lekin still due care ka requirement hai.
Example: Mis-typing amount in working paper.
FRC (Financial Reporting Council) Report 📑🔍
FRC ne observe kiya ke bohot auditors properly document nahi karte controls aur risk assessment.
Documentation ka role: Provide audit evidence aur show karta hai auditor ka process.
Kya Note Karna Zaroori Hai? 📝
Control identified kaise hua?
Test of control ka procedure kya tha?
Results kya aaye aur kya conclusion nikla?
Sir ne emphasize kiya: “If it’s not documented → it’s not done.” 🚨
Fraud vs Error 🔎⚖️
Error: Intentional nahi hota (mistake).
Fraud: Deliberate misstatement hota hai.
Auditor ko dono ke liye skeptical rehna zaroori hai.
Threats Reminder ⚠️
Self-interest threat: Kam fee lena / financial benefit.
Advocacy threat: Client ki taraf strongly defend karna.
Familiarity threat: Staff rotation na hone ki wajah se close relation build ho jana.
Audit Evidence & Controls 🛡️📂
Effective audit ke liye sirf inquiry enough nahi hai.
Documentation + testing = stronger evidence.
Example: Agar billing report reconcile nahi hoti, toh control effective nahi maana jaayega.

1. Safeguard / Integrity Discussion 🛡️
Kabhi client sirf one side info de raha ho → check safeguards.

Example: Agar Director Finance ka kaam pehle verify ho chuka ho, to further provision / recommendation carefully consider karte hain.
2. Self Interest Risk ⚠️
Self-interest ka matlab: Agar pehle advice di ho aur ab audit me same case me involved ho → risk of bias / compromised integrity.
Solution:
Disclosure
Avoid conflicting involvement
Follow safeguards
3. Professional Competence & Confidence 💪
Agar audit ke andar do principles heads ho jaye → complexity barh jati hai.
Audit me Professional Behavior + Integrity + Competence ke standards maintain karne hote hain.
Confidence ka bhi role hai: advice aur review ka balance maintain karna.
1. Self-Interest & Integrity ⚖️
Scenario Example: Gift or benefit mil raha hai → self-interest ho sakta hai.
Agar employee / principal ka objective compromised ho jaye → integrity pe risk.
Key point: Self-interest + fear of exposure = possible bias.
Example: Gift hospital me diya → employee fear ki wajah se complain nahi karega → objective compromise ho sakta hai.
2. Audit Timing & Billing ⏱️
Audit ke time management aur billing ka impact.
Agar audit delay → billing zyada → objective ya quality pe effect.
Client interaction / documentation pe bhi time ka effect hai.
3. Goods & Services Concept 📦
Buy & sell goods/services concept:
Jab company ya client se goods/services purchase karte ho → transaction record aur scope clear hona chahiye.
Insurance example:
Property insurance / coverage check → risk mitigation.
Agar full building insured nahi → potential issue, self-interest risk.
Audit principle: Segregation & independence maintain karni hai → conflict avoid karna.
4. Conflicts & Related Party Transactions 🔗
Scenario: Family member ya personal interest → conflict ho sakta hai.
Example: Agar spouse ya family ka interest involved → self-interest consider karna zaroori.
Key rule:
Agar audit client me personal benefit ho → do not audit or disclose.
5. Tendering & Selection 🏢
Tendering / contracting process:
Proper selection process follow karna → ensures fairness & independence.
Example: Insurance service selection via quotations → correct tendering ensures no bias.
Audit principle: Agar client influence ya personal gain → skip audit or report independence risk.
6. Self-Interest Risk in Gifts / Job Offers 🎁
Gifts / benefits → consider self-interest.
Job offers / promotions → same principle:
Agar personal benefit involved → audit independence aur objectivity at risk.
Rule of thumb: Avoid auditing transactions where personal interest exists.
1. Self-Interest Concept ⚖️
Definition: Self-interest = personal benefit ya gain jo ek individual kisi situation me receive kar sakta hai.
Ek hi scenario me multiple self-interest scenarios ho sakte hain.
Example: Gift accept karna → personal benefit → self-interest trigger hota hai.
Family ya friend involvement → personal benefit aur friendship influence se judgement affect ho sakti hai.
2. Accepting Gifts / Ethical Boundaries 🎁
Jab gift accept karte ho → self-interest consider karna zaroori.
Rule: Accept na karo agar personal benefit ya bias ho sakta ho.
Example: Client gift dena → agar accept karoge → judgement aur objectivity risk me aa sakti hai.
Policy safeguard: Firm ka basic policy → koi gift accept na karein, team members ko clear karna.
3. Transaction Review & Segregation 📊
Transactions pe self-interest risk check karna zaroori:
Value, discount, pricing → consider karna.
Third-party effect → assess karna.
Scenario Example:
Insurance policy → agar ek client ka portfolio ya claim high ho → self-interest risk increase.
Agar ek client ke pass claim stuck ho aur audit kar rahe ho → risk alert.
4. Business Relationship Check 🔗
Client acceptance ke time → business relationship check karo:
Kisi ne policy already purchase ki?
Employee / auditor ka personal gain involved?
Agar conflict ho → do not audit ya transfer work to another auditor.
5. Magnitude of Transaction (Magnet Effect) 💰
High-value / magnet transactions → self-interest risk zyada:
Example: Million-value claim → ek hi auditor ke pass stuck → objectivity affect.
Low-value transaction → risk kam, normal audit flow.
Rule: High-value ya magnet transactions → extra caution & safeguards.
6. Insurance Example & Audit Independence 🏢
Agar insurance coverage terminate ho gayi → audit process safe.
Agar still active hai aur auditor same client handle kar raha → risk: self-interest.
Key principle: Personal benefit aur client relation ke basis pe audit assignment adjust karo.
7. Practical Safeguards 🛡️
Audit me self-interest risk minimize karne ke steps:
Gifts / benefits → reject.
High-value transactions → review thoroughly.
Business relationships → confirm with policies.
If personal benefit involved → reassign audit work.
Documentation → transparent record maintain karo.
1. Self-Interest & Offer Acceptance 🎁
Case: Cash ya offer accept karna → self-interest create kar sakta hai.
Agar offer already accept ho chuki → personal benefit aur bias risk barh jata hai.
Key principle: Agar offer accept ho gaya → potential self-interest consider karna zaruri hai.
Example: Employee ka offer accept karna aur client ke saath kaam → judgement aur objectivity pe impact.
2. Family & Thread Factors 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦
Family involvement → influence + potential self-interest.
Thread likhna:
Reason for action
Third-party effect
Impact on principal
Time division & effort allocation → signature aur decision-making pe effect dalta hai.
3. Employment & Department Considerations 🏢
Scenario: Employee ka offer accept karna, department change, aur audit team involvement.
Agar employee current audit client pe kaam kar raha → potential conflict risk.
Department aur position track karna → decision-making aur audit assignment safe rakhta hai.
Rule: Offer accept ho → department aur client relation check karo → reassignment consider karo.
4. Signature & Risk Assessment ✍️
Signature = level of exposure aur potential risk indicator.
High-value / sensitive transactions → signature factor high.
Example: Audit ke liye position aur thread ko link karna → self-interest aur objective evaluate karna.
5. Control & Compliance 🛡️
High-level job / sensitive position → strict controls applied.
Rules:
Offer ya employment jo audit client se related hai → inform & document.
Accept / reject → decision officially record karo.
Ignore / bypass rules → panel action ya escalation.
Example: Employee ne offer accept kiya, but audit team ko inform nahi kiya → risk of bias → control mechanisms enforce hote hain.
6. Practical Safeguards & Ethics ⚖️
Ethics & quality manager monitor karte hain → compliance ensure karna.
Steps:
Self-interest & offer → assess karo.
Thread aur family influence → evaluate karo.
Department & position → verify karo.
High-value / magnet transactions → signature factor consider karo.
Controls & escalation → compliance ke liye implement karo.
7. Case Studies / Examples 📊
Case 1: Employee offer accept → audit team me involved → control lagaya → informed → risk mitigate.
Case 2: Employee offer accept nahi kiya → thread aur department info clear → conflict avoid.
Case 3: High-value transaction (magnet) → signature high → careful review required.

🌟 Key Concepts
Self-Interest Threat ⚠️
Jab auditor ek hi client pe dependent ho → self-interest threat create hota hai.
Reason: auditor may avoid misstatements disclose karna to retain client (fear of losing fees).
Client ko khush rakhne ki bias aa sakti hai.
Significance of Threat
Depend karta hai:
Firm ke total clients kitne hain.
Us client ka share revenue me kitna hai.
Kitni services provide ki ja rahi hain (audit + tax + advisory).
Example: agar ek group (Vodafone) ka revenue >15% of firm revenue → high significance threat.
Safeguards 🔐
Firm policy: agar ek client pe heavy dependence ho:
Ek additional partner assign hoga for Engagement Quality Review (EQR).
Engagement partner sign nahi karega jab tak quality reviewer approve na kare.
Independent reviewer hona chahiye (jo client handle nahi karta ho).
Purpose: ensure independence & objectivity maintained rahe.
Independence in Appearance 👀
Sir ne emphasize kiya: Independence auditor ki nazar me nahi, third party perception me honi chahiye.
Agar third party ko lage auditor compromised hai → independence lost.
Practical Example (PTCL Audit)
Client significant tha → EQR lagaya gaya.
Reviewer ne complex areas (cash close 390 days, consumption variances, etc.) challenge kiye.
Independent review ne ensure kiya ke judgments compromise na ho.
Remedies for Heavy Dependence
Refuse providing non-audit services (tax/advisory) to reduce dependence.
Increase number of clients to diversify revenue.
Client ko adjust karna (transfer some services to other firms).
Automated monitoring tools (RMS – Revenue Monitoring System) detect karte hain revenue % dependence.
🌟 Key Points
Quality Review in Audit
Quality reviewer ke bina audit report sign nahi hoti ✔️.
QC ka role = throughout audit process (risk assessment + execution + final reporting).
Agar reviewer approve na kare → audit sign karna impossible.
Audit Engagement Scenario (SWL case)
Client = Significant client → higher risk ⚠️.
Board demand → audit complete in 1 month only.
Threat: audit quality compromise due to unrealistic deadline.
Fundamental Threats
Self-interest threat → agar firm jaldi audit complete kare to secure revenue/relationship.
Intimidation threat → client pressure for deadline.
Familiarity threat → if maintaining “good relationship” wrongly considered as threat (but sir ne clarify kiya ke relationship itself ≠ threat).
Correct Reasoning for Threats 🚨
Quality of audit affect hogi kyun ke:
Procedures skip ho sakti hain.
Judgemental areas overlook ho sakte hain.
Misstatements detect nahi honge.
Incorrect reasoning: “good relationship with client” → NOT a valid threat ❌.
How to Write in Exam ✍️
Simple phrasing:
“Due to stringent deadline, auditor may overlook misstatements → compromise audit quality.”
“Self-interest threat as firm may try to secure client by reducing procedures.”
“Intimidation threat from client demanding fast completion.”
Avoid vague wording like “audit will not be efficient.” Instead → directly link to quality compromise.
Fundamental Principles Compromised
Integrity & Objectivity → pressure se judgement compromise.
Professional competence & due care → unrealistic timeline → proper procedures perform nahi honge.
Independence in appearance → third party may perceive auditor compromised.
Safeguards
Refuse to accept unrealistic deadline.
Clear communication with TCWG (those charged with governance).
Allocate additional staff/resources.
Escalate if pressure continues → may need to withdraw.
New Standards (Pakistan context)
Companies required to give sustainability + risk disclosures 🌍.
Focus on going concern, business sustainability, challenges, threats.
Purpose → provide users holistic view of survival & continuity.
🌟 Key Concepts
Early Adoption of New Reporting Standards
Auditor faces issue if client adopts new standard early.
Problem: Audit staff lacks training/knowledge about new reporting standard.
⚠️ Risk: Misapplication of standard → wrong audit opinion.
Fundamental Principle affected 👉 Professional Competence & Due Care.
Self-interest threat: Auditor may compromise quality to meet deadlines.
Training & Confidentiality
Trainer suggested discussing real client scenarios in training.
❌ Not allowed: Client-specific confidential data cannot be shared.
✅ Allowed: Public information can be discussed.
Principle impacted 👉 Confidentiality 🔒
Insurance Company Example (Unit Linked Business)
Insurance company takes premiums → invests → provides benefits.
Market risk: If returns fall, clients stop paying premiums.
Reporting standard requires: Future outlook disclosure for shareholders.
⚠️ If misapplied → financial statements misleading → shareholders misguided.
Self-Review Threat (Training without Knowledge)
If staff audits without proper knowledge → misinterpretations.
Client’s business model misunderstood → wrong judgments.
Compromises Professional Competence principle.
Temporary Staff Services
Sometimes audit firms send staff to client location for bookkeeping tasks.
Threat: Staff may become too involved in client’s accounting system.
This can create Self-Review Threat → auditor later audits their own work.
Example: Bookkeeping staff handling expense calculations.
⚖️ Threats Discussion
Self-review threat → jab apna hi kaam dobara evaluate krna ho.
Advocacy threat → client ki taraf se defend/represent krna ya uske favor me argument build krna.
Familiarity threat → zyada closeness ya long association ki wajah se objectivity kharab hona.
🧾 Management Responsibility ka Concept
Auditor management ka kaam nahi karega → sirf verify karega jo management provide kare.
Agar auditor khud decide kare kahan debit/credit krna hai → ❌ management responsibility le raha h.
Agar management khud model bnaye (sales, price forecast, etc.) auditor sirf calculation verify kare → ✅ allowed.
Tax return file krna aur uske liye laws apply krna → 🚫 self-review + management responsibility threat.
👥 Example Scenarios
Client ke kehne pr simple calculation (inflation % lagana, multiply karna) → ✅ threat minimal.
Client ke kehne pr tax return file karna / deductions decide karna → ❌ auditor management ki role le raha h.
Excel model bnana with mgmt. assumptions only → ✅ auditor safe h.
📌 Safeguards (Self-Review & Advocacy)
Additional reviewer assign karo jo independent ho.
Audit team se alawa kisi aur se review karwao.
Functional activities (jo mgmt. kaam hain) avoid kro.
🔑 Familiarity Threat Factors
Time duration → jitna lamba relation, utna zyada risk.
Position/role → agar senior manager ya CFO jesa role lia h → threat ↑
Nature of work → jitna significant judgment involve hoga, threat utna zyada hoga.
Close relationships (friends/family) → independence compromise ho skti h.
💡 Long Association Risks
Client ke sath zyada arsa rehne se:
Objectivity loss ho skti h 😕
Personal relationships develop ho skti hain → bias.
Safeguard: rotation of staff, independent review, cooling-off period.

🌐 Concept: Business Relationship with Audit Client
Definition: Jab audit client ke sath firm ya team member ka business transaction / relationship ho jaye → isko business relationship kehte hain.
Example:
Firm ya individual ne client se insurance policy buy ki.
Individual (audit team member) ka apna ya family owned business ho aur wo audit client ko goods/services provide kare.
⚖️ Risks (Sir ne mention kiye):
Self-interest threat: Apna business ka fayda audit independence ko affect karega.
Intimidation threat: Client keh sakta hai “agar tumhari baat na mani to contract cancel kar denge.”
Long Association risk: Agar client aur auditor ka relationship purana aur lamba ho jaye.
🔑 Factors jo determine karte hain:
Kis bande ka involvement hai (Manager / Partner / Director ya junior staff?)
Nature of transaction (small / significant)
Magnitude of transaction (minor vs. major impact)
Duration (long association ya ek dafa ka deal)
🛡️ Safeguards
Agar individual level par business hai:
Us bande ko audit team se hata do ✅
Ya uske kaam ka independent review karwa lo 🔍
Agar firm level par business hai:
Dekhna hoga ke service kis nature ki hai.
Agar significant impact on financial reporting (e.g. IFRS 9 / IFRS 16 module design & supply) → 🚫 Audit client accept nahi karna chahiye.
Agar administrative system (non-financial impact) ho (e.g. employee attendance, order tracking system) → normally acceptable.
📊 Examples discussed:
Firm ne IFRS 9 module design kiya for client → Ye directly financial reporting ko affect karta hai → Self-review threat create hoga → Audit client accept nahi karna chahiye.
Agar sirf administrative system (non-financial) diya hai (e.g. monitoring orders, employee lists) → normally acceptable, kyunki reporting impact nahi hai.
🌐 Core Concept: Business Relationship – Independence Threat
Auditor independent nahi reh sakta jab client ke sath ongoing business relationship ho.
Options:
Ya to audit accept hi na karo 🚫
Ya to business relationship khatam karo before audit ✅
⚖️ Risks (Sir ne mention kiye):
Self-interest threat → apna business fayda compromise karega independence.
Advocacy threat → auditor apne hi provided software/system ko defend karega.
Intimidation threat → agar family member powerful position me ho, to auditor pressure me aa sakta hai.
Familiarity threat → close family members aur long association ki wajah se objectivity compromise ho sakti hai.
🔑 Important Points:
Agar software/service financial reporting impact karta hai (e.g. IFRS 9, IFRS 16 modules) → audit accept nahi karna ❌.
Agar administrative system diya hai (sirf support ya tracking ka, financial reporting impact nahi) → normally acceptable.
Magnitude of transaction → jitna bara system/software, utna bara threat.
Position of family member → agar high-level role (e.g. CFO) par hai → threats zyada high.
Departmental dealings irrelevant hain → service firm ko pay ki jati hai, department-wise separation matter nahi karta.
🛡️ Safeguards:
Individual level:
Involved staff ko audit team se hata do 👥
Ya unke kaam ka independent review karwao 🔍
Firm level:
Agar business relationship end ho gaya hai aur ab koi reporting impact nahi → audit possible.
Agar abhi bhi maintenance/support ongoing hai aur financial impact hai → audit reject karo.
📊 Examples discussed:
Mizan Bank example → firm ne IFRS 9 module develop kiya → ab audit client accept nahi kar sakte kyunki self-review threat.
Family member CFO position → self-interest + intimidation threat.
Junior family member (low-level role) → threat kam, safeguard = replace member or independent review.
🌟 Key Points
Client Info Usage & Confidentiality
Agar auditor confidential info kisi aur client ke liye use kare → Violation of Integrity & Professional Behavior.
Confidentiality ka matlab: jo info mili hai, bina permission ke disclose/share nahi karni 🚫.
Professional behavior demand karta hai k profession ko discredit na ho.
Self-Interest Threat
Offer accept karna for financial/personal gain → self-interest threat.
Especially jab knowledge/experience na ho specific industry (insurance case).
Safeguards (if applicable)
Normally safeguards likhe hote hain:
Refuse offer if confidentiality compromised.
Training/ new hiring for specialized knowledge.
Independent consultation/quality review.
Agar safeguards possible nahi → audit accept nahi karna chahiye.
Insurance Industry Complexity 🏢
Complex industry → require specialized knowledge.
Agar firm ke paas knowledge nahi → risk of audit failure.
Lack of expertise = Self-interest threat + lack of professional competence.
Audit Team & Knowledge
Change in audit team head agar insurance ka experience nahi.
Safeguard → hire experienced staff, arrange training, involve consultants.
Otherwise → reject engagement.
Professional Competence & Due Care
Accepting audit without industry knowledge = against professional competence.
Must ensure sufficient training, knowledge, and supervision.
Quality Control (ISQC) Note 📝
For high-risk/complex clients, Quality Control Review required.
Problem: agar firm mein ek hi banda ho → dono audit + QC review karna possible nahi.
Solution: external consultation / extended timeline / hiring.
Possible Additional Threat: Intimidation
Lack of knowledge → client may dominate/threaten auditor.
This creates intimidation threat alongside self-interest.
