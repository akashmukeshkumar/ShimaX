🛠️ Procedures:

🔄 Periodic Method Recap:
Jo companies periodic inventory method follow karti hain, wo mahine ke end me physical inventory count karti hain takeh inventory ky existence ko sure kr sakein khy theft to nahi hu gye.
Phir purchases ko add karti hain aur closing stock minus karke COGS (Cost of Goods Sold) nikalti hain.
🌀 Perpetual Method Recap: Jo company perpetual method pe chalti hai, usme jo inventory aayegi wo bhi record hoti hai aur jo chali jaati hai wo bhi.

✅ Inventory Listing Completeness:

Total ko GL ya Financial Statements se trace kiya jata hai 📊
📌 Yani jo items stock report me given hain unki total value GL me reflect honi chahiye.
✅ Floor to Sheet Testing:
Auditor floor pe jata hai (warehouse/factory) aur random items pick karta hai 🏭
In items ko inventory report se match karta hai 📋
❗ If any item is found on the floor but not on the sheet:
Management se inquiry ki jati hai:
“Kya yeh item kisi customer ke behalf pe rakha gaya hai?”
“Kya yeh item kisi aur third party ka hai jo humne apne warehouse me store kiya hai?”
✅ Auditor Response (If floor par item hai lekin inventory report me nahi hai):
Agar item kisi customer ke liye held hai → customer acknowledgement + GDN verify karein
Agar item third-party ka hai → agreement, confirmation, correspondence inspect karein

Stock sheet se item uthao aur floor pe physically check karo 🔄
🧾 Existence via Third Party Inventory Confirmation:

Management se inquiry karo 🔍

🪙 Cash Verification
🏦 Audit Focus: Cash vs Bank Balance
Jab client ka audit ho, toh cash AND bank balance dono verify krne hote hain.

Kuch organizations multiple branches mein operate karte hain (e.g., bank, telecom).
Example: Final bank audit 31 Dec ko ho raha tha, 410 branches thi.
🗂️ What We Do:

ATM + Cash in hand = Total cash

📌 Risk (agar yeh na karein to):

✂️ Cut-Off Test – Why It’s Crucial
🌐 Concept:
Count 31 December ka hai, but agar koi transaction 1st Jan ka hai aur wo purani date mein reflect ho rahi hai, to cut-off issue hota hai.
🔁 Example:
Counted cash: PKR 150,000
GL (General Ledger) cash showing: PKR 140,000
Difference: PKR 10,000
Why? Ek expense ka cash payment voucher (CPV) 1st Jan ko bana, lekin cash physically 31 Dec ko exist karta tha.
🧠 Sir’s Logic:
“Agar paisa physically tha 31 Dec ko, to uska expense bhi 31 Dec ka hona chahiye.”
🧾 Cash Payment Voucher (CPV) – Key Evidence for Cut-Off
What to check in CPV:
Date of expense
Voucher number
Supporting reason for the transaction
Entry posted in GL kab hui?
🧮 Test:
CPV 1st Jan ka, but cash was with entity on 31 Dec → Cut-off issue
GL entry check karni hai: recorded in wrong period?
🔄 Logic:
“Kya tumne actual cash 31 Dec ko handover kiya tha ya nahi?”
GL mein entry kis date ki gayi hai?
📦 Cut-Off Test Logic in Inventory vs Cash
Sir ne kaha: “Yehi logic inventory pe bhi lagta hai.”
Inventory Example:
4 GDNs banay 1st Jan ko
But goods physically dispatched on 31 Dec
Agar 1st Jan ko record kiya, to inventory over/under stated ho sakti hai
🧾 GDN = Goods Dispatch Note
Inventory ke liye:
Outward: GDN + Invoice
Inward: GRN + Purchase invoice
🧠 Why Cut-Off Matters in Both Cash & Inventory
Cash: To match actual cash with accounting books
Inventory: To ensure correct stock position year-end pe
📌 Sir’s Tip: “Kya yehi logic tum log inventory pe apply karte ho? Haan! Toh phir cut-off ka test cash ke sath bhi karo.”

📄 Bank Reconciliation – Kya Hota Hai?
Bank Confirmation vs General Ledger (GL):
Kabhi kabhi confirmation ka balance aur GL ka balance match nahi karta.
Difference ko reconcile karna padta hai.
📊 Example:
Bank Statement shows: PKR 7,696,800
GL shows: PKR 7,640,000
Difference = PKR 56,800
🔍 Reconciliation Item:
Jaise hi check issue hota hai, GL me record ho jata hai (Bank credit)
Lekin wo check abhi tak bank me present nahi hua
Isko kehte hain: Unpresented Checks
👨‍🏫 Sir’s Flow:
GL balance lein
Unpresented checks add karen (bank ne abhi clear nahi kiye)
Adjusted GL balance = Bank confirmation balance

📌 Logic:
Agar check ab tak clear nahi hua = reconciliation valid hai
Agar clear ho gaya hai but GL me record nahi = error in books

🧱 PPE (Property Plant Equipment)
Balance Sheet mein PPE waise hi show hota hai jaise FAR1 mein  padha tha.
Example diya: Building, Office Equipment, Vehicle .
🖥️ Example: PTCL jese institutions
PTCL ke paas 30,000–35,000 laptops! Har item ka record kaha hota hai? ➤ Fixed Asset Register – Yeh hi audit ka main base banta hai.
📒 Why Do We Need Fixed Asset Register?
Kyunki ismein detailed breakdown hoti hai: kis department ko kya diya, kab diya, kis value pe.
Sir’s Discussion:
Tag lagta hai, GRN + FAR ban’ti hai, system auto post karta hai.
📊 Verification Technique: Opening Balance Verification
Step-by-Step:
Filter on Class: e.g., sirf Equipment select karo.
Filter on Date: e.g., sirf 01-Jan-2024 tak ke items.
Sum: Opening items ka sum nikalo.
Match: Total opening balance se match hona chahiye.
📌 “Opening ki relativity check karo, mathematical accuracy trace karo.”
🔍 Addition Check – Process
Same filter approach: Class + Date filter lagao.
Sum of additions nikalo ➤ Match karo additions total se.
🧾 GL vs FAR (Fixed Asset Register) Mismatch Testing
Sir’s Technique:
Date by date check karo – kya sab branches mein asset ki entries properly gayi hain?
Dekha gaya kuch assets ka depreciation to record hua but acquisition entry hi nahi bani.
📌 Audit Insight:
Agar GL mein entry nahi → FAR incomplete hai → Control weakness confirmed.
📋 Completeness Assertion – Real Test of Audit
Scenario Discussion:
Audit ke dauraan kuch assets milte nahi → possibility of incomplete FAR.
Admin department ko bola gaya: “Yeh tabhi report issue hogi jab aap sign karenge.”
Sir ne ek aur warning di: “Kal ko agar issue hua, tumhara sign record mein hai.”

🧠 Why GL & FAR Link is Important?

FAR aur GL match hona chahiye – warna assertion of completeness breach ho jaata hai.
🔐 Principal Audit Flow: Always Start with Completeness!

PROCEDURES ON REVALUATION
1. 📄 Obtain the schedule / working of revaluation from management evidencing:
   - 🏷️ Asset type
   - 📊 Carrying amount
   - 💰 Fair value → We need to verify this
   - 📈 Surplus balance → Remaining balance after transfer of incremental depreciation
   Check mathematical accuracy and trace the total to financial statements
2. 📑 Obtain the management's expert valuation report and check whether the fair value in the expert’s report matches the schedule / working of revaluation
3. 🧠 Evaluate the management expert’s competence and capabilities (assess qualifications, conduct discussions, review published reports)
4. 🔍 Evaluate the independence of the management’s expert
5. ⚖️ Check the reasonableness of fair value by comparing with similar market transactions (if any)
6. 📝 Consider appointing the auditor’s own expert:
   - 🧠 Evaluate competence, capabilities, and objectivity
   - 📚 Obtain understanding of the field of the auditor’s expert
   - 🤝 Agree on terms of engagement with the expert
   - 🔎 Review the work of the auditor’s expert
7. 📊 Compare the report provided by the auditor’s expert with that of the management’s expert
8. ❓ If differences arise, make inquiries from management / management’s expert
9. 🤝 Arrange a meeting between the auditor’s expert and the management’s expert
10. ✅ Ensure the entire class of assets has been revalued
11. 🧮 Recalculate incremental depreciation and ensure the transfer from revaluation surplus to retained earnings is appropriate
12. 📌 Ensure disclosures are made as per IAS 16 requirements:
   - 📝 Historical carrying amount
   - 🏷️ Name of the valuer
   - 💵 FSV value
PROCEDURES ON ACCOUNTING POLICY CHANGE
1. ⚡ Evaluate whether the accounting policy change results in more reliable and relevant information
2. 📋 Inspect minutes of the board of directors’ meeting where the accounting policy was approved
3. 📂 Obtain working of the retrospective adjustment of the accounting policy from management
4. 🔍 Evaluate whether the accounting policy change has been appropriately accounted for in accordance with IFRS
5. 📝 Evaluate the appropriateness of disclosures made by management regarding the change in accounting policy
THE END

🛠️ Replacement Cost Method Explanation
🔄 Replacement cost method se valuation:
💰 Fresh asset ki current market price calculate ki jati hai.
📉 Us mein se accumulated depreciation (wear and tear) subtract kiya jata hai to reach actual fair value.
📝 Expert ki report mein methodology aur assumptions clearly documented honi chahiye.
📌 Auditor’s Procedures on Expert Report
✅ Methodology ki clarity aur appropriateness verify karna.
📋 Expert ko provided data accuracy aur completeness se confirm karna.
📐 Expert ki assumptions ki reasonableness verify karna.
🔍 Auditor ka expert independently cross-check aur validate karta hai management expert ki findings ko.
⚠️ Risks
❌ Management expert ki incompetence ya biased valuation se inaccurate results aa sakte hain.
⚡ Auditor ki taraf se expert ki independence aur objectivity confirm na karna risky ho sakta hai.
⚡ Important Points
🛡️ Auditor ko apne appointed expert ki independence, confidentiality, aur competence ensure karni zaruri hai.
✅ Management ki valuation reports critically evaluate karna auditor ki responsibility hai.
📌 Methodology Assessment
🔍 Auditor evaluate karta hai ke management ke expert ne kaun sa valuation method use kiya hai.
🔄 Replacement Cost Method mein fresh asset ki current market price calculate ki jati hai aur us mein se accumulated depreciation minus kiya jata hai.
💰 Income Method mein future mein asset kitni income generate karega uski present value calculate hoti hai.
📌 Correct Valuation Method Kaise Choose Karein
❗ Replacement Cost Method hamesha suitable nahi hota, especially jab asset ki active market na ho ya company asset ko exit karne ki bajaye continue karna chahti ho.
✅ Income Method zyada suitable hai jab ongoing income generation evaluate kar rahe hon, jaise telecom towers.
🏗️ Example: Telecom Tower Valuation
📡 Telecom towers ki valuation Replacement Cost Method se accurate nahi hoti kyunke is mein towers ki income-generating potential ignore hoti hai.
📈 Actual valuation mein future income streams ko consider karna chahiye.
📌 Expert Valuation Procedures
✔️ Expert ko diye gaye data ki accuracy aur completeness confirm karni hai.
📊 Expert ki assumptions aur projections historical aur industry-standard data ke saath match honi chahiye.
🔍 Expert ki report ko thoroughly cross-check aur validate karna hai.
📌 Auditor’s Expert Involvement
🧐 Agar management expert ki competence ya independence pe doubt ho, auditor apna independent expert hire karega.
🔎 Auditor ka expert yeh verify karega:
🛠️ Valuation methodology.
📋 Management expert ki assumptions.
📑 Dono experts ko diye gaye data accurate aur matching hon.
📌 Agreement with Auditor’s Expert
📝 Formal engagement letter mein scope, objectives, confidentiality terms aur timelines clearly define karna hai.
📅 Deliverables, reporting deadlines, aur communication protocols clearly set karne hain.
📌 Dispute Resolution
⚖️ Agar management aur auditor ke expert mein discrepancies aayen:
🤝 Direct discussion se differences resolve karne ki koshish karein.
🎯 Unresolved ho to unbiased third expert involve karein.
📌 Differences adjustments ya misstatements ki evaluation financial statements mein kar sakte hain.
⚠️ Risks
❌ Inappropriate valuation methodology inaccurate asset valuation ka risk hai.
⚠️ Incorrect assumptions ya misalignment se valuation process mein issues aa sakte hain.
⚡ Important Points
✅ Valuation methodology correct honi chahiye aur asset ke intended use (exit vs. continued use) ko reflect karni chahiye.
✔️ Auditor ko independently valuation assumptions aur data validate karna hai.

2. Correct Recognition of Revenue 💰💡
o    Revenue ko correct period mein record karna hota hai. Agar sales December mein hoti hai, toh usko December mein hi book karna chahiye.
o    Example: Agar kisi product ki sale October mein book ho gayi thi, toh wo sale December ke financials mein na aaye.
3. Collateral and Payments Review 🔑💳
o    Collateral ko review karna aur ensure karna ke wo properly recorded hai. Bank se collateral ki confirmation lene ki zarurat hoti hai.
o    Transaction Verification: Jo bhi payments aur transactions ho rahi hain, unko verify karna aur ensure karna ke wo sahi period mein reflect ho.
4. Business Knowledge and Accounting Practices 🧠📚
o    Business ki operations ko samajhna zaroori hai taake aap verify kar sakein ke financial records sahi hain ya nahi.
o    Example: Agar company ne commission ke liye payments kiye hain, toh unko accurately account karna padta hai, aur kisi bhi discrepancies ko identify karna hota hai.
5. Verification of Payment Schedules 🗓️💸
o    Key Step: Payment schedule ko check karo aur ensure karo ke wo proper accounting practices ke mutabiq book ki gayi ho.
o    Relevancy: Ensure karo ke saare payments aur receivables relevant accounting period mein book ho rahe ho.
6. Transaction Adjustments and Recalculation 🔄📊
o    Agar payments ya adjustments ki recalculation ki zarurat ho, toh unko check karo aur ensure karo ke calculations accurate hain.
o    Example: Agar commission ka rate change hota hai, toh recalculated amount ko verify karna padta hai.
7. Verification of Minimum Requirements ✅🔢
o    Jab company ki performance ya financials ki verification ho rahi ho, toh minimum financial requirements ko ensure karna zaroori hota hai.
o    Key Concept: Revenue aur sales target ko verify karte hue, unka minimum threshold cross hona zaroori hai.
8. Complete Verification Process ✔️🔍
o    Final Verification: Sabhi transactions ko inspect karo, aur ensure karo ke wo book ho rahi hain. Is process mein business knowledge ka hona zaroori hota hai.
o    Final Step: Jo jo records check kiye hain, unko final approval ke liye review karo aur cross-check karo.

🔹 Introduction to Lease Audit 🏢📝🔍
Agar kisi entity ki multiple branches hoon jo rent pe operate kar rahi hain (like banks), toh auditor ko IFRS 16 ka impact samajhna hota hai. Jab client ne kaha ke “lease arrangement hai,” toh auditor ke dimaag mein IFRS 16 ka bulb on ho jana chahiye. ⚡🧠📘
🔹 Risk Identification 🔍⚠️📌
Completeness Risk: Ho sakta hai kuch leases properly record na hui hoon.
Classification Risk: Short-term ya low-value leases ko galat classify kar diya gaya ho.
Measurement Risk: Present value calculation galat hui ho (discount rate issue).
🔹 Procedure Overview 🛠️🧾✅
Audit procedures hamesha kisi risk ke response mein perform hoti hain.
Example:
Agar info milay ke branch rent pe hai, toh auditor IFRS 16 impact check karega.
🔄 Pehla kaam hoga: Lease Schedule Management se lena
Obtain Lease Schedule from client
Ensure schedule mein:
Opening ROU
Additions
Depreciation
Closing Balance
➡️ Total lease schedule ka FS ke Lease figure se match hona chahiye
🔹 New Lease Identification 🆕📂🔎
Inspect Board Minutes for approval of new lease arrangements 📑🏛️🖊️
Scan Vendor Ledger to spot any new rental entries
Check Rental Payment Trend: 10 leases dikh rahi hain, toh 12 months mein payment honi chahiye
🔁 Completeness check through cross-verification
🔹 Sample Based Verification 🔬📄✔️
Sample lease pick kar ke following cheezein verify karo:
Lease Agreement: Rental, Discount Rate, Tenure
Board Approval: Exists or not
Discount Rate Appropriateness: Agar rate missing hai, toh borrowing rate lagaya gaya?
Lease Life Reasonability: Kya useable life realistic hai?
🔹 Recalculation of Lease Liability & ROU Asset 🧮📉📊
Parameters verify karo (rental, tenure, discount rate)
Recalculate Present Value (PV) of lease payments
Match with client-provided ROU & Liability
➡️ Discrepancy aaye toh reason pucho, ya correction suggest karo
🔹 Payment Verification 💳📆📂
Inspect Bank Statements
Rental payment hui ya nahi?
Correct period mein hui?
➡️ Payment trace karo Lease Schedule ke sath
🔹 Disclosure in FS 📘📢🧾
Lease liability & ROU asset properly disclose hua?
Notes to FS mein maturity profile diya gaya?
✅ Ensure presentation complies with IFRS 16
🔹 Real-life Logic Example 🧠🏠📊
Agar koi lease last year exist karti thi, magar iss saal nahi dikhaayi gayi:
Auditor ko puchna chahiye: Lease terminate hui? Kya documentation hai?
Lesson: Har change ka proper reasoning aur documentation hona chahiye
