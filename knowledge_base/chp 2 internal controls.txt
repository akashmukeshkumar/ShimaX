📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔑 Topic: Internal Control
➡️ Internal control ka basic matlab hai:
Policies & Procedures (قاعدے قوانین) ✅
Organization banata hai apne objectives achieve karne ke liye
Objectives: fraud avoid karna, error minimize karna, assets safe rakhna 💡
📌 Core Definition:
Internal Control = Policies + Procedures jo ensure karte hain:
Fraud na ho ❌
Error na ho ❌
Cheezen properly manage hon ✅
🧩 Example (Receivables & Bad Debts):
Objective: Avoid Bad Debts 🛑
Control: Credit policies banaana
Naye customer ko check karna ✔️
Uski financial health assess karna 💳
Guarantee lena dusre party se 🔒
➡️ Agar ye control na ho → har kisi ko udhaar de dena = risk of bad debts ⚠️
🔑 Internal Control – Receivables
➡️ Controls on Receivables:
Credit Limit ✅
New customer ko limit assign hoti hai (e.g., 5 lakh).
Agar 4 lakh already le chuka hai → sirf 1 lakh ka udhaar allowed.
Aging Report 📊
Report batati hai har invoice kitne din purani hai.
Example: 1 Jan ko sale hui, 30 days ka credit → 30 Jan due.
Agar June tak outstanding hai → aging report me months ke hisaab se show hoga.
Purpose: Identify karo kitni invoices overdue hain aur kab se.
Recovery Officer 👨‍💼
Sirf aging report banana kaafi nahi.
Recovery officer appoint hota hai jo overdue payments collect kare.
📌 Control Objective
Har policy/procedure ka ek objective hota hai.
Example: Credit limit, aging report, recovery → objective = avoid bad debts & ensure timely recovery.
🧐 Auditor’s Responsibility
Auditor ki duty hai design of controls ko samajhna.
Do cheezen check karni hoti hain:
Kya control exist karta hai ya nahi ❓
Agar exist karta hai to design effective hai ya nahi ✅
➡️ Example:
Agar credit limit set karne ka control hai lekin sales rep sirf apna bonus achieve karne ke liye ignore kar raha hai → design effective nahi.
Independent recovery officer hona chahiye jo ensure kare ke limit aur recovery properly follow ho.
🔑 Aging Report & Control Design
Aging report agar sirf month-end pe update hoti hai → issue ⚠️
Example: Credit period 30 days hai.
Lekin update month-end pe → effectively 60 days ban jata hai.
Result: Customer extra time le leta hai → recovery risk ↑
➡️ Solution: Aging report daily update honi chahiye ✅
📌 Recovery Issues
Credit Limit Check fail ho sakta hai agar data timely update nahi.
Result: Goods sell ho jati hain un-creditworthy customers ko.
➡️ Bad debts ka major reason = ineffective control design ⚠️
🏦 Example: Microfinance Bank Audit
Recovery officers = sales officers.
Problem: Recovery karte waqt extra kharche (car rent, food, etc.) kar lete the.
Example: 40k loan recovery → 30k kharch.
Net loss → bad debts aur zyada ho gaye.
Action taken:
Pre-approval from Head of Recovery mandatory.
Ek hi car use hogi poore region ke liye → cost control ✅
🧐 Control vs. Control Design
Control hona aur control ka design effective hona → dono alag.
Example:
Recovery officer = sales director ❌
Sales director kabhi apne customer relation kharab nahi karega.
Result: Recovery ineffective.
➡️ Right design = Independent recovery department
🔑 Key Concepts
Control existence → Pehle check karo control hai bhi ya nahi. Agar hai hi nahi to design check karne ka faida nahi.
Design effectiveness → Agar control exist karta hai, tabhi uska design check hoga (e.g., camera warehouse pe laga hai? kis angle pe?).
Operation of control → Sirf design hona kafi nahi, check karo kya control actually operate ho raha hai ya sirf naam ka hai.
📊 3 Levels of Control Check
Existence – Control hai ya nahi?
Design – Jo hona chahiye wo design correct hai ya flawed?
Operation – Practical life me control implement ho raha hai ya bas formality?
🏭 Example (Warehouse/Receivables)
Warehouse Example: Camera laga hi nahi → control exist hi nahi → test possible nahi.
Credit Officer Example:
Credit officer hona chahiye (control exist karta hai ✅).
Credit limit design ki gayi (design correct ✅).
Lekin practice me majority cases me credit limit set kiye bina maal issue ho gaya (operation ❌).
👉 Yahan se samajh aya: Design aur Operation dono alag cheezein hain.
🔹 Audit Start karne se pehle:
Dekhna hota hai controls exist karte hain ya nahi ✅
Agar exist karte hain → check karna ke operate ho rahe hain ya nahi ⚙️
Ye decide karega ke auditor ko aage zyada kaam karna hai ya kam.
🔹 Example 1: Camera Control
Camera exist karta hai lekin wall ki taraf hai → design sahi nahi 🚫
Agar camera sahi jagah hai but band hai → exist karta hai lekin operate nahi kar raha ❌
Matlab: Control hona, design theek hona, aur operate karna – teenon alag cheezein hain.
🔹 Example 2: Credit Officer
Policy: Credit officer limit assign karta hai.
Reality: Officer 2 months se absent tha → control exist karta hai but operate nahi kar raha.
Result: Auditor ko zyada verification karni padegi 📑
🔹 Important Clarification:
Auditor jab control test karta hai → wo sirf activity check karta hai, amount ki asal verification alag se hoti hai.
⚠️ Inherent Limitation of Controls:
Agar staff jaan bujh ke involve ho jaye aur collusion ho (e.g., mil ke camera off kar dena, chori karna) → phir controls fail ho jate hain.
Isi wajah se audit hamesha karna zaroori hai 🔍
Lecture- 2

📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔑 Who Implements & Monitors Controls?
➡️ Controls Implement:
Implement karta hai Management 👨‍💼
Objective: fraud aur errors ko avoid karna, financial statements sahi banana.
➡️ Example Objectives & Controls:
Receivables ki limit set karna → bad debts avoid karna ✅
Inventory ke liye camera aur security guard lagana → theft avoid karna ✅
📌 Monitoring of Control
TCWG moniter krty ha.
Agar implementation aur monitoring dono management hi kare → issue ⚠️
Kyun? Jo implement kar raha hai, wahi apna kaam monitor karega → errors/frauds detect nahi honge.
🏢 Internal Audit Department
Team hoti hai jisme accounting/finance background ke log hote hain.
Kaam: Company ke processes & controls ko monitor/Test karna.
Report banakar submit karte hain Audit Committee ko.
➡️ Audit Committee:
Board of Directors ki banai hui committee.
Chairman hamesha independent hota hai.
Internal Audit Department kaam poora saal karta hai aur report karta hai Audit Committee ko.
Internal Auditors check karte hain ke policies aur procedures follow ho rahe hain ya nahi.
Lekin final assurance shareholders ko External Auditor deta hai.
🔑 Testing of Controls
Agar controls effectively operate kar rahe hain → auditor apna detailed work kam kar deta hai ✅
Agar controls weak hain → zyada verification karni padegi ❌
➡️ Is stage pe auditor sirf activities check karta hai, koi amounts verify nahi karta.
“Yahan maine ek bhi figure check nahi ki, sirf dekha ke activity perform ho rahi hai ya nahi.”
🏛️ Governance vs. Management
Directors = part of governance, na ke daily management.
Unka role: business chalane ke liye nahi, balki governance aur monitoring ke liye hai.
⚖️ Role of External Auditor
Internal audit aur audit committee ke kaam ke bawajood, external auditor required hai.
External auditor:
Controls ki design & operation ko samajhta hai.
Jo issues internal audit report me miss ho jayein → unko detect karta hai.
🔹 Revenue Controls & Assertions
Management ka daawa (assertion):
Revenue properly valued hai ✔️
Revenue accurately recorded hai ✔️
Revenue actually occurred (genuine) hai ✔️
Revenue completeness maintain hai ✔️
🔹 Key Controls in Pricing & Revenue Recording
Avoid Bad Debts & Wrong Discounts → Objective clear.
Predetermined Price List →
Example: 21,000 → live discount → 18,000.
System mein fixed hota hai (barcode scanning, Imtiaz example).
Limited excess of system → user price change ni kr skta.
Automatic discount apply hota hai.
Risk in Valuation →
Agar price control hata dia jaye → koi bhi record kr skta hai.
Misstatement ka risk: Over/Under valuation.
🔹 Occurrence Assertion
Revenue tabhi record hoga jab service/product deliver hogi.
Control: Segregation of Duties (SOD) →
Jo fee slip bana raha hai, wohi portal access issue nahi krta.
Different persons handle → fraud risk kam.
👉 Risk: Agar slip ke baghair access de diya jaye → Fake revenue record ho skta hai ❌.
🔹 Completeness Assertion
Har slip ke against access hona chahiye.
Agar slips aur access ka proper reconciliation nahi hoga → completeness risk.
🔹 Revenue Recording & Risks
Agar slip issue hui but access na mila → revenue miss ho sakta hai (understatement risk ⚠️).
Agar 60 slips record hui lekin actually issue hui 50 → overstatement risk ⚠️.
Agar 40 record hui lekin issue hui 50 → understatement risk ⚠️.
Solution: Reconciliation between slips issued & access granted.
👉 Control: Reconciliation kisi aur se banwayi jati hai (independent check) → fraud/ error detect.
🔹 Sales Cycle Start
Customer se Purchase Order (PO) milta hai.
Sales order department process karta hai.
Important: Credit limit check hona chahiye before sales.
Agar new customer hai → unique customer code assign hota hai.
Example: Credit limit set = Rs. 800,000.
System control: Limit Check Control → ensure customer ki credit worthiness check ho.
Credit Limit Control (Limit Check Control)
Purchase order aata hai → system check karta hai against credit limit.
Example: Customer ne 6 lakh ka already purchase kia, limit 8 lakh hai → agar 4 lakh ka aur invoice dalenge toh system auto reject karega ❌.
Isko kehte hain Limit Check Control / Validity Control ✅.
Authorization & Responsibility
Credit Officer: credit limit set karta hai aur check karta hai.
Sales representative ke paas authority nahi hoti limit change karne ki.
Input Authorization / Limited Access Control 🔒
Sales rep order banata hai, but system auto picks values (e.g., quantity, price) from approved records.
Usko modify/change karne ka option nahi hota → Limited Access Control.
Pricing Control 💲
Prices must come from approved price list.
Agar sales rep khud price dal de → control weakness ⚠️.
Correct design: Discount/price approval specific authority ke through hota hai.
Gate Pass Analogy 🚪
Jaise warehouse se bina parchi ke maal nahi ja sakta, waise hi sales order ke bina inventory release nahi hogi.
Sales Order Numbering (Pre-numbering Control) 🔢
Sales order automatically system me number assign hota hai.
Purpose:
Koi order miss na ho.
Tracking aur completeness ensure karna.
Objectives of Sales Order 📋
Inventory company ke warehouse se bina authorization bahar na jaye.
Customer order proper track ho (no unfulfilled orders).
Source of Quantity & Price
Quantity → customer purchase order se.
Price → approved price list se.
Sales Order Copies 🗂️
Ek order banne ke baad multiple copies banti hain:
Customer copy 🧾
Warehouse copy 🏭
Finance copy 💰
Important Point
Sales order ban gaya ≠ Sale record ho gayi.
Sale tabhi record hogi jab goods deliver honge ✅.
✅ Assertions Mentioned:
Completeness Assertion → Pre-numbering ensures no order missing.
Occurrence Assertion → Sale record tab hogi jab actual delivery hogi.
Lecture- 3
📚 Reference: Book Pages -
📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔑 Main Concepts Discussed:
Segregation of Duties (SoD)
Sales transaction bina process ke record nahi hoti.
Finance ko delivery note ke bina record karne ki ijazat nahi.
Warehouse ko mal bina document ke release karna mana hai.
👉 Is se ensure hota hai ke unauthorized inventory release na ho.
Purpose of Controls
Bad debts avoid karna.
Sales valuation sahi rakhna.
Unauthorized discount avoid karna.
Customer satisfaction maintain karna (tracking & numbering system).
Approved Price List
Pricing sirf approved price list se honi chahiye.
Access control lagaya jata hai ke koi unauthorized banda price list change na kare.
👉 Agar control na ho → risk of wrong valuation of sales.
Sales Order Tracking
Sales orders ko unique free number assign karna zaroori hai.
Purpose: tracking aur pehle order ko pehle fulfill karna (FIFO approach).
👉 Agar missing ho to customer dissatisfaction aur order tracking issue hoga.
Quality & Quantity Check before Dispatch
Goods dispatch se pehle quality aur quantity check honi chahiye.
👉 Agar na ho:
Risk of Sales Returns (defected goods).
Risk of Understatement/Overstatement of Sales agar returns properly record na hon.
Example: Laptop shop → defective mouse pad deliver, return karna pada.
⚖️ Assertions Mentioned: - Valuation assertion → Approved price list ensure karti hai ke sales value correct ho.
- Existence & Completeness assertion → Warehouse bina delivery note ke inventory release nahi karega.
- Cut-off assertion → Tracking number ensure karega ke orders proper sequence me fulfill hon.
⚠️ Risks Highlighted: - ❌ Unauthorized sales pricing → Wrong valuation in audit.
- ❌ No order tracking → Customer dissatisfaction.
- ❌ No quality check → Sales returns & misstated sales records.
🔑 Main Concepts Discussed:
Sales Returns & No Return Policy
Agar company ki no return policy hai → customer khud check karega before taking goods.
Otherwise, defective goods return honge → risk of over/understatement of sales.
Goods Dispatch Note (GDN)
Bohat important control document.
GDN ke andar hota hai:
Quantity supplied 📦
Sales Order number 📝
Date 🗓️
Kisne count aur quality check kiya ✔️
Signatures of warehouse incharge + receiver ✍️
Purpose: Proof ke kitni quantity deliver hui aur kis order ke against.
Multiple GDNs per Sales Order
Ek sales order (e.g., 50,000 units) ke against multiple GDNs ban sakti hain (10,000 + 5,000 + etc.).
Tracking easy hoti hai ke kitna fulfill hua aur kitna pending hai.
Matching & Verification
Customer jo sales order leke aaye usko company records ke sales order se match karna hoga.
Verification ke bina fake/edited documents ka risk hota hai.
Acknowledgement by Customer
Dispatch ke waqt customer ke signature lena lazmi hai.
Agar acknowledgement (sign) missing ho → risk: customer falsely claim kar sakta hai ke goods receive nahi hue.
Ye failure → business loss + audit risk (inventory overstated).
Audit Implication
GDN evidence hai ke goods inventory se legally issue hue.
Without GDN → no proof, audit me issue hoga (existence/rights assertion fail).
⚖️ Assertions Mentioned:
Existence & Rights assertion → GDN confirm karta hai ke inventory legally aur actually dispatch hui.
Completeness assertion → Har dispatch ka record GDN me hona chahiye.
⚠️ Risks Highlighted:
❌ No return policy missing → Over/understatement of sales.
❌ No GDN prepared → Inventory falsely overstated.
❌ No customer acknowledgement → Customer falsely claim kar sakta hai goods receive nahi hue → business loss.
🔹 GDN (Goods Dispatch Note) mey required details:
Supplier ka naam (jis ne mal dia)
Warehouse wala naam (receiver)
Counter ka naam (jis ne count kia)
College inspector ka naam (inspection proof)
Quantity mention (mal kitna deliver hua)
Signature required ✅
✨ Control point:
GDN pe sign hone ka matlab: supplier aur receiver dono ne confirm kia ke quantity sahi hai.
False claim ka risk kam ho jata hai (customer claim kare ke mal aya hi nahi).
Entry ke waqt employee card ki copy bhi record hoti hai.
🔹 Sales process & GDN relation:
Purchase order ki copy customer service/warehouse ke pass hoti hai 📄
GDN + Sales order ki matching hoti hai → issi basis pe sales record hoti hai.
Half copy sales order department ke pass jati hai (proof ke order fulfill hua).
Agar claim aye to proof present kia ja sakta hai.
✅ Point: GDN ≠ Sales record → Sales record alag se maintain hota hai.
🔹 Risk Discussion (Inventory & Claims):
Fake country entries → final statement me misstated balances ka risk.
Insurance companies me bhi claim recording ka risk hota hai (Completeness assertion).
Example: EFU Insurance – countrywide claims → risk ke sare claims record na hon (intentional ya error).
📌 Assertion: Completeness (risk ke kuch claims record nahi hue).
📌 Risk: Understatement of claims (claims exist karte hain but GL me record nahi hue).
🔹 Audit procedure (Completeness test):
Client se poocha: tumhare pass kya mechanism hai ke sare claims record hue?
Call center reporting ☎️
Web portal reporting 💻
Designated emails 📧
Direct branch reporting 🏢
Auditor check karega ke report → record tak flow complete hai ya missing.
Agar koi “reconciliation / dukan” (control) nahi banta → high risk of understatement.
🔹 Use of Excel in Audit (CAATs – Computer Assisted Audit Techniques):
Claims file (customer codes) vs GL file ko Excel VLOOKUP se match kiya 🔎
Jo codes GL me nahi mile → highlight red 🚨
Jo match hue → highlight green ✅
Result: ~2.5 billion worth claims record nahi hue ❌
🔍 Audit Targeting & Controls
Auditor ka kaam hota hai risk identify karna aur phir check karna k control exist karte hain ya nahi.
Yahaan risk tha: Claims complete record nahi hue.
Client ka jawab: "Next period me record karenge" ❌ – par matching concept ke hisaab se same period me record hona chahiye.
Impact: Profit 3.5B → sirf 1B reh gaya (understatement due to incomplete recording).
⚖️ Assertion Focus
Completeness: Claim hona chahiye tha par record nahi hua.
Occurrence ulta case hai: Record ho gaya jabke hona nahi tha.
Yahaan sirf Completeness risk ka discussion tha.
📦 Sales Order aur GDN (Goods Dispatch Note)
Objective: Ensure jitne orders receive hue, unki fulfilment properly record ho.
Control: Daily reconciliation between Sales Order & GDN.
Agar control na ho → Goodwill damage aur Impairment risk.
🧾 Sales Order vs GDN Numbering System
Sales Order No. aur GDN No. → hamesha separate sequence honi chahiye.
Example:
Sales Order: 1023
GDN: apna unique seq (e.g. 2342, 2343, etc.)
Agar GDN no. ko Sales Order no. bana diya →
Tracking impossible ❌
Double sales booking ka risk ✅
💡 Key Control
Finance system me Data Validation lagta hai → ek hi GDN pe ek hi sale book hogi.
GDN par hamesha apna unique sequence + reference to Sales Order No. likhna chahiye.
📦 Sales Process Flow Recap
Documents involved:
Sales Order 📝
GDN (Goods Dispatch Note) 🚚
Invoice 💵
Purpose:
Sales Order = Customer ka demand.
GDN = Proof of dispatch & correct quantity.
Invoice = Sales recording (basis: SO + GDN).
⚠️ Risk in Invoicing
Agar Invoice quantity wrong pick hui →
From Sales Order (instead of GDN) ❌ → Over/Understatement risk.
Correct Basis: Quantity from GDN ✅, Price from Sales Order ✅.
📌 Assertion at risk:
Accuracy / Valuation (amounts correctly calculated?).
Occurrence (sale recorded but goods dispatched nahi hue).
🔍 Examiner’s Trap
Statement: Finance personnel prepares invoice using Sales Order.
Student sochta hai correct hai → but actually risk hai ⚠️.
Invoice should always rely on GDN for quantity.
📑 Inquiry as Audit Procedure
Auditor client se inquiring karega: “Tumhari invoicing process kya hai?”
Agar client bole: “Quantity SO se pick karte hain, price bhi wahi se” → red flag 🚨.
Internal control testing ke liye inquiries powerful tool hain:
Objective se related question pucho.
Example: “What controls ensure all orders are genuine?” → Answer should reference GDN signatures.
💡 Audit Technique (Inquiry & Benchmarking):
Client se process poochho (Invoice kaise banti hai, Sales/Lease accounting kaise hoti hai).
Uske jawab ko noting down karo aur check karo ke controls exist karte hain ya nahi.
Wrong answer bhi evidence hai → agar control missing hai toh wahi auditor ka focus ban jata hai 🎯.
📊 Lease Accounting Example (Side Note):
Client ne bataya: “Same rate use karte hain har lease pe.” ❌
Reality: Har lease ke time ka apna market rate hota hai.
Result: Poori lease accounting galat thi → FS misstatement.
📌 Assertion Risk: Valuation/Accuracy (lease rate incorrect).
🔹 Invoicing Risks
Invoice ban sakti hai galat tariqe se:
Quantity wrong source se li → SO instead of GDN ❌
Price galat uthaya → agar system me update na ho.
Risk: Understatement / Overstatement of Sales.
Example: 400 GDN issued but sirf 350 invoices bani → completeness issue (understatement). 📉
🔹 Invoice Recording & General Ledger (GL)
Invoices banne ke baad → record GL me hota hai.
Risks:
Invoice hi na bani → GL me miss hogi (completeness). ⚠️
Invoice wrong amount se record ho → misstatement (existence/accuracy).
Control: Reconciliation b/w GDN, Invoices & GL entries 🔄
🔹 Key Controls in Invoicing System
Existence Check Control 🛡️
Finance staff jab SO no. dalta hai → system verify karega ke SO exist karta hai ya nahi.
Agar SO missing → invoice ban hi nahi sakti ❌
Duplicate Check Control 🔁
Prevents multiple invoices against same SO / GDN.
System auto-block karega duplicate entry.
Limit Check Control 📏
Har SO ke liye quantity ka cap hota hai.
Agar poori quantity invoice ho chuki hai → system aur invoices banne nahi dega.
🔹 Audit Objective 🎯
Ensure:
All invoices issued (completeness).
Invoices recorded correctly (accuracy).
Sales not overstated/understated (validity).
Procedures:
Inquiry with client (process understanding).
Observation of controls in action.
Reconciliation testing.
🔑 Key Concepts:
Future Orders & Numbering
Sales order numbers never end → numbering system ensures uniqueness 🔢
Duplicate control ≠ limit check → alag cheez hai ✅
Internal Control Weakness (ICW)
Definition: Jab control design sahi nahi hai ya exist hi nahi karta ⚠️
Auditor ka kaam: Identify → Understand → Note down → Report.
Applications of ICW
Audit Implication: More work for auditor, higher risk of misstatement.
Management Implication: Business risk (loss, errors, fraud).
Communication
Weaknesses likh ke Management letter me record hoti hain 📩
Report Management + Those Charged with Governance (TCWG) dono ko.
Not an extra task, yeh auditor ke normal kaam ka hissa hai ✔️
Drafting Technique (Exam focus)
Exam me 15–20 marks ka question aata hai:
Identify Weakness (Deficiency) ❌
Explain Impact (Risk/Implication) ⚡
Suggest Recommendation (Control fix) ✅
Shortcut Writing Formula
Deficiency → “Control missing / wrongly designed”
Implication → “May lead to misstatement / fraud / inefficiency”
Recommendation → “Control should be designed / implemented / checked”
Examples Given
Credit limit not set →
Deficiency: “Credit limit not set by independent person.”
Implication: “Risk of bad debts / over-crediting customers.”
Recommendation: “Credit limits should be set & approved by independent authority.”
Invoice based on SO instead of GDN →
Deficiency: “Invoices prepared using Sales Order only.”
Implication: “Risk of overstated sales / wrong quantity billed.”
Recommendation: “Invoice should use Quantity from GDN & Price from SO.”

📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🌟 Intro
Exam me approach:
Missing / flawed control identify karo ✍️
Us ka FS pe impact likho ⚡
Recommendation do ✅
🛠️ Walkthrough Concept
Walkthrough = transaction ko practically trace karna → pehle kya hota hai, baad me kya hota hai.
Purpose: Samajhna ke control exist karta hai ya nahi, aur iska FS par kya impact hai.
🏭 Case: SPL (Production & Marketing of Tech)
Sales Rep (Aslam) → customers dhoondta hai (yeh theek hai ✅).
Customer order deta hai → us order me credit limit likhi hoti hai.
Sales Rep credit limit confirm karta hai verbally director operations se 📞.
❌ Problems
Verbal Confirmation of credit limit (no written proof).
Risk: dispute ho sakta hai → later deny kar diya jaye.
Segregation of Duties issue
Sales Rep khud sales bhi kar raha hai + credit limit confirm bhi kar raha hai.
Risk: Sales Rep zyada credit dilwa ke apna sales target achieve kar sakta hai → bad debts ka risk 💸.
📌 Impact
Sales ho sakti hai un customers ko jo pay na karein.
Receivables badh jayenge → doubtful debts risk ⚠️.
✅ Recommendation
Credit limit ka written documentation ho (email/signed approval).
Credit approval independent person kare.
Proper segregation of duties maintain ho.
👤 Performer Qualification
Control check karte waqt yeh dekhna hota hai → jo banda perform kar raha hai kya wo qualified & experienced hai bhi ya nahi ✅.
Agar banda unqualified hai → control ka design flawed hai ⚠️.
📦 Dispatch Process
Supervisor warehouse se goods dispatch karta hai.
Delivery note banata hai (multiple copies).
Issues:
No quality & quantity check before dispatch 🚫.
Risk → wrong / damaged goods deliver ho sakte hain → customer complaints + goodwill damage.
Customer acknowledgement (sign) missing ✍️.
Risk → customer deny kar dega → loss of inventory risk.
Unfulfilled orders track nahi hote → partial supply issue → goodwill damage.
📑 Pre-numbering of Docs
Delivery notes (GDN) pre-numbered hone chahiye 🔢.
Manual pre-numbering bhi acceptable hai (jaise invoice books, carbon copies, bakery slips, bank deposit slips) → agar sequence maintain ho raha hai to ok.
Risk tab hai agar numbering hi na ho → missing records, fraud chances.
🔄 Copies Distribution
Delivery note ki copy Sales Order Dept. ko jani chahiye.
Purpose → record maintain karna ke kaunse orders fulfill hue / pending hain.
Agar copy nahi jati → incomplete records → risk of disputes & wrong outstanding balances.
🧾 Invoicing Process
Factory accountant manual invoice prepare karta hai.
Issues:
Invoice prepared using Sales Order (not GDN) ❌.
Risk → Invoice quantity ≠ actual dispatched quantity → misstatement of sales & receivables ⚠️.
Pre-numbering of invoices essential 🔢 → taake reconciliation aur settlement possible ho.
📌 Impact
Wrong / duplicate billing → misstated revenue & receivables.
Loss of inventory (if no acknowledgement).
Customer dissatisfaction → goodwill damage 😕.
✅ Recommendations
Proper quality & quantity checks before dispatch.
Customer ka acknowledgement/signature lena.
Pre-numbered delivery notes & invoices maintain karna (manual or system-based).

Current design → Credit limit check ho rahi hai invoice banate waqt ❌.
Sahi design → Credit limit check honi chahiye order receive karte waqt ✅.
Risk:
Goods deliver ho jate hain non-creditworthy customers ko.
Bad debts ka risk ↑ ⚠️.
📌 Impact
Incorrect revenue recognition (invoice ≠ actual dispatch).
Credit sales to unworthy customers → bad debts.
Control ka design flawed hai.
✅ Recommendations
Invoice should be prepared using GDN for quantity and Sales Order for price.
Credit limit should be checked at time of order acceptance, not invoicing.
Segregation of duties → Invoice preparer ≠ Credit checker.
🕒 Timely Invoicing
Sometimes invoices delayed in sending to customer 📩.
Risk →
Working capital cycle disturb hota hai ⏳.
Payment delays → finance cost ↑ (interest on borrowings).
Recommendation → Invoices should be sent timely along with delivery note.
💻 Recording & Aging
Invoices entered weekly / monthly in computer.
Issue → Aging analysis delayed / incomplete ⚠️.
Risk → Management ko timely pata nahi chalta kitna receivable outstanding hai.
Recommendation → Records update daily / real-time.
🔒 Missing Controls
GDN not matched with original purchase order → incomplete verification 🚫.
Customer acknowledgement (sign on GDN) missing ✍️.
Impact → risk of disputes, loss of inventory.
Recommendation → GDN should be matched with customer PO & signed copy retained.

Definition → always lift from case (e.g., “credit limit checked after new order”).

HR defines Basic Pay.

Employee card swipe karega → entry/exit record hoga.

🔹 Link with Payroll
System: Total working hours × Daily Rate = Salary.
Wage workers (factory staff) → attendance directly linked to per-day wages.
Office staff → salary portion bhi attendance se linked.
🔹 Manual vs Automatic Mechanism
Automatic System → clock card, biometric, camera; accurate but costly ⚡
Manual System → attendance register, supervisor verification; cheap but less reliable 📋
Cost-Benefit Analysis 💹 → agar system cost > expected fraud loss, to controls avoid karte hain.
Example: small office me 20–25 employees → expensive biometric system ka benefit low.

Reliability ❌
No proper monitoring 👀
CFO ya supervisor check hi nahi karte → loopholes.

Attendance khud employee na likhe.

Manual + Independent Verification
Factory labour → supervisor mark kare.

Prevents fake overtime.

Minimum aur maximum limits set hoti hain.

Prevent karein false claims ya inflated payroll expenses.
⚠️ Risk (if Control Removed):
Overstatement of Expense:
Agar control remove kar diya jaye to Wages & Salaries Expense overstate ho sakta hai 💸.
Occurrence Assertion Violation:
Recorded overtime may not have actually occurred.
Authorization Risk:
Without review, unapproved payments process ho sakte hain 🚨.
📚 Assertions Affected:
Occurrence: Recorded work may not have happened.
Accuracy: Overtime amount incorrect record ho sakta hai.
Authorization: Payments processed without proper approval.
💡 Recommended Controls / Suggestions:
✅ 1. Multi-Level Authorization:
Overtime sheet pe signature required from Supervisor + Department Head before payroll processing. 🖋️
✅ 2. Exception Reporting:
System should auto-generate report for all rejected or abnormal overtime claims 📊.
This improves transparency and control evaluation.
✅ 3. Periodic Review:
Department head should periodically review overtime patterns to identify misuse or inefficiency. 🔍
✅ 4. Training & Awareness:
Supervisors and HR staff should understand control objectives clearly. 🎓
💻 Topic: Payroll System Controls & Segregation of Duties
🔹 Scenario Overview:
This part explains the authorization and verification controls in payroll processing — particularly focusing on how passwords, system access, and segregation of duties impact internal control efficiency and fraud prevention ⚙️.
⚙️ Key Control Procedures:
System Authorization:
Payroll data (like working hours and overtime) automatically transfer hoti hai from time recording system to the payroll software 💾.
This process is controlled by a password or authorization code 🔐 — only authorized users can transfer or modify payroll data.
Password Control:
Har department ek specific code word (authorization password) use karta hai — for example, “domestic cat” 🐱 code name for payroll file.
Problem: Yeh code sab ko pata hai 😬 — means any staff member can access and alter payroll data.
Weakness: Shared passwords reduce system confidentiality and allow unauthorized changes.
Payroll Calculation & Data Flow:
System automatically computes gross wages, overtime, and deductions.
Final net pay list is sent to the accounts department 🏢.
Accounts clerk then verifies bank details before salaries are transferred to employee accounts.
Segregation of Duties (SOD):
Problem: Same clerk verifies and authorizes payroll payments 😐.
This is a clear violation of segregation of duties principle.
⚠️ Control Weaknesses:
Shared Passwords: Unauthorized persons can change or tamper payroll data 🧾.
Lack of Independent Authorization: Same person verifying and approving payroll = risk of manipulation 💣.
System Access Not Restricted: Absence of user-level access control exposes payroll data to fraud.
💡 Applications / Risks:
Risk 1: Payroll tampering or salary transfers to fake accounts 💸.
Risk 2: Unauthorized overtime addition.
Risk 3: Payroll expense overstatement in FS 📊.
Risk 4: Violation of internal control objective — accuracy, authorization & safeguarding of assets 🔒.
📚 Assertions Affected:
Authorization: Payments without proper approval.
Accuracy: Incorrect payroll calculations.
Occurrence: Non-existent (ghost) employees paid.
🔧 Recommendations / Suggested Controls:
✅ 1. Strong Password Policy:
Passwords should be unique, complex, and confidential. 🔐
Change regularly and restrict access to authorized staff only. 🔄
✅ 2. Independent Review:
Payroll authorization should be by Department Head or Senior Manager, not the same clerk who prepares it. 👨‍💼
✅ 3. Access Control System:
Implement user-level access with audit trails 📜.
Record all modifications in the system log.
✅ 4. Dual Authorization for Payroll Transfer:
Two signatures required before payment release (Maker–Checker principle) 🖊️✅.
✅ 5. Segregation of Duties Enforcement:
Separate roles for data input, verification, and authorization.
This prevents fraud and error.
🧾 Topic: Payroll Controls, Email Acknowledgment, and Employee Termination Process
🔹 Scenario Overview:
This lecture continues the payroll internal control discussion — focusing on authorization timing, acknowledgment controls, and employee termination 🧩. It explains how weak coordination between HR, payroll, and accounts can lead to salary overstatements or unauthorized payments.
⚙️ Key Control Concepts:
Timing of Review:
Financial accountant verifies total wages after few weeks — which is too late. ⏰
Ideally, review should occur before payment, not after.
Control Objective: detect errors before money leaves the company 💰.
✅ Recommendation:
“Review of wage amount should be done before payment, not weeks later.”
Segregation of Duties:
Same person preparing, verifying, and paying salaries ❌.
Duties should be segregated: preparation → verification → authorization (by senior or independent official).
Without segregation, risk of fraud or error increases.
👥 Employee Termination Process:
Current Process:
When an employee leaves, HR sends an email to payroll department notifying termination 📩.
Payroll team must stop salary from next cycle.
Weakness: HR sends email, but no confirmation (acknowledgment) that payroll received or acted on it.
Technology Control:
Introduce email acknowledgment receipt system 📨 — ensures HR knows payroll has seen the message.
In organizations like EY, this is standard practice — sender receives auto-confirmation once email is opened. ✅
Purpose of Control:
Prevent salary payments to ex-employees 🏦.
Ensure records updated immediately upon termination.
If Control Missing:
Payroll might continue paying ex-employees = salary overstatement risk ⚠️.
Unnecessary expense recorded → financial misstatement.
💡 Application / Risks:
Risk 1: Salary paid to terminated staff (ghost employees).
Risk 2: Payroll expense overstated 💸.
Risk 3: Weak monitoring → loss of funds + accountability issues.
📚 Assertions Affected:
Occurrence: Payments made to non-existing (ex) employees.
Completeness: Some terminations not recorded in payroll.
Accuracy: Payroll expense figures incorrect.
🔧 Recommendations:
✅ 1. Acknowledgment Control:
Implement auto-confirmation email receipts for HR → Payroll communication.
Ensure payroll staff must acknowledge receipt before changes take effect.
✅ 2. Termination Checklist:
HR must follow structured checklist to confirm:
Employee removed from payroll list.
Final settlement approved.
✅ 3. Pre-Payment Verification:
Finance or audit team should verify any large or unusual payroll variances before release. 📊
✅ 4. Access Control:
Remove system access (logins, cards, IDs) of terminated employees immediately 🔐.
🧠 Audit Insight (Directional Testing):
When performing audit testing, focus on directional testing — check where the risk originates.
Example: Auditor checks employees who left the company, then verifies if their salaries stopped.
If any ex-employee still received payment → indicates control failure + overstatement risk 🚨.

🔹 Inventory Process Overview
Inventory ek asset hoti hai jo balance sheet me show hoti hai. 🧾
Jab hum inventory purchase karte hain to entry banti hai:
Inventory (Debit) → Trade Payable (Credit)
Matlab humne maal khareeda, asset badh gaya aur liability create ho gayi.
🔹 Objective of Inventory Process
Objective ye hai ke sirf zaroori inventory order ki jaye. ✅
Do main reasons:
Holding cost zyada na ho (extra maal rakhne se cost barhti hai).
Over-purchase loss se bachna (agar demand kam ho to price gir jati hai).
🔹 Controls in Inventory Process
Management lagati hai controls taake unnecessary order na ho.
Auditor check karta hai ke process properly follow ho raha hai ya nahi.
Auditor ka main kaam: inventory purchase process ko understand aur verify karna.
🔹 Auditor ka Perspective
Auditor yeh dekhta hai ke organization ka inventory system controlled aur documented hai.
Wo check karega ke proper authorization aur evidence maujood hain ya nahi.
🔹 Inventory Debit / Payable Credit Timing
Sir ne bola: “Inventory Debit aur Payable Credit to baad me hota hai, pehle decide hota hai ke order karni hai ya nahi.”
Pehla step hota hai Decision to Order — yeh depend karta hai Re-order Level par.
🔹 Re-order Level Concept
Re-order level ka matlab hai wo point jahan quantity itni kam ho jaye ke naya order dena zaroori ho jaye.
Example: Agar company ka re-order level 5,000 units hai — jaise hi inventory 5,000 tak girti hai, system automatically order initiate kar deta hai.
Re-order level depend karta hai:
Product ki demand par 📊
Delivery time (kitna time lagta hai nayi inventory aane me)
🔹 Automatic Controls
Re-order level ke liye automatic controls lagaye ja sakte hain:
Limit Check Control: Jab inventory minimum level se neeche jaye to system alert kare ya exception report generate kare ⚠️
Just-in-Time (JIT) Arrangement: System dusre system se linked hota hai, aur jaise hi re-order point aata hai, automatically purchase order generate ho jata hai 🔁
🔹 Purchase Order Generation
Jab hum khareed rahe hote hain to hum Purchase Order (PO) banate hain.
Ye PO supplier ko bheja jata hai taake wo goods deliver kare.
Is se pehle sirf order decide hota hai, PO create hone ke baad hi purchase process formally start hoti hai.
🔹 Control for Avoiding Holding Cost
Control lagaya jata hai taake unnecessary inventory hold na ho.
Control ke examples:
Re-order level setting
Limit check control
Just-in-Time arrangement
🔹 Risks / Implications
Agar re-order level set na ho to:
Inventory zyada order ho sakti hai → Holding cost increase 💸
Ya kam order ho sakti hai → Stock-out risk ⚠️
Agar control missing ho, to NRV losses bhi ho sakte hain (jab inventory ka market value cost se neeche chala jaye).
🔹 Determining Order Size
Re-order level set karne ke baad decide hota hai ke kitna order karna hai.
Factors:
Demand quantity (sales orders pending kitne hain)
Available stock (warehouse me kitna maal hai)
Buffer stock (safety margin rakha jata hai future demand ke liye)
🔹 Demand & Supply Link
Sales orders aur warehouse stock ka link hona zaroori hai.
Agar linkage missing ho to:
Wrong quantity order ho sakti hai (zyada ya kam dono).
Control linkage: Sales Order ↔️ GDN (Goods Dispatch Note)
Agar yeh link missing ho, to record incomplete hoga aur ordering me error ho sakta hai.
🔹 System Automation Insight
Advanced systems me yeh poora process automated hota hai:
Sales order system se link hota hai,
GDN automatic generate hoti hai,
Re-order level check hota hai,
JIT system ke zariye purchase order auto create ho jata hai.
🔹 Sales Order aur GDN Linkage
Agar ek Sales Order (e.g., #1024) ke multiple GDNs (Goods Dispatch Notes) ban jayein aur phir bhi kuch quantity deliver na ho, to wo system me Remaining Quantity ke form me record hoti hai.
System me ek Limit Check Control hota hai jo ye ensure karta hai ke agar unfulfilled quantity minimum level se zyada ho jaye to auto order place ho jaye (Just-in-Time system ke zariye).
🔹 Manual System (Non-Automated Environment)
Agar company fully automated nahi hai (jaise Pakistan me aam hai), to process manual hota hai.
Example:
Demand = 50,000 units
Available stock = 2,000 units
Required = 48,000 units
Sales aur warehouse department milke ye identify karte hain ke kitni quantity order karni hai.
🔹 Purchase Requisition (PR)
Jab demand aur available stock ka difference nikalta hai, to Purchase Department ko inform kiya jata hai.
Ye information Requisition Form ke zariye hoti hai — jise Purchase Requisition (PR) kehte hain.
PR ka matlab: Request to purchase goods.
🔹 Buffer Stock Concept
Buffer stock wo quantity hoti hai jo demand fulfill hone tak backup ke liye rakhi jati hai.
Example:
Agar lead time (delivery ka time) 5 days hai aur 3,000 units roz consume ho rahe hain, to buffer stock ≈ 15,000 units rakha jata hai.
Purpose: Jab tak naya maal nahi aata, operation continue rahe.
🔹 Purchase Department Controls
Purchase department tab tak Purchase Order (PO) generate nahi karega jab tak:
Re-order level cross na ho jaye 📉
Proper requisition document receive na ho 📄
Demand aur buffer stock verify na ho jaye ✅
Ye control ensure karta hai ke Unnecessary Inventory Purchase na ho.
🔹 Segregation of Duties
Sales Department: Demand identify karta hai.
Purchase Department: Purchase initiate karta hai.
Purpose: Fraud aur over-ordering se bachna.
Sales wala apni taraf se directly purchase nahi kar sakta.
🔹 Practical Example (Fixed Asset Purchase Analogy)
Jaise agar kisi employee ko mobile chahiye, wo directly khareed nahi sakta.
Pehle Requisition raise karega → Manager approve karega → Purchase department process karega.
Yani har purchase ke liye proper approval chain honi chahiye.
🔹 Approved List of Suppliers (ALS)
Purchase department ke paas ek Approved List of Suppliers hoti hai.
Jab requisition aati hai, purchase officer:
Quotations mangwata hai from multiple approved suppliers.
Compare karta hai pricing aur quality.
Select karta hai best supplier (lowest price + good quality).
🔹 Purpose of Quotation Process
Fraud aur favoritism avoid karna.
Ensure karna ke:
Price fair ho 💰
Quality acceptable ho ✅
Company ke funds misuse na hon 💼
🔹 Head of Purchase Responsibility
Head of Purchase final approval deta hai.
Uska kaam hai:
Lowest price aur good quality supplier select karna.
Final Purchase Order (PO) issue karna.
🔹 Responsibility for Quotations & Purchasing
Quotation mangwana: Purchase department ko hi responsibility di jati hai — wo approved suppliers se quotations lete hain.
Koi bhi employee apni marzi se purchase nahi kar sakta; approved process follow karna zaroori hai.
Agar kisi specific vendor ka exclusive right ho (e.g., telecom towers), to quotations limited vendors se li ja sakti hain (policy based).
🔹 Approved List of Suppliers (ALS)
Company ka Approved List of Suppliers hota hai — sirf inhi se quotations aur purchases recommended hote hain.
ALS se suppliers ko select karne se quality aur pricing control hota hai.
🔹 Quotation Evaluation & Approval
Purchase officer multiple quotations (usually 3+) le kar price + quality compare karta hai.
Lowest price + acceptable quality select karna standard practice hai.
Agar deviation chahiye (higher price ya non-approved supplier), to additional approval (e.g., CEO/MD) required hoti hai.
🔹 Segregation of Duties / Governance
Segregation of duties: Sales, Purchase, Receiving, Accounts alag teams hongi. Kisi ek bande ke paas poora control nahi hona chahiye.
Internal Audit aur Audit Committee governance ko monitor karte hain.
Audit Committee ka chairman independent director hota hai — wo CEO se bhi findings report karta hai.
🔹 Internal Audit Role / Monitoring
Internal audit purchase process, approvals, supplier selection, aur quotations verify karta hai.
Audit ka kaam hai controls ka verification — na keval documents dekhna, balki activity bhi verify karna.
🔹 Fraud Red Flags (Common Issues)
Price/Value mismatch: Invoice value market price se zyada ho (valuation issue).
Pay for goods not received (“Pay for the goods not actually purchased”) — major fraud sign.
Single-person procurement without quotations or approvals.
Use of non-approved suppliers or favoritism in selection.
🔹 Controls to Prevent Fraud
Require multiple quotations and maintain comparison sheet.
Use Approved Supplier List and restrict purchases to ALS unless higher approval.
Purchase Requisition (PR) must be supported with demand documents (sales orders, GDNs) and approved before PO.
PO Numbering and multiple copies: copies for Purchase, Finance, Warehouse (for delivery matching).
Goods receipt required before inventory recording and payment processing.
🔹 Document Flow & Recording
Sales/Consumption → Demand identified.
Requisition (PR) raised with supporting docs.
Quotations from approved suppliers.
Quotation evaluation → Approval by Head of Purchase.
Purchase Order (PO) generated (with PO number, qty, price, supplier, date).
Goods Receipt / GRN → Warehouse receives and issues GRN.
Invoice & GRN match → Finance records liability and processes payment only after verification.
🔹 Practical Controls & Workflow Notes
PO alone = no inventory entry. Inventory recorded only on receipt (GRN) and matching invoice.
Maintain three/four copies of PO: Purchase, Supplier, Warehouse, Finance.
Head of Purchase ensures lowest price + quality; he approves final PO.
For high value or exceptional purchases, additional higher-level approval mandatory.
🔹 Audit Verification Tips
Auditor checks: quotations, ALS, PR → PO → GRN → Invoice → Payment chain.
Look for inconsistencies: missing quotations, incorrect valuation, PO without PR, invoice > PO, payment before receipt.
Focus on controls effectiveness, not just presence of documents.

🔹 Quick Recap of Inventory Process
Risk: Company bila wajah maal order na kare.
Control: Re-order Level aur JIT Arrangement lagaye jate hain.
Purchase tab tak nahi hoti jab tak:
Re-order Level na arrive kare,
Requisition create na ho,

🔹 Demand Creation Concept
Business me demand ka idea Sales Orders aur Available Stock ke comparison se aata hai.
Formula: Sales Orders − Available Stock = Quantity to be Ordered 📊
🔹 Requisition Process
Ye Warehouse ya Sales Department dono me se koi initiate kar sakta hai.
Requisition banne ke baad wo Purchase Department ke paas jati hai.
Purchase Department ka role: Purchase initiate tab kare jab need confirm ho.
🔹 Quotations & Approved Supplier List
Purchase Department Approved List of Suppliers (ALS) se hi quotation mangwata hai.
Purpose: Kisi non-approved ya unreliable vendor se goods order na ho.
Example Control: “Pay for the goods not actually ordered” risk avoid karna.
Agar koi item 100 Rs ki hai to system ensure kare ke 200 Rs ka payment na ho.
🔹 Controls Example (Manual & Automatic)
Manual Control: Purchase Order manually banate waqt supplier aur price manually likhe jaate hain (quotation ke base par).
Automatic Control: System me Access Rights aur Limit Checks lage hote hain jisse supplier list change na ho sakti.
🔹 System-Based Procurement Example (e.g., PTCL)
Purchase portal pe employee order tabhi kar sakta hai jab:
Requisition received ho,
Supplier list fixed ho,
Access Rights restricted hon.
User “Send to Order” click kare to system auto-email karta hai approved suppliers ko.
Agar koi supplier approved list me nahi hai, system usko block kar deta hai.
🔹 Control Objectives
Unapproved Vendor se purchase avoid karna 🛑
Control: Approved List of Suppliers + Access Restriction.
Overpricing se bachna 💸
Control: Quotation process + Price comparison sheet.
🔹 Purchase Order (PO) Creation
PO banate waqt system automatically:
Quantity pick karta hai requisition se,
Price pick karta hai quotations se.
Har PO ka unique number hota hai taake tracking ho sake (for audit trail).
PO copies multiple departments ko milti hain:
Supplier, Finance, Warehouse, aur Self Copy.
🔹 Goods Receiving Stage
Jab goods warehouse me aate hain, receiving clerk check karta hai:
PO number ke against delivery match hoti hai ya nahi.
Agar PO missing hai → goods accept nahi honge 🚫
Is step me control ye ensure karta hai ke excess ya unauthorized goods receive na ho.
🔹 Quality Check & Impact on Financials
Quality check compulsory hai ✅
Agar quality check nahi hoti to risk hota hai:
Inventory value overstated ho jati hai,
Ya defective goods record ho jate hain.
Audit Perspective: Ye Existence assertion ka risk hai (goods physically exist nahi karte ya acceptable condition me nahi hote).
🔹 Quality Check & Its Impact
Agar Quality Check nahi hoti to inventory me problem aata hai ❌
Sir ne bola: “Quality check nahi hui to inventory me issue aayega, apko ye yaad rakhna hai.”
Reason: Quality check na hone se recorded quantity aur actual quantity mismatch hoti hai.
🔹 Affected Audit Assertions
Completeness – Transaction properly record nahi hui ho sakti.
Valuation – Wrong value record hone ka risk hota hai.
Existence – Inventory physically exist nahi karti lekin books me record ho jati hai.
🔹 Understanding the Risk (Existence Risk)
Management ka claim hota hai ke transaction hui hai — lekin auditor verify karega ke actually hui bhi hai ya nahi.
Example:
Purchase entry: Inventory Dr → Payable Cr.
Lekin agar goods physically aaye hi nahi, to transaction record karna false existence risk hai ⚠️
Result: Overstatement of Inventory due to recording items that don’t exist physically.
🔹 Explanation with Example
Suppose 10,000 units record kiye gaye hain, lekin actual me 5,000 aaye hain.
To recorded figure zyada ho gaya — ye Existence assertion ka violation hai.
Risk statement: > “Inventory might be overstated due to goods recorded but not physically received.”
🔹 Why This Happens
Jab Quantity Check Control missing hota hai, tab recording without verification hoti hai.
Transaction record ho jati hai bina physical check ke.
Is se management ka claim galat ho jata hai — ke goods exist karte hain.
🔹 Auditor’s Assessment
Auditor control test karega: kya quantity properly check hoti hai? ✅
Agar control absent hai, auditor conclude karega: > “Risk of Overstatement in Inventory due to lack of quantity verification.”
🔹 Correct Drafting for Exam
Risk likhne ka sahi tariqa: > “If quantity is not checked before recording, inventory may be overstated as goods may not actually exist.”
🔹 Key Learning
Existence Assertion = Physical presence verify karna.
Completeness Assertion = Koi transaction chhuti na ho.
Valuation Assertion = Correct value par record karna.
🔹 Completeness vs. Existence Difference
Agar 5,000 units hone chahiye the lekin 10,000 record kar diye gaye — iska matlab Overstatement hua 📈
Agar kuch goods record nahi hue jabke aaye the — to Completeness ka issue hai 📉
Yani:
Existence issue: Record kiya hua maal physically exist nahi karta.
Completeness issue: Physical maal aya, par record hi nahi hua.
🔹 GRN (Goods Received Note)
Jab goods receive hote hain to GRN banayi jati hai.
GRN ek proof document hota hai ke maal physically aaya hai.
GRN me likha hota hai:
Date of receipt
Quantity received
Supplier name
PO (Purchase Order) reference number
GRN par Receiver aur Supplier dono ke sign hote hain ✍️
🔹 Purpose of GRN
GRN ensures ke sirf wahi maal record ho jo actually deliver hua hai.
Isse duplicate ya fake recording avoid hoti hai.
Jab tak GRN nahi banti, purchase entry (Inventory Dr → Payable Cr) record nahi hoti.
🔹 Linking GRN with PO
GRN hamesha Purchase Order (PO) ke reference se match hoti hai.
Agar GRN PO se zyada quantity show kare to system alert karega ⚠️
Ye control prevent karta hai ke excess quantity record na ho.
🔹 Updating Inventory Sheet
Jaise hi GRN ban jaye, warehouse inventory sheet update hoti hai.
Agar sheet update nahi hui, to next time Sales Department ko lagega maal kam hai.
Wo phir naya order karega → unnecessary holding cost aur NRV losses 💸
🔹 Example
Pehle 5,000 units the, 10,000 aur aagaye → total 15,000 ho gaye.
Agar record update nahi hua aur sales wale ko lagta hai abhi bhi 5,000 hain, to wo aur order kar dega.
Result: Over-purchase, holding cost, aur NRV loss.
🔹 Quality vs. Quantity Check
Quantity Check: Kitni goods aayi hain — Existence assertion se link.
Quality Check: Goods sahi condition me hain ya nahi — Valuation assertion se link.
Dono controls missing honge to:
Quantity mismatch → Overstatement / Existence risk.
Quality issue → NRV losses, valuation risk.
🔹 Auditor’s Focus
Auditor verify karega:
GRN properly prepared hai? ✅
GRN aur PO quantities match karte hain? 🔍
Quality inspection reports maintain hui hain? 🧾
Missing control = potential misstatement in Existence, Completeness, or Valuation.
🔹 Valuation & NRV Impact
Agar defective goods receive ho gaye aur quality check nahi hui, to:
Inventory value inflate ho jati hai.
Later sale me NRV loss aata hai (sell karni padti hai cheap rate par).
Example: > “If goods are not inspected for quality at the time of receipt, inventory may be overstated, leading to NRV losses or future sales returns.”
🔹 Government Grant Misclassification Example
Sir ne ek example discuss kiya jahan grant received for construction of an asset ko galti se income me record kar diya gaya tha ❌
Correct treatment: Agar grant asset ke construction ke liye hai to usay deferred income ke taur pe treat karte hain, aur asset ke useful life ke sath amortize karte hain.
Misclassification ka risk tab hota hai jab grant direct income me record ho jaye.
Risk: Misstatement due to misclassification of capital grant as revenue income.
🔹 Capitalization vs. Expense Understanding
Agar grant ya borrowing asset financing ke liye hai, to related cost capitalized hoti hai.
Agar operating expense ke liye use hui hai, to P&L expense ke taur pe treat hoti hai.
Wrong classification → valuation aur matching concept ka violation ⚠️
🔹 Single Supplier Risk
Jab ek hi supplier se regularly purchase hoti hai (Single Supplier Dependency), to risk ye hota hai ke:
Supplier apni pricing control kare 🧾
Company ko poor quality accept karni pade 😬
Fraud ya overpayment ho sakta hai.
Control Definition: > “Company should not rely on a single supplier; alternate quotations or backup suppliers must be maintained.”
🔹 Salary Control Example
Example: Agar salary prepare karte waqt purani salary slips se comparison kar rahe ho, to ye galat approach hai ❌
Correct control: HR records aur attendance system se verify karo.
Reason: Ho sakta hai koi employee resign kar gaya ho ya terminate hua ho — purani slip se compare karne se error hoga.
Risk: Overstatement of salary expense due to outdated employee data.
🔹 Invoicing & Recording Concept
Jab goods deliver ho jaate hain aur invoice supplier se receive hoti hai, tab purchase entry record hoti hai.
Entry: Inventory Dr → Trade Payable Cr
Lekin agar invoice delay ho jaye to inventory aur payable dono record late honge.
Proper control: Three-way matching system 🔍
Purchase Order (PO)
Goods Received Note (GRN)
Supplier Invoice
🔹 Matching Process Explanation
Jab supplier invoice aati hai, finance department usay verify karta hai:
Price → PO se match kare
Quantity → GRN se match kare
Quality → Inspection report se verify kare
Agar sab match hota hai tabhi liability record hoti hai ✅
Risk: If invoice is recorded without matching PO and GRN, overstatement of expenses or payables may occur.
🔹 GRN to GL Flow
Jab GRN banti hai, tab inventory sheet update hoti hai — not the General Ledger (GL).
GL entry tab hoti hai jab invoice verify aur match ho jati hai.
Iss se ensure hota hai ke sirf valid aur verified purchases hi financials me record hon.
🔹 Recording Inventory at Time of Invoice
Agar finance team inventory ko record karti hai invoice receive hone par, to ye Completeness risk create karta hai ⚠️
Reason: Goods warehouse me aa chuki hoti hain (exist karti hain) lekin abhi record nahi hui.
Result: Financial statements me understatement of inventory and liabilities ho sakta hai.
Risk: If inventory is recorded at the time of invoice receipt instead of actual receipt, completeness assertion may be violated.
🔹 Completeness Assertion Clarified
Existence: Record me item hai, par physically nahi — overstatement.
Completeness: Item physically hai, par record nahi — understatement.
Agar GRN (Goods Received Note) ban chuki hai lekin invoice delay ho gaya hai → Completeness issue.
🔹 Financial Statement Impact
Agar goods receive hue hain par invoice nahi aayi →
Inventory physically exist karti hai ✅
Record nahi hua ❌ → Completeness issue.
Iska impact:
Inventory understated
Liability understated
Financial statements inaccurate ho jati hain.
🔹 Control: Three-Way Matching
Finance department ko invoice record karte waqt ye 3 cheezein match karni chahiye:
Purchase Order (PO) – Quantity & price reference.
Goods Received Note (GRN) – Quantity actually received.
Invoice – Supplier billing confirmation.
Control Objective: Ensure every recorded purchase is valid, complete, and correctly valued.
🔹 Invoicing and Payment Controls

🧠 6. Control Applied – Four-Eyes Principle 👀👀
Definition: Every FAR entry is verified by two independent persons.

Checker: Reviewer who validates entry correctness before posting.

🧩 Fix Asset Confirmation Process – Discussion
✅ Control Objective:
Purpose yeh dekhna tha ke:
“Asset physically exist karta hai ya nahi?”
Kya yeh PP (Property, Plant) mein exist karta hai?
Aur inventory/warehouse mein verify ho raha hai ya nahi?
➡️ Control Objective: Ensure physical existence of asset aligns with what’s in GL (General Ledger).
🧱 Identified Deficiency (Definition C):
🚨 Deficiency Statement (Incorrect Example):
“Asset confirmation is sent by the person in charge of the asset to the Admin Department on an annual basis.”
📌 Issue:
Yeh statement weak hai — self-confirmation is not a strong internal control.
Jis ke paas asset hai, wohi confirm karey? This lacks independent validation.
📎 Correct Deficiency Statement Should Be:
“Fix asset confirmation is performed by the person in charge of the asset, rather than an independent party.”
🧠 Logic:
GL mein jo exist karta hai, woh shayad physically na ho.
Independent party chahiye jo verify kare, warna insider misstatement ka risk hai.
🛠️ Application (Practical Impact):
Agar confirmation by asset holder hoti hai:
Insider bias ho sakta hai 🤐
GL aur physical asset mismatch ka risk ⚠️
💡 True Application:
“If confirmation is performed by the same person who controls the asset, they may confirm incorrect info, resulting in overstatement in GL.”
📢 Correct Recommendation:
Recommendation:
“Fix asset confirmation should be performed by an independent person who physically verifies the asset and sends the report to the Admin Department.”
Frequency:
Annual basis preferred ✅ (unless risk level suggests more frequent checks).
Sir ne bataya ke usually annually hi hoti hai — aur yeh acceptable hai.
💬 Key Insights from Sir:
Wrong practice: Same person (asset holder) confirms asset — highly flawed.
Correct practice:
Independent person jaaye physically verify kare ✅
GL entries ko validate kare with actual existence
Why?
Because this eliminates bias & ensures accurate reporting 🧾
Sir ne emphasis diya ke:
Deficiency define karo → uska impact samjhao → phr recommendation do ✔️
“Recommendation doge, to samjhao ke karne se hoga kya. Sirf likhna nahi, logic bhi dena hai.”
✅ Clarified Understanding of Control Deficiency
❌ Weak Design (Recalled from Part 1):
Confirmation is sent by the same person in charge of the asset ➡️ Biased / unreliable
✅ Deficiency Statement:
Lack of segregation of duties — same person confirms the asset who is responsible for it.
💡 Impact:
Asset exists in GL, but may not exist physically.
Misrepresentation ho sakta hai — control is flawed.
🛠️ Recommendation:
An independent person should physically verify the asset and send confirmation to Admin department.

📦 Duplicate Purchase Order Control Case
🧩 Scenario Given:
System allows generation of multiple POs against the same Purchase Requisition (PR).
❌ Deficiency Statement:
“System allows creation of duplicate POs against a single purchase requisition.”
🔥 Impact:
“This might lead to ordering goods that are not required, causing NRV loss or unnecessary inventory.”
➡️ Iska matlab: Organization ke resources waste ho sakte hain.
✅ Recommendation:
“System should implement a duplicate check control that prevents creation of more than one PO against the same PR.”
💡 Control Type:
If automated: Apply ID-based check within system (unique PR identifier)
If manual: PR marked as “PO Created” after first use to prevent repetition
🔁 Clarification on ‘Routine Basis’ vs. ‘Frequency’
Sir ne yeh misconception clear kiya:
🔄 Routine basis ≠ daily — har company ki routine frequency alag hoti hai (monthly, yearly, etc.)
✅ “Routine” ka matlab: “As per company’s set frequency” — e.g., annually, quarterly
🗣️ Example:
“Our company counts assets annually — that is our routine.”
📌 Lesson:
“Routine basis” ka matlab samajhna zaroori hai — usko “daily” assume mat karo ❗
🧾 Additional Control Case: Purchase Order Repetition
🧩 System Issue:
System allows multiple POs against same PR
💣 Impact:
Leads to excess ordering, inventory bloat, or NRV loss
Example: Same item ordered twice unknowingly — wastes company resources
🛠️ Recommendation:
“System should automatically restrict duplicate PO generation by using unique PR tags or flags.”
➡️ Whether automated or manual, duplicate prevention must exist:
Manual → Mark PR as “used” after first PO
Automated → PR number can’t be reused
🧾 PO Generation & Prevention Control
🔍 Scenario:
User ne PR banaya jisme kuch items missing hain.
System ne assistant ko restrict kiya ke duplicate PO na banaye.
✅ Correct Control:
“System should prevent PO generation against same PR by implementing ID-based check or flag after PO is created.”
🛠️ If automated: Use unique PR number to lock further PO creation. 🛠️ If manual: Stamp PR as “PO Created” once used.
📌 Purpose: Prevent unintentional reordering and financial mismanagement.

⚠️ Issue:
Control too late — PO ban chuka hota hai.
Budget check at this stage is detective, not preventive.
✅ Correct Control Point:
Budget check should happen at PR or PO creation stage.
📌 Logic:
Asset purchase must align with department’s approved budget before ordering.
💡 Impact of Missing Budget Control:
Department may purchase in excess of its budget limit 💸
Leads to:
Over-procurement
Waste of resources
NRV losses

Excess assets beyond need = Impairment risk
Asset may become obsolete or underutilized

👀 When auditing:
Focus on process gaps
Identify where controls are missing or ineffective
Recognize risks arising from poor controls
📌 Example:
If asset is purchased beyond need or budget, it could impair valuation.

🧱 Fixed Asset Recording Process
Jab organization fixed assets acquire karti hai, to unka recording process aur control design accurately maintain karna zaroori hota hai.
Recording Flow:
Jab asset receive hota hai → Goods Received Note (GRN) banti hai.
GRN ke basis par asset Fixed Asset Register (FAR) me record hota hai.
Invoice receive hone par liability aur valuation finalize hoti hai.
General Ledger (GL) me entry tab hoti hai jab invoice mil jati hai.
⚠️ Completeness Issue
Agar company asset ko tab tak record nahi karti jab tak invoice na mile, to problem hoti hai:
Asset physically exist karta hai lekin GL me record nahi hota.
Ye completeness issue create karta hai, kyunki FAR me asset hai lekin GL me missing hai.
Example: Asset deliver ho gaya lekin invoice late aayi, to GL entry delay hui. Is se asset record incomplete ho gaya.
Impact:
Asset record incomplete rehta hai.
Financial statements understate hoti hain.
Audit testing me missing entries samne aati hain.
🧩 Control Weakness
Process me fault ye hai ke asset tab tak GL me record nahi hota jab tak invoice na mile.
Is wajah se FAR aur GL me synchronization break hoti hai.
Result:
Asset physically maujood hota hai par books me record nahi hota.
✅ Recommended Control
Improvement:
Asset ko FAR me record karna chahiye jab GRN generate ho. Invoice ka wait nahi karna chahiye.
Baad me reconciliation perform karni chahiye FAR aur GL ke beech.
Recommendation:
Reconciliation prepare ki jaye between Fixed Asset Register (FAR) and General Ledger (GL) to ensure completeness.
📦 Inventory Recording Issue
Organization advance payment ke waqt hi inventory record kar leti thi jab ke goods physically receive nahi hue hote.
Is practice se inventory overstate hoti hai aur existence issue create hota hai.
Problem:
Payment ke time inventory record ho jati hai.
Goods receive hone ke baad GRN banne ke bajaye pehle hi entry pass hoti hai.
System me inventory ka balance zyada dikhai deta hai, jab ke physical count kam hota hai.
Example:
Advance payment ki base par goods record hue lekin warehouse me physically available nahi.
Result: Inventory books me zyada, actual me kam.
⚠️ Impact on Financial Statements
Existence Issue: Inventory record hui lekin physically exist nahi karti.
Classification Error: Advance payment ko Inventory me record kar diya gaya jab ke use Advance to Supplier me record hona chahiye tha.
COGS Distortion: Jab goods deliver nahi hue phir bhi record kiye gaye, to Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) wrong calculate hota hai.
Overstatement Risk: Financial statements me inventory balance overstate hota hai.
🧾 Correction & Classification Adjustment
Correct Treatment:
Advance payment ko Advance to Supplier account me record kiya jaye.
Inventory tab record ho jab GRN (Goods Received Note) ban jaye.
Jab goods receive ho jayein, tab advance amount adjust ki jaye.
Example:
Advance payment 5 million ki thi. Goods abhi tak receive nahi hue.
Ye amount “Advance to Supplier” me dikhni chahiye, na ke “Inventory” me.
🧩 Recommended Control
Control Steps:
System me control lagaya jaye ke inventory entry sirf GRN ke generate hone par hi ho.
Advance payments ko Inventory module me record karne ki permission disable ho.
Periodic reconciliation ki jaye between Advance Accounts aur Inventory Records.
Purpose:
Ensure karna ke sirf physically received goods hi inventory me record hon.
🧱 Asset Removal Policy
Asset ko Fixed Asset Register (FAR) se tabhi remove karna chahiye jab actual disposal ho jaye.
Disposal ka matlab: asset sold, scrapped, ya damaged beyond use ho chuka ho — aur proper approval mil chuki ho.
Sirf zero WDV (Written Down Value) hone ka matlab nahi ke asset remove kar diya jaye.
⚠️ Issue Identified
Company prematurely assets ko FAR se remove kar rahi thi bina disposal ke.
Result: Asset physically exist karta tha lekin FAR me record nahi hota tha.
Problem Points:
Existence issue: Record me nahi, par ground par asset maujood.
Misappropriation risk: Tracking remove hone se asset chori hone ka khatra badhta hai.
Reconciliation problem: FAR aur physical verification match nahi hoti.
✅ Correct Control Design
FAR se removal tabhi ho jab disposal transaction authorized aur documented ho.
Tagging system maintain rakha jaye — har asset ka tag aur custodian updated ho.
Zero WDV assets ko remove na karein, unko status “Idle” ya “To Dispose” mark karein.
Physical verification regular basis par ki jaye — annual aur surprise checks dono.
Reconciliation between FAR aur physical count sheets compulsory ho.
🧾 Proper Disposal Workflow
Initiation: Concerned department disposal request raise kare.
Verification: Asset ka tag, location, aur status verify kiya jaye.
Approval: CFO ya Asset Committee se disposal approval li jaye.
Documentation: Sale invoice, scrap note, ya damage report attach ki jaye.
Accounting Entry: Disposal entry record ho aur gain/loss recognize ho.
FAR Update: Disposal date aur supporting docs FAR me record hon, phir asset remove ho.
🧮 Accounting Example
On Disposal:
Dr. Bank / Scrap Proceeds
Dr. Accumulated Depreciation
Cr. Asset Cost
Dr./Cr. Gain or Loss on Disposal (balancing figure)
🧠 Auditor’s Focus
Check karein ke koi asset FAR se prematurely remove na hua ho.
Zero value assets ke physical presence verify karein.
Annual count aur reconciliation reports review karein.
🧩 Asset Recovery & Tracking
Jab employee resign karta hai ya transfer hota hai, uske paas allocated assets ka handover karna mandatory hota hai.
Company HR/Administration department asset recovery ko monitor karta hai.
Control Objective: Ensure karna ke assigned assets (e.g., laptops, devices) return ho jayein before clearance.
Process:
Asset allocation record maintain hota hai (with employee name & branch).
On exit, HR/Admin calls & verifies handover status.
Supporting documents (allocation slips, tags) trace kiye jaate hain.
Missing items report kiye jaate hain aur responsible person se recovery hoti hai.
Risk: Agar tracking proper na ho to high-value assets (e.g., bank laptops worth lakhs) misuse ya theft ka risk badhta hai.
🏦 Governance & Clean Records
Kuch institutions FAR se assets remove karte hain taake register “clean” lage — lekin yahi approach control weakness create karti hai.
Clean FAR ≠ Accurate FAR.
Agar asset FAR se delete ho gaya lekin physically exist karta hai, to reconciliation aur tracking impossible ho jati hai.
Auditor’s View: Real control tab hai jab record aur physical existence dono sync me hon.
📦 Inventory & Bin Card Update
Problem: Bin Cards warehouse me timely update nahi ho rahe.
Concept:
Bin Card = warehouse record of quantity available per item.
Jab Goods Delivery Note (GDN) banti hai, tabhi Bin Card update hona chahiye.
If Not Updated:
Inventory record wrong rahega.
Warehouse staff samjhega ke stock available hai jab ke actually sell ho chuka hoga.
Leads to over commitment & stock-out risk.
Example:
System me 50 units dikh rahe hain lekin physical count 40 hai — Bin Card update delay ke wajah se.
⚙️ Control Recommendations
Immediate Bin Card Update: GDN ke saath hi inventory record update ho.
Reorder Triggers: System automatically alert kare jab stock threshold cross kare.
Warehouse & GL Reconciliation: Periodically match warehouse quantity aur system record.
Employee Exit Protocol: HR clearance tab tak na de jab tak asset handover confirm na ho.
Documentation: Every handover/disposal ka proper acknowledgment maintain ho.
Lec-20 – Petty Cash Cycle
💸 Petty Cash System 💼💵📌
🧾 Definition:
Petty Cash ek choti cash amount hoti hai jo daily office expenses ke liye use hoti hai (e.g., tea/snacks, local conveyance). 💬🗂️🧾
⚙️ Process:
Organization petty cash ki daily requirement define karti hai (e.g., 25,000 – 30,000 limit).
Employee petty cash withdrawal ke liye request raise karta hai.
Cash Payment Voucher (CPV) prepare hota hai with supporting bills.
CFO/Admin se approval liya jaata hai.
Available cash balance check hota hai.
CFO signed cheque ke zariye petty cash withdraw hota hai.
Cash secure safe ya lockbox mein rakha jaata hai.
🔍 Practical Insight:
Agar petty cash requirement 30,000 hai, aur already 5,000 available hai, toh sirf 25,000 ka cheque sign hoga. 💡🧾✅
⚠️ Risk:
Agar petty cash ki defined limit se zyada cash rakha gaya to: - Cash chori hone ka risk barhta hai. - Policy violation hoti hai. - Zyada withdrawal ka misuse ya unauthorized use ho sakta hai. 🚨💸📉
💬 Assertions:
Existence: Agar cash physically maujood nahi hai, to record fake ho sakta hai.
Valuation: Cash amount accurately recorded hona chahiye.
Rights: Sirf authorized log petty cash ka use karein. 📊📋📌
📟 CPV Approval System 🖋️📄✅
🧾 Definition:
Cash Payment Voucher (CPV) petty cash spending ko document karta hai. Ismein approval aur relevant supporting documents lagte hain. 📑📝🧾
⚙️ Process:
Employee petty expense ke liye CPV initiate karta hai.
Admin assistant CPV fill karta hai.
Supporting bills (e.g., receipts, chits) attach kiye jaate hain.
CFO ya Admin Head se approval liya jaata hai.
Approved CPV ke basis par petty cash release hota hai.
⚠️ Risk:
Agar CPV approval process follow nahi kiya gaya to: - Unauthorized log personal use ke liye cash nikaal sakte hain. - Duplicate vouchers issue ho sakte hain. ⚠️📛🔒
💬 Assertions:
Occurrence: Agar transaction actually hui nahi aur record mein dikh rahi ho to woh fake entry hogi.
Authorization: Agar CPV approved nahi hai, to transaction valid nahi hai. 📎🛡️📜
🔍 Example:
Admin Head ki approval limit 10,000 hai. Agar koi CPV 9,800 ka hai, to woh Admin Head approve karega. 10,001 se zyada amount ke liye CFO ka sign zaroori hai. 🧾🖊️📌
🔁 Cash Reconciliation 📊💵🧮
🧾 Definition:
Cash reconciliation ka matlab hai petty cash ka actual balance match karna with vouchers aur system record (GL entries). 🧾📈📚
⚙️ Process:
Day-end par CPVs collect kiye jaate hain.
GL entries ke sath CPVs match kiye jaate hain.
Physical cash count hoti hai.
Agar difference mile to woh record kiya jaata hai aur investigate hota hai.
Reconciliation independent person karta hai (segregation of duties). ✅
⚠️ Risk:
Agar reconciliation timely aur accurately nahi hui to: - Over ya under reporting ho sakti hai. - Cash fraud ya error detect nahi ho paayega. ⚠️📉🚨
💬 Assertions:
Completeness: Agar koi CPV miss ho gaya, to records incomplete honge.
Existence: Agar cash record mein hai par physically nahi mila, to woh existence assertion fail karega. ✅📌📋
🔍 Example:
GL amount: 10,000 Recorded vouchers: 9,000 → 1,000 ka farq = fraud ya galti ki possibility 📊🔍📉
📌 Surprise Cash Count 🕵️‍♂️💰📅
🧾 Definition:
Surprise cash count ek sudden branch visit hota hai jahan actual petty cash verify kiya jaata hai. 🧾📋🔍
⚙️ Process:
Opening balance note kiya jaata hai.
Us duration ke vouchers subtract kiye jaate hain.
Jo closing balance expected hai usse compare kiya jaata hai actual cash se.
Agar difference ho to uska investigation hota hai.
⚠️ Risk:
Agar branch ko pehle se pata ho: - Toh woh advance adjustment karke fraud hide kar sakti hai. - Actual cash shortfall detect nahi hoga. ⚠️🚫🕳️
💬 Assertions:
Existence: Jo cash record mein hai woh physically hona chahiye.
Rights & Access: Cash sirf authorized person ke access mein ho. 🔐📍🛡️
🔍 Example:
Expected: 18,000 Actual: 15,000 → 3,000 shortfall = issue logged 📉🚨🔍
🏦 Bank & Cheque Controls 💳📁🔐
🧾 Definition:
Bank aur cheque controls ensure karte hain ke cheques sirf approved transactions ke liye issue hon, aur access secure ho. 🏦📑✅
⚙️ Process:
Cheques sirf approved vouchers par issue hon.
No pre-signed cheques.
Checkbook limited access mein ho.
Passwords strong aur regularly change hon.
OTP/Dual approval bank transaction ke liye.
⚠️ Risk:
Agar passwords ya cheques sab ke paas hon to: - Unauthorized withdrawal ho sakta hai. - Pre-signed cheque misuse ho sakta hai. 🔓⚠️🚨
💬 Assertions:
Authorization: Har cheque authorized person ke signature se hi nikle.
Occurrence: Har transaction real aur documented honi chahiye. 📋🧾✅
🔍 Example:
System alert deta hai: “Invoice pehle pay ho chuki hai – dobaara mat karo.” ⚠️🔁🚨
🧠 Preventive, Detective & Authorization Controls Summary
🔐 Preventive Control:
Woh control jo pehle se hi galti ya fraud ko hone se roke. - Example: Limited access to checkbooks, approval before CPV
🔎 Detective Control:
Woh control jo galti hone ke baad usay identify karta hai. - Example: Daily reconciliation, system alerts
✅ Authorization:
Kisi bhi transaction ke valid hone ke liye uska authorized hona zaroori hai. - Example: CFO or Admin signature before payment approval
🔐 Call-Out: Never compromise segregation — same person shouldn’t own the entire cycle. This ensures transparency and fraud prevention. 🛡️🔍📌
Lec-21 – Internal Control Elements
📚 Reference: Book Pages 42–44
📐 Five Elements of Internal Control
Control Environment – Yeh woh mahol hai jahan controls operate karte hain. Agar top management ethical aur responsible ho, toh neeche tak discipline maintain rehta hai.
Example: Company mein har choti chori pe action hota hai, jisse deterrence create hota hai 🛑
Entity’s Risk Assessment – Organization khud assess karti hai ke kaun se business risks uske objectives ko affect kar sakte hain, aur kya un risks ke liye controls design kiye gaye hain.
Example: Agar company credit sales karti hai, toh bad debts ka risk assess karna zaroori hai 📉
Information System Relevant to FS – Financial reporting tak ka pura data flow system. Jaise PO generate hua, GRN bana, invoice receive hui, aur payment process hui – yeh sab traceable hona chahiye.
Example: Payment sirf tab ho jab GRN aur invoice dono available hon 📑
Control Activities – Yeh woh daily actions hain jisse risks mitigate hote hain, jaise approvals, maker-checker roles, reconciliations.
Example: Petty cash voucher approve hone ke baad hi cash issue hota hai ✅
Monitoring of Controls – Yeh ensure karta hai ke controls sahi kaam kar rahe hain. Internal audit ya management review ke through monitoring hoti hai.
Example: Internal audit team quarterly testing karti hai aur Audit Committee ko report karti hai 🔍
🔐 Whistleblowing & Code of Conduct
Whistleblowing: Confidential reporting system jahan employee unethical act ko anonymously report kar sakta hai.
Code of Conduct: Har employee ko diya jata hai – acceptable aur unacceptable behavior define karta hai.
Example: EY mein quarterly Code of Conduct email aata tha, jisme likha hota tha: “yeh karna allowed hai, yeh nahi.”
Risk: Agar Code of Conduct (CoC) issue nahi kiya gaya ya employees tak formally nahi pohnchaya gaya, toh fraud hone ke chances barh jate hain, kyunki logon ko acceptable aur unacceptable behavior ka structure samajh nahi aata. 🔒
Control: Signed acknowledgment of CoC + Disciplinary policy
📊 KPI-Linked Performance Risks
Concept: KPI (Key Performance Indicator) agar performance-based ho to motivate karta hai—but agar galat cheez pe incentive mile toh fraud hoga.
Examples: ✅ HR ko bonus: agar employee turnover kam ho
❌ HR ko bonus: jitne zyada penal actions lene
Risk: Agar KPIs galat cheezon se linked hon (jaise zyada penal actions lena), toh toxic culture develop hota hai jahan log jaan bujh kar colleagues ko punish karte hain taake apna bonus le saken.
Control: Performance KPIs should be ethical and conflict-free, jismein employee development, teamwork, aur genuine improvement ko reward kiya jaye.
