📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔑 Main Concept
Auditor kab expert use karta hai? Jab auditor ke paas apni expertise (knowledge/competence) kisi specific area mein nahi hoti.
Standard ka naam: Using the Work of Auditor’s Expert.
📝 Key Breakdown
Related Party recap
Pichli class mein Related Party (RP) khatam hua tha.
RP risks: normal ho sakte hain, control risks ho sakte hain, going concern risks ya RP risks.
Auditor ko in sab ko evaluate karna hota hai.
Auditor’s Expert ki need
Jab financial statement mein aisi cheez ho jo auditor ki knowledge se bahar ho.
Example: Fair Value of Investment Property.
Auditor ko evidence lena hoga expert se, kyun ke auditor khud valuation expert nahi hota.
Case Example – Property Valuation
Client ki investment property ka fair value 500 million ka jump dikhata hai → auditor ko suspicious lagega.
Property valuer hire kiya gaya → certified by Pakistan Engineering Council.
Auditor decide karta hai ki is expert ka kaam use kare.
Risk Point
Agar management ka expert use kar liya jaye bina independent review ke → Bias aur Conflict of Interest aa sakta hai.
Independence impaired → Auditor ki objectivity compromise ho jayegi.
Auditor vs Management Expert
Auditor Expert → independent, auditor ke liye kaam karega.
Management Expert → client ke behalf pe valuation ya opinion deta hai.
Auditor ko management expert ke kaam ko blindly accept nahi karna.
Examples
Property Valuation (fair value gains).
Legal Cases / Pension liabilities (complex legal matter, auditor ke paas law expertise nahi hoti).
Forensic reports, actuarial valuations, environmental reports, etc.
🔑 Main Concept
Auditor har wo area jahan uske paas apna competence/knowledge nahi hai, wahan expert appoint karega.
Expert kaam sirf support evidence ke liye hota hai, final judgment auditor ka hota hai.
📝 Key Breakdown
Legal Cases (Law Expert)
Auditor legal expert nahi hota → auditor sirf accounting/auditing samajhta hai.
Agar case ka decision uncertain ho (pension cases, litigation, tax disputes) → auditor ko lawyer ki opinion leni hogi.
Example: Company ka legal advisor letter deta hai → auditor ko verify karna hoga ki kya us letter ka claim reliable hai ya biased.
Internal vs External Expert
Expert internal bhi ho sakta hai (firm ke andar) ya external (bahar se hire).
Auditor ko assess karna hai: kya expert ki independence/objectivity intact hai?
Telecom Example – Permanent Establishment in Tax
Foreign operators (Zain, Vodafone, etc.) Pakistan network use karte hain → tax ka issue banta hai.
Concept: Permanent Establishment (PE) → agar foreign company ka Pakistan mein fixed place of business ho, tab tax liability banti hai.
Auditor ko decide karna hai ki:
Agar khud ke paas enough knowledge hai → expert ki zaroorat nahi.
Agar complex interpretation hai → legal/tax expert ki zaroorat hogi.
Insurance Example – IBNR (Incurred But Not Reported Claims)
Insurance companies mein balance sheet par liability hoti hai → claims incurred but not reported.
Auditor ko actuarial expertise chahiye hoti hai → actuary se claim development tables aur assumptions samajhne padte hain.
Example: Marine insurance – ship dub jaye to claim months baad report hota hai → liability record karni padti hai.
1. Knowledge vs Expert Reliance
Auditor ka role: Agar auditor ke paas knowledge hai but application aur execution uski capacity se bahar hai → Expert ki zarurat.
Example: Claim Development Table (IBNR – Insurance)
Auditor samajh sakta hai framework aur logic.
Lekin calculation aur estimation expert ka kaam hai (actuary / industry specialist).
👉 Key Point: Auditor ki zimmedari audit opinion ki hai. Agar expert galat hoga, tab bhi zimmedari auditor ki hi rahegi.
2. Risks in Using Experts
Control Weakness: Agar management ne expert appoint kiya but unke kaam ka review/control nahi hai → High risk of material misstatement.
Reliance Check: Auditor ko yeh dekhna hoga ke:
Expert ka kaam logically sahi hai?
Auditor ke paas usko verify karne ke liye sufficient knowledge hai?
3. Claim Development Example (Insurance)
Timeline Example:
2020 → Accident hua.
2021 → Reported claim aya.
2022 → Aur bhi delayed claims report huay.
Claim Development Table:
Year-wise reported claims record.
Unreported claims ke liye actuarial estimation.
Complexity Level: High → requires expert involvement.
4. Auditor ki Responsibilities Before Appointing Expert
Auditor ko evaluate karna hoga:
Kya auditor khud perform kar sakta hai?
Agar sufficient expertise hai (training, education, prior experience) → Ho sakta hai expert ki need na ho.
Kya management ne expert appoint kiya hai?
Agar management expert rakhti hai (e.g., IBNR ke liye actuary) → Auditor ko bhi uske kaam evaluate karna aur rely karna padega.
Risk of Material Misstatement (ROMM):
Agar area high risk hai (complex valuation, estimation, actuarial assumptions) → Strong chance auditor ko expert chahiye hoga.
Engagement Team ki Capacity:
Kya kisi team member ke paas relevant specialization already maujood hai?
Agar nahi → External expert appoint karo.

📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔹 Expert Appointment ki Need Kab Parti Hai?
Jab auditor ke paas confidence ya competence nahi hota kisi specialized area me.
Typical areas:
Legal advisors (lawsuits, legal matters ⚖️)
Taxation matters
Actuarial science (insurance, pensions, etc.)
Valuation of assets (investment property, infrastructure)
💡 Complex areas (e.g. actuarial calculations, ECL – Expected Credit Loss) → auditor ko expert appoint karna padta hai.
🔹 Purpose of Using Expert
2 key objectives:
Whether to use an expert’s work – kya is case me expert ki zarurat hai?
Whether to use the work of management’s expert – agar management ne expert appoint kiya hai, kya auditor uska kaam rely kar sakta hai?
🔹 Factors for Considering Need of Expert
Risk of Material Misstatement (high risk → expert ki need)
Complexity of subject matter
Auditor’s own knowledge / experience
Management ka expert use (agar management ne expert use kiya hai → auditor bhi evaluate karega)
🔹 Evaluating Competence & Capability of Expert
Before appointing expert, auditor should check:
Competence & Qualification
Relevant degree, certification, membership (e.g. Actuarial Society, Engineering Council).
Experience
Past engagements in same industry.
Prior work with auditor (positive track record).
Independence & Objectivity
No conflict of interest.
Neutral stance.
Market Reputation
Published reports, research, references from others.
🔹 How Auditor Can Evaluate Expert?
Discussion / Meeting → test knowledge, ask relevant questions.
Previous Experience → same expert ka past performance review.
Published Reports → credibility aur data sources check karna.
Reference Checks → peers, industry, other auditors.
Professional Membership → accredited body ka member hai ya nahi.
🔹 Risk of Appointing Wrong Expert
Agar expert competent nahi hai → wrong evidence mil sakta hai.
Auditor phir misstatement conclude nahi kar paayega.
Responsibility auditor ki hi rahegi, expert ki nahi ❗
🔹 Example
Property Valuation Expert → check karna hoga ke wo Pakistan Engineering Council ya relevant body ka certified member hai.
Actuarial Expert → Actuarial Society membership.
Legal Expert → Licensed lawyer.
🔹 Valuation Example – Tower Valuation
Auditor ko samjhaya gaya example: Telecom towers (e.g. Ufone, Telenor) 🏗️
Expert ne valuation ki base kiya:
Material (steel, iron weight) ⚙️
Market price compare karke multiply
Depreciation adjust karke final value nikalna
Yeh normal auditor ka kaam nahi hai → specialized expert ki zarurat.
💡 Auditor ko yeh samajhna hai ke expert ne kaunsi basis / method se valuation ki.
🔹 Auditor ka Role vs Expert ka Role
Auditor ka kaam = Evaluate karna expert ki report
Expert ka kaam = Technical valuation provide karna
Final responsibility auditor ki hoti hai, expert sirf helper hai ✅
Auditor ko check karna hota hai:
Competence, capability, objectivity of expert
Methodology used (cost method, replacement cost, income method)
🔹 Competence vs Capability
Competence = Knowledge + Qualification (e.g. actuarial, legal, engineering)
Capability = Ability to apply that knowledge practically 💪
Example: Auditor ko ECL (Expected Credit Loss) sikhaya gaya → theory seekh li, lekin bank audit me practically apply karna capability hai.
🔹 Experience ka Role
Experience = Knowledge ko real situations me apply karna
Auditor ki tarah expert ka bhi past experience evaluate karna hota hai.
Example: Expert ko pata hai zung lagne se tower ki value kam hogi → kyunki usne market transactions observe ki hoti hain 👀
🔹 Documentation ka Importance
Sir ne emphasize kiya: Auditor ko har evidence document karna zaroori hai ✍️
Agar expert ka kaam rely kar rahe ho → file me likhna hoga:
Kis expert kaam use hua?
Kya methodology thi?
Kya auditor ko reliance mila?
Agar documentation nahi hui → regulator objection raise karega ❌
🔹 Valuation Methodologies
Cost Method
Tower me kitna material hai (iron/steel weight × market price)
Depreciation adjust → final value.
Replacement Cost Method
Agar aaj naya tower banayein → uski cost kitni hogi?
Used when asset ko replace karna ho.
Income Method
Tower se kitni income / cash inflows generate ho rahi hain (e.g. telecom signals revenue).
Us basis pe valuation.
⚖️ Har method ka use depend karta hai → management ka intent (sell karna hai, use karna hai, ya replace karna hai).
🔹 Auditor ka Challenge with Expert’s Work
Expert ki report ko blindly accept nahi karna 🚫
Auditor ko evaluate karna hota hai:
Kya methods relevant hain?
Kya assumptions reasonable hain?
Kya source data reliable hai?
💡 Auditor ne ensure karna hai ke expert ki kaam se audit evidence sufficient aur appropriate ban raha hai ✅
🔹 Example – Building Valuation 🏢
Expert ne bola: Building ki value = 10 crore
Auditor ka role:
Expert se puchna: kis method se calculate kiya?
Cost method use hua ya income method?
Assumptions valid hain?
Agar expert ne depreciation factor ignore kar diya → auditor ko doubt raise karna chahiye ❗
🔹 Auditor ki Documentation Responsibilities ✍️
Har cheez record honi chahiye:
Expert ka naam, qualification
Method used (cost, income, replacement)
Assumptions aur inputs
Auditor ka evaluation
Agar regulator (ICAP, PCAOB, etc.) file review kare → har step documented hona chahiye 📑
🔹 Limitation of Auditor’s Knowledge
Auditor expert nahi hota engineering, actuarial science ya law me ⚖️
Lekin auditor ko reasonable understanding develop karni hoti hai expert ka method samajhne ke liye.
Example: Auditor ko actuarial valuation formulas detail me samajhna zaroori nahi, lekin concept samajhna compulsory hai.
🔹 Risk of Using Expert
Agar expert ki report flawed ho → auditor ka opinion galat ban sakta hai ⚠️
Auditor ko safeguards rakhne chahiye:
Cross-check with alternate evidence
Challenge unrealistic assumptions

📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔹 Expert ka Purpose Recap
Expert hire karne ka main sawaal: “Use karna hai ya nahi?”
Auditor evaluate karta hai:
Kya management ne expert use kiya? 👥
Kya expert ka data reliable hai?
Auditor ki apni knowledge / team capability sufficient hai ya external expert chahiye?
🔹 Competence & Qualification Check
Auditor check karega:
Degree / certification 🎓
Experience / past work
Publications ya industry reputation
Example: agar expert actuarial kaam kar raha hai → uska actuarial qualification hona chahiye.
💡 Past experience important: agar auditor ne pehle bhi us expert kaam dekha hai → reliability zyada hogi.
🔹 Subject Matter Expertise
Dekhna hai: jo area audit ho raha hai, usi me expert skilled hai ya nahi?
Example: building valuation ke liye civil engineer suitable hai 🏗️, lekin actuarial expert nahi.
🔹 Objectivity / Independence Check ⚖️
Expert ka judgement bias-free hona chahiye.
Risks:
Self-interest conflict 💰
Relationship with management 👥
Safeguard:
Management se written representation lena ke expert independent hai.
Expert se bhi written confirmation lena ke koi conflict of interest nahi.
🔹 Understanding Expert’s Field
Auditor ko expert ki field aur methodology ka basic samajhna zaroori hai.
Example: valuation ke methods (cost, income, replacement) auditor ko samajhne chahiye 📊
Check karna ke expert ki approach industry standards ke mutabiq hai ya nahi.
🔹 Data Sources Evaluation
Expert ka data internal vs external dono ho sakta hai:
Example external: Insurance Association of Pakistan ka accident data 🚗
Example internal: Company ka apna aging report 📑
Auditor ko ensure karna hai:
Data reliable hai ✅
Agar entry point (input data) galat hai → output bhi flawed hoga ❌
🔹 Engagement with Expert
Auditor aur expert ke beech formal agreement hona chahiye:
Scope of work defined
Responsibilities clarify
Deliverables aur timeline set
Example analogy: Bike mechanic 🛵
Agar mechanic (expert) sirf “temporary fix” karega to kaam reliable nahi.
Auditor ko ensure karna hoga ke expert kaam properly kare.
🔹 Expert vs Auditor Discussion
Sir ne clarify kiya: expert ≠ auditor 👨‍💼👨‍🔧
Auditor expert ki working ko samajhne ke liye us se understanding le sakta hai.
Agar firm me dusra auditor ya partner ne kaam kiya hua hai → us se bhi samajh le sakta hai ✅
Lekin final responsibility auditor ki hi hai ⚖️
🔹 Relevant Requirements ka Check
Auditor check karega:
Kya expert ki field relevant hai accounting/auditing requirements ke liye?
Example: agar ECL (Expected Credit Loss) ya IBNR calculate karni hai → kya expert ko IAS/IFRS requirements ka awareness hai? 📊
Agar expert kaam karta hai lekin accounting standards follow nahi karta → output unreliable ❌
🔹 Agreement me Documentation
Agreement me likhna zaroori hai:
Nature of Work → Expert se kya karwana hai? e.g. ECL calculation, IBNR, property valuation 🏠
Scope & Limitations → Kis boundary tak kaam karna hai, kya cover hoga aur kya exclude hoga ✍️
Objective of Work → Auditor ka maksad kya hai? e.g. material estimate verify karna, financial statement impact assess karna ⚖️
💡 Sir ne bola: Yeh 3 cheezein (Nature, Scope, Objective) auditor ko expert ke saath clearly document karni hain.
🔹 Example: Employees’ Gratuity (IAS 19)
Company apne employees ko gratuity deti hai → final salary × service years ka %
Ye estimate complex actuarial calculation hota hai:
Future salary estimate
Employee turnover (churn rate) 👥
Discounting (present value of future obligation) 💰
Is liye auditor expert ko engage karega → expert actuarial assumptions aur data analysis karega ✅
🔹 Auditor ka Role vs Expert ka Role
Auditor: Review & evaluate karega ke expert ka method aur assumptions sahi hain ya nahi.
Expert: Detailed calculation aur data analysis karega.
Example:
Agar expert ne replacement cost method use kiya property valuation me jab required tha value-in-use → auditor usko accept nahi karega ❌
🔹 Standards aur Methodology
Agreement me likhna hoga ke expert kaam karte waqt relevant standards follow kare.
Example:
IAS 19 (Employee Benefits)
IFRS 9 (Financial Instruments – ECL)
IAS 40 / IAS 16 (Property Valuation)
Yeh ensure karega ke expert ka output GAAP/IFRS compliant ho ✅
🔹 Methodology Clear Ho Gayi ✅
Sir ne kaha: methodology aur approach ab clear hai, ab hum practical angle pe chal rahe hain.
Final responsibility hamesha auditor ki hoti hai ⚖️, expert sirf support deta hai.
🔹 Ek hi Kaam ke liye Multiple Experts? 🤔
Question aya: kya ek hi kaam ke liye 2 experts ho sakte hain?
Sir ne clarify kiya:
Generally ek hi field ka ek expert hota hai.
Lekin agar nature alag ho jaye (e.g. property vs telecom towers valuation) → alag experts hire kiye ja sakte hain 🏢📡.
Objective same ho sakta hai (valuation) but nature different → multiple experts allowed.
🔹 Documentation ke Key Points ✍️
Agreement me expert ko clearly likhna hai:
Nature of Work → kis type ka kaam hai.
Scope → kis extent tak kaam cover karega.
Objective → kis maksad ke liye auditor use kar raha hai.
💡 Auditor expert ko ye bhi batayega ke audit report sign hone se pehle agar koi update aayi toh expert inform kare 🔄.
🔹 Effective Date & Testing Period 📅
Expert ko clearly batana hoga ke kis date ki valuation chahiye.
Example:
FS close hoti hai 31 Dec → valuation bhi isi date ki honi chahiye ✅
Agar expert Feb ki valuation dega → mismatch ho jayega ❌
Isliye auditor ko agreement me effective date mention karni hai.
🔹 Professional & Industry Standards 📏
Expert ko apna kaam relevant professional/technical standards ke mutabiq karna hoga.
E.g.:
Valuation → Net Replacement Value ya Value-in-Use method.
IAS/IFRS ke relevant standards ka reference dena hoga.
🔹 Management’s Expert vs Auditor’s Expert
Question aya: agar management ne apna expert hire kiya aur auditor apna → dono ki opinion differ kar sakti hai?
Sir: Haan difference ho sakta hai.
Auditor apne expert ki opinion ko consider karega.
Agar dono ke results me material difference ho → auditor ko evaluate karke reporting me consider karna hoga 📊.
Important: Auditor sirf management ke expert pe blindly rely nahi karega.
🔹 Updates & Events After Date 🕒
Auditor expert ko bolega: agar audit report sign hone se pehle koi relevant event/update aaye toh usko communicate karo.
Example:
Dec ki valuation hui.
Jan/Feb me koi court case ya major event hua → expert auditor ko update karega.
🔹 Rare Fields with Limited Experts ⚠️
Question: kya koi field aisi ho sakti hai jahan expert replaceable hi na ho?
Sir: theoretically possible, lekin auditor ensure karega ke expert ke kaam ka scope clear ho.
Agar management normal banda kaam kar sakta hai → phir expert ki zaroorat nahi.
📝 Auditor–Expert Engagement Agreement Key Points
1️⃣ Planning Stage
Auditor jab appoint hota hai (e.g. 2023 audit ke liye), planning usi saal ke operations aur FS ke basis pe hoti hai. 📊
Auditor ko clear hona chahiye kiske financials audit karne hain & kon expert involve hoga.
2️⃣ Responsibilities Split
Auditor: assess karega data, expert ka kaam evaluate karega, aur apni report me reference karega.
Expert: calculation/valuation karega (e.g. ECL ageing data), aur apne working papers auditor ko allow karega. ✅
3️⃣ Agreement Clauses
Data Validation: Pehle hi decide hoga ke data (e.g. ageing, claims) kiska responsibility hai.
Discussion & Disclosure: Auditor expert ki valuation ko mgmt ke saath discuss karega & agar zaroorat hui to apni report me reference bhi de sakta hai.
Access to Records: Expert ko client ke records & details dekhne ka right milega (subject to confidentiality). 🔑
4️⃣ Confidentiality 🔒
Expert & auditor dono bound hain client data ko misuse na karne ke liye.
Medium of communication (email, secure portal) clearly define hoga.
5️⃣ Working Papers 🗂️
Auditor ke papers bhi expert review kar sakta hai (relevant area only).
Expert apne papers 5 saal tak preserve karega (audit documentation requirement).
6️⃣ Ownership & Control
Audit report auditor ki hoti hai, opinion auditor deta hai.
Expert ki valuation ek input hai, lekin ownership auditor ke conclusion ki hoti hai. ✍️
7️⃣ Association with Audit Report
Expert apna naam ya report market me auditor ke opinion ke saath independent use nahi kar sakta. 🚫
Auditor disclose kar sakta hai ki expert involve tha, but final responsibility auditor ki hai.
8️⃣ If Work Not Clear
Agar auditor ko expert ka kaam samajh nahi aata ya gap hai, toh auditor additional procedures karwayega.
Agreement me ye clear hona chahiye ke expert cooperation karega.

🔹 Recap from Previous Lecture
Sabse pehle auditor apna hello to use check karta hai → kya mai khud ye kaam kar sakta hoon ya nahi 🤔
Agar auditor khud kar sakta hai → expert ki need nahi.
Agar auditor ne decide kiya ke expert hire karna hai → uske liye factors evaluate karne hote hain.
🔹 Factors for Deciding Expert Appointment 👨‍💼
Nature & Complexity of Matter → kya ye area itna technical hai jo auditor khud assess nahi kar sakta? (e.g. IBNeR calculation, complex property valuation).
Materiality → agar area FS me material impact create karega to expert ki need zyada hogi ⚖️.
Management’s Work → agar mgmt khud expert appoint karti hai, auditor ko check karna hoga ke uska kaam reliable hai ya nahi.
🔹 Evaluating the Expert ✅
Auditor ko expert ki reliability assess karni hoti hai by checking:
Qualification 🎓
Experience 💼
Past Work References 🔍
Reputation in market
Agar doubt ho to auditor direct discussion with expert karega ya uske kaam ko verify karega.
🔹 Subject Matter Expertise 🔬
Auditor ko ensure karna hai ke expert ka subject matter relevant ho.
Agar expert ki field alag hai → uski opinion kaam ki nahi hogi ❌
🔹 Independence & Objectivity ⚖️
Auditor check karega ke expert independent hai ya nahi.
Methods:
Expert se written representation lena (no conflict of interest).
Management se inquiry karna about relationship with expert.
🔹 Engagement Agreement 📝
Agreement me ye cheezein clear define hongi:
Objective of work → e.g. valuation of insurance claims.
Methodology → expert kaunse standards/methods use karega (e.g. IBNeR, actuarial methods).
Effective Date → kis date ki valuation chahiye (mostly FS date).
Scope & Responsibility → expert ki vs auditor ki responsibility.
🔹 Auditor’s Understanding of Expert’s Field 📚
Auditor ko expert ki field ka basic understanding lena zaroori hai:
Applicable Standards (e.g. Insurance Rules 2017, IAS/IFRS).
Nature of Data Used → kya reliable hai ya nahi.
Limitations of expert’s work.
💡 Exam Tip: Auditor ko apni responsibility yaad rakhni hai → expert ki report final nahi, sirf input hai.
🔹 Example 💡
Agar expert IBNeR valuation kar raha hai → auditor ko ensure karna hoga ke:
Claim development data reliable hai 📊
Methodology (present value vs replacement cost) clearly documented hai.
Period of valuation specified hai.
🔹 Auditor–Expert Agreement Issues 📝
Kabhi bhi blind trust nahi karna expert par 🙅‍♂️ — auditor ko apna homework karna hota hai.
Agreement me expert ki responsibilities clearly likhni hoti hain:
Timely communication of issues ⏰
Access to records & data 📂
Independence maintain karna ⚖️
Confidentiality ensure karna 🔒
🔹 Communication Responsibility 📢
Expert ko auditor ko time par inform karna chahiye agar:
Koi limitation aa rahi hai ⛔
Koi data client se nahi mil raha ❌
Koi independence issue arise ho raha hai ⚠️
Agar expert deadline miss kare ya delay kare → auditor ka kaam ruk sakta hai (e.g. AGM deadline pressure).
🔹 Independence of Expert ⚖️
Auditor ko ensure karna hai ke expert ki objectivity impair na ho:
Written representation lena ke expert independent hai ✍️
Agar baad me independence issue arise hota hai → expert ko immediately report karna hoga 📢
Example: Expert ne client ki shares khareed liye → ab uski independence compromise ho gayi 🚫
👨‍🏫 Practical Treatment:
Aise case me auditor ek naya expert hire kar sakta hai 💸
Pehle expert ka kaam waste ho sakta hai, ya ek second expert uska kaam verify karega 🔍
🔹 Confidentiality 🔒
Agreement me likhna zaroori hai ke:
Expert client ka data misuse nahi karega 🚫
Sensitive data leak nahi hoga 🔐
Example: PDC L case → concern tha ke expert data apne fayde ke liye use karega → agreement me confidentiality clause add kiya gaya ✅
🔹 Limitation & Reservations ⚠️
Expert agar apna kaam complete nahi kar pata ya koi limitation hoti hai → wo auditor ko turant batayega.
Auditor ko assess karna hoga ke:
Kya limitation se expert ka kaam unreliable ho gaya hai ❌
Kya alternate expert ki zaroorat hai ✅
🔹 Auditor ka kaam after expert report
Jab expert apna kaam complete karke deta hai ➝ auditor ko blindly accept nahi karna chahiye ❌.
Auditor ko evaluate karna hai: kya expert ka kaam objective hai ya advocacy (biased) ho sakta hai?
Quality control inspection example: ek firm ne expert ki report bina review kiye accept ki ➝ regulator ne penalty lagayi 🚨.
Documentation me auditor ne show karna hai ke expert ka kaam kaise review kiya gaya.
🔹 Audit procedures on expert’s work
Check source data 📊
Agar expert ki working (jaise ECL/IBNR valuation) kisi data pe based hai ➝ us data ki reliability, relevance, accuracy, completeness check karni hai.
Example: ECL ke liye ageing of receivables chahiye. Auditor ko dekhna hai ke ageing properly ban rahi hai ya nahi.
Verify accuracy ✅
Sample invoices check karo (e.g. January 2020 ageing vs invoices issue date).
Ensure ke receivable balances aur due dates sahi reflect ho rahe hain.
Agar data irrelevant ya incomplete hai ➝ working unreliable hogi.
Check origin of data 🔍
Data kahan se aa raha hai? (e.g. client billing system).
Billing system ki reliability test karni hogi (controls, invoice issuance, GDN vs invoice matching).
Controls over data 🛡️
Data generation process pe controls check karna.
Example: ek staff assigned hai invoices generate karne ka, system auto ageing banata hai, exception reports nikalta hai.
Access rights limited hone chahiye (ID control).
Processing of data ⚙️
System parameters sahi set hain ya nahi?
Test of one: ek sample transaction check karo → agar system correct process kar raha hai ➝ assumption ke baki bhi sahi honge.
Transmission of data 📩
Expert ko jo data management se mila hai, wohi auditor ke paas bhi ho.
Auditor ko verify karna hai: email trail / excel file ka line-by-line match.
Taake ensure ho ke expert aur auditor dono same dataset pe kaam kar rahe hain.
🔹 Transmission aur Source Verification
Auditor ko sirf management ka diya hua confirmation accept nahi karna chahiye 🚫.
Zaroori hai ke email trail ya original source documentation verify karein 📩.
Example: Management bole “data expert ko forward kar diya” → auditor ko confirm karna hoga ke jo data expert ko gaya wohi auditor ke paas hai.
Agar data admin ke bande se aa raha ho → suspicion increase hota hai 🔍.
🔹 Integrity of Data
Jab source system se verify karte ho → usse data integrity ka bhi andaza lagta hai.
Agar billing system se directly aya hai aur system generated hai → chances of tampering kam hote hain 🔐.
Limited exercise ka matlab hai system pe control strong hai (ek dafa invoice ban gaya → usko change nahi kar sakte).
🔹 Completeness Check
Jab expert ko data transmit hota hai → auditor ko check karna hai ke data complete hai ya nahi.
Simple test: aging total vs FS total match hona chahiye ✅.
Agar mismatch hai → data incomplete ya inaccurate ho sakta hai.
🔹 Internal Consistency
Ek hi report ke andar parameters consistent hone chahiye.
Example: 5 Jan ki invoice “30 days” me show ho rahi hai, aur 15 Jan wali “not yet due” me → ye inconsistency hai ⚠️.
Internal consistency ka test se auditor ko pata lagta hai ke report sahi ban rahi hai ya manipulation hui hai.
🔹 Expert’s Methodology Review
Auditor ko expert ki assumptions aur methods challenge karne hain.
Example: Expert ne ek ratio banaya (closing receivable ÷ opening) → auditor ko check karna hoga ke yeh valid method hai ya flawed.
Correct approach: Monthly trend analysis (customer behaviour, payment patterns) 📊.
🔹 Recoveries aur Collateral Consideration
Agar loan secured hai (collateral) → ECL alag hoga vs unsecured.
Auditor ko check karna hai ke expert ne collateral ka effect consider kiya ya nahi 🏦.
Recoveries (write-off ke baad jo paisay wapas aaye) ko bhi model me include karna hota hai.
🔹 Credit Loss Example (ECL)
Expected loss = Probability of default × Loss given default.
Agar historically 20% recoveries hui hain → loss ko gross pe calculate karke phir net karna hoga.
Auditor ensure kare ke expert ki model assumptions realistic aur evidence-based hain ✅.
🔹 Inquiry with Expert
Auditor ko expert se directly poochna chahiye ke unhone apni methodology kaise develop ki.
Example: Pension valuation expert ne assume kiya “10 years tenure for executives” → auditor ko poochna hoga: ye assumption data se justify hoti hai ya nahi?
🔹 Provision & Calculation Concepts
Teacher ne example di gratuity provision ki → ek banda salary ko 10 years aage le gaya for calculation.
Industry practice vs company practice check karni hoti hai.
Agar evidence na lo to calculation galat ho sakti hai.
Auditor ko inflation, present value, future increments sab consider karna hota hai.
Auditor ko expert ki report ki validity & exemption evidence verify karni hoti hai.
🔹 Auditor’s Role with Expert
Auditor challenge kar sakta hai expert ko → expert ne kaam sahi kiya ya nahi.
Agar expert kaam weak hai → auditor reject kar sakta hai.
Auditor ki zimmedari hai acceptability decide karna.
Expert ki working auditor ko reperform / recalculate bhi karni parti hai (matching concept).
🔹 Insurance Example (Actuarial Valuation)
Auditor insurance ke liye IBNR provision dekhte hain (incurred but not reported claims).
Iske liye auditor ko accident rate, historical claim data, actuarial assumptions verify karni hoti hain.
Agar expert justify na kare to auditor usko kaam dobara karne ko bolta hai.
🔹 Reporting & Disclosure
Audit Report me Expert ka naam normally mention nahi hota.
Kyun? → Responsibility auditor ki hi hoti hai, shift nahi hoti.
Agar naam mention karein bhi → explicitly likhna hoga “Auditor’s responsibility reduce nahi hoti”.
Legal requirements agar force karein to hi expert ka naam report me aa sakta hai.

📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔹 Basic Concept
Auditor ko pehle assess karna hota hai ke kya expert ki zaroorat hai ya nahi.
Agar matter auditing & accounting ke scope me aata hai → auditor khud expert hai (no external expert needed).
Agar matter technical field (e.g. valuation, actuarial, engineering, legal) se related hai → external expert ki requirement ho sakti hai.
🔹 Example: Provision for Doubtful Debts
Ye accounting matter hai → auditor khud expert hai is area me.
No external expert required.
Lekin agar matter building valuation, actuarial valuation, medical reports, geological surveys ho → phir auditor expert nahi hai → external expert ki zaroorat hogi.
🔹 When to Involve an Expert ✅
Jab matter complex aur specialized ho jo auditor ki knowledge se bahar ho.
Jab material impact FS pe ho sakta ho.
Jab auditor ko lagta ho ke management expert ka kaam evaluate karna hai.
🔹 Key Justification Points
Auditor is responsible → expert ka kaam sirf input hai, responsibility shift nahi hoti.
Independence & Competence of Expert check karna hoga.
Expert ki methods, assumptions, aur data verify karna auditor ka role hai.
Auditor expert ke kaam ko challenge, review aur recalculate bhi kar sakta hai.
🔹 Reporting Guidance
Normally audit report me expert ka naam mention nahi hota.
Agar mention karna ho (legal requirement ya specific disclosure) → clearly likhna hoga “Auditor’s responsibility reduce nahi hoti”.
🔁 Materiality vs. Acceptance — Don’t Mix Them
Materiality sirf work focus set karta hai; acceptance (true/false) alag judgement hai.
Kisi item ki amount overall materiality se upar ho → work increase karo, but automatically “accept” nahi hota.
Example: Sales invoice materiality se upar hai → verify basis, not just assume 80m “sahi” hai; may be 82m/78m.
👨‍💼 Kab Expert Involve Karein?
Expert tab consider hota hai jab:
Matter auditing/accounting ke field se bahar ho (e.g., complex valuation, IBNeR/actuarial models).
Matter financial statements ke liye material ho.
Auditor khud sufficient appropriate evidence generate nahi kar sakta without specialist techniques.
💡 Agar auditor khud knowledge build karke (training, standard reading, direct testing) sufficient evidence obtain kar sakta/ sakti hai → expert optional (document the rationale).
✅ Before You Use an Expert (Planning Checklist)
Define objective & scope (exact deliverable, effective date, FS line item).
Data sources: what dataset the expert will use (client systems, cut‑off dates).
Assumptions & methods to be applied (e.g., development triangles, discounting approach).
Materiality & performance materiality link to the expert’s procedures.
Timing & dependencies (so deadlines/AGM don’t slip).
🔍 Reviewing, Not Just Accepting (Evaluating Expert’s Work)
Auditor must evaluate adequacy of the expert’s work:
Data: relevant, complete, accurate? (origin, transmission, reconciliation to FS totals).
Assumptions: reasonable, consistent with history & current conditions?
Methods/Models: appropriate for purpose (e.g., monthly default/ recovery patterns, not a crude year-end ratio).
Results: do outputs make sense vs. trends, sensitivities, ranges?
Independence/Objectivity: any new facts impairing it? get written confirmation.
If inadequate → ask for additional work / revise methods; document challenges & responses.
📝 Referencing the Expert in the Auditor’s Report
Kab refer karein?
Only when law/regulation requires, or when a modified opinion ko samjhanay ke liye reference necessary ho (e.g., valuation drives a qualification/emphasis).
Before referencing, ensure:
Permission: Expert ki written consent to be named/ referred.
Wording: Clear that auditor’s responsibility remains intact; reference does not reduce auditor’s responsibility.
Describe: nature of the expert’s work at a high level (no raw conclusions that look like outsourcing the opinion).
Report Language Reminders
“Reference to the expert does not diminish the auditor’s responsibility.”
If law mandates naming, still add a line that overall responsibility auditor ki hai.
