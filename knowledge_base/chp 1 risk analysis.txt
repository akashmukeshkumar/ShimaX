📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
📌 Core Concept: Audit Hota Kyun Hai?
Audit ka purpose hai: “To enhance the confidence of users”
Users (jaise banks, investors) ko financial statements pe trust tab hoga jab unka audit hoga.
🧠 Example-Based Explanation
Ek company bank se loan lene jaati hai.
Bank puchega: “Tere paas kya assets hain jo guarantee ke tor pe rakh sakein?”
Company dikhati hai:
PPE
Inventory
Trade Receivables
Lekin bank ka kehna: “Mujhe kaise yakin ho yeh assets asli hain?”
Is point par bank demand karta hai: Audit karwao.
✅ Independent Auditor
Auditor company ka banda nahi hona chahiye.
Independence essential hai taake unbiased audit ho.
Auditor verify karega:
Kya inventory waqai exist karti hai?
Kya value theek hai?
🗂️ Key Audit Terms
User: Jo financial statements ka use kare (e.g., bank)
Management: Jo financial statements prepare karti hai
Assertions by management:
Inventory exist karti hai
Ownership bhi unka hai
Value bhi theek hai
📦 Verification Techniques
Example: PTCL ke submarine cables audit karne hain.
Kya team physical cable check karegi? Nahi — they will use system reports (IDN) to verify.
Dusri technique: Insurance contract
Agar inventory insured hai, to it proves it exists and has value.
🧾 Audit Process Overview
Management ne financial statements banaye
Users ko trust nahi (confidence chahiye)
Independent auditor appoint hota hai
Auditor:
Evidence collect karta hai
Assertions verify karta hai
Audit opinion/assurance deta hai
📚 Important Terms Recap
Why audit is conducted? → To enhance user confidence
Who appoints auditor? → Company (but auditor must be independent)
Audit Evidence → Proof jo auditor collect karta hai
Audit Procedures → Wo kaam jo auditor karta hai evidence collect karne ke liye (inspect, confirm, observe, etc.)
💬 Real-World Adaptation
PTCL example: Submarine cable audit
Physical jaake count nahi karte
Use system reports (live connection data)
Insurance example:
Insurance document proves asset existence
🚩 Risk of Misstatement
Example: Management claim karti hai ke inventory 100 ki hai
Auditor check karta hai: 50 ki hi exist karti hai
Iss mismatch pe auditor bolega: “Misstatement hai, correct karo”
🧾 Audit Assertion Framework
Jab bhi financials banaye jayein:
Existence
Rights & Obligations
Valuation
Completeness
Presentation & Disclosure
🧠 Assertions: Existence vs. Rights
Sir ne clear kiya ke existence aur rights do alag cheezein hain.
Example: Kisi aur ki inventory agar aapke godown me rakhi ho — wo exist to karti hai, lekin aapki nahi hai (no rights).
Audit me sirf exist karna kaafi nahi, auditor ko yeh bhi verify karna hota hai ke:
Inventory aapki hi hai (ownership proofs like invoice/title deeds)
Value theek hai (impairment test if needed)
📦 Real Audit Example – Investment Property
Ek client ki books me investment property record thi.
Physical check se pata chala:
Property kisi aur ke control me thi.
Yeh prove karta hai ke management ka ownership ka da’wa false tha.
Audit action:
Disclose kiya gaya ke asset pe dispute hai.
Bank ko warn kiya gaya ke is asset ko collateral na maanay.
💰 Value & Impairment Testing
Management ka claim hota hai ke asset ki value theek hai.
Auditor test karta hai:
Kya asset sell ho sakta hai?
Kya uska fair value kam ho chuka hai?
Indicators of impairment — e.g., zero income from asset, unsellable items, or damaged goods.
🧪 Techniques for Verification
Rights check:
Invoices
Title deeds
Insurance documents
Existence check:
Physical inspection
IDN reports (for telecom)
Value check:
Post year-end sales test (e.g., sold item was recorded at higher value)
Excel analysis (compare cost vs. selling price)
Aging reports (old inventory = likely impaired)
📉 Risk of Overstatement
Management often overstates value to present better financials.
Auditor’s role is to identify these misstatements using evidence.
📂 Key Audit Assertions (Applied)
Existence: Asset physically present?
Rights & Obligations: Ownership ka proof?
Valuation: Value realistic hai ya inflated?
🧾 Practical Audit Tools
Excel-Based Testing:
Compare item cost vs. sale price
Use formulas to flag mismatches
Aging Report:
Identify stagnant/old inventory
Indicates impairment risk
External Events:
News reports (e.g., floods damaging stock)
Help identify risk indicators
🔍 Sampling Risk
Example: 6 inventory items check kiye:
4 wrong the, 2 correct
Lekin ho sakta hai total 100 items me se sirf 4 galat hon — to sample misleading ho sakta hai.
Auditor ko sampling technique aur risk targeting dono samajhne chahiye.
📌 Decision-Making Based on Financial Statements
Users (e.g. banks, regulators) decisions financial statements ki base par lete hain.
Auditor ka kaam hota hai yeh ensure karna ke:
Financials reliable hain ✅
Assertions (existence, rights, valuation, completeness) sahi hain 🔍
💬 Receivables – Audit Assertions
Assertion: Trade receivables exist karte hain + hamare hi hain (ownership + existence)
Ownership verify karne ke liye:
Sales agreement / invoice
Agar property hai to title deeds
Existence verify karne ke liye:
Confirmation letter customer ko bhejna
Response se prove hota hai ke receivable real hai
📩 Audit Confirmation Process
Auditor client ke naam se confirmation letter send karta hai:
Letterhead par likha hota hai: “Kitne paisay dene hain?”
Customer likh kar confirm karta hai: “Itni amount due hai.”
Yeh response = audit evidence
🧪 Sampling for Confirmations
Sare receivables check nahi karte (sampling karte hain)
Sampling ka base:
Aging report: Kaunse receivables kitne purane hain?
Old & doubtful balances = higher risk = sampling me zarur aayenge
🧠 New Concept: Reasonable Assurance
Auditor 100% check nahi karta — provide karta hai:
Reasonable assurance, not absolute
Based on sampling + experience + technical judgment
⚠️ Risk Indicators in Receivables
Receivable bohot purana ho (e.g., 12+ months) → doubtful
Balance recorded hai, lekin delivery ka gate pass nahi bana — may be fake sale
Year-end pe large volume entries bina delivery ke — fraud risk 🚨
🧾 Fraud Detection via Targeting
Auditor 31st Dec ke aas paas ki entries ko alag extract karta hai:
Compare karta hai delivery docs, gate pass etc.
Jahan delivery ka proof nahi hota, wahan fraud suspicion hota hai
📊 Analytical Procedures (Excel Based)
Data export karke:
Sale price vs. cost compare
Unusual margins identify
Unrealistic sales spike detect (e.g., 500 fake entries at year-end)
Excel formulas se instantly flag hoti hain risky entries
🔁 Sales Cut-Off Test
Verify karta hai:
Kya sale correct period me record hui?
Kya delivery hua bhi ya sirf entry ki gayi?
📂 Other Key Terms
Aging Report: Kitni purani receivables hain
Cut-off testing: Transactions proper period me hain ya nahi
Gate pass: Proof ke goods physically dispatch huay
🎯 Focus for Auditor
Blind checking nahi karni — pehle risk areas identify karo
Auditor ka kaam:
High-risk areas target karna
Assertions verify karna based on evidence

🆕 New User: Shareholder (Company Owner)
Aaj ka naya user: Shareholder (Company ka real owner).
Shareholders khud day-to-day business nahi chala rahe:
Finance handle karta hai CFO
Records maintain karte hain staff
Operations manage karti hai management
Shareholders rely karte hain financial statements par to assess:
Kya unka paisa safe hai?
Kya business genuine performance show kar raha hai?
🧠 Real-Life Link: Shareholder’s Risk
Management agar apni performance artificially inflate kare:
Jaise fake revenue book kar lena
Receivables dikhana jo kabhi recover nahi hone
Ye sab kar ke bonus le lena
Iss fraud ka direct nuksaan shareholder ko hota hai, kyunke:
Financials misstate ho jaate hain
Profit galat dikhaya jaata hai
Company ka actual position distort ho jaata hai
🏛️ Shareholder Controls: Governance Structure
Shareholders appoint karte hain Board of Directors (BOD)
BOD appoint karta hai Management
BOD ka kaam hai:
Governance: ensure company ethical, efficient aur compliant hai
Accountability: management se jawab lena performance par
Decision rights: budget approve karna, reporting monitor karna
🔍 Director’s Role Explained
Directors:
Quarterly reports check karte hain
Budget targets vs. actual review karte hain
Management se reasoning mangte hain: “Ye expense kyu hua?”
Directors hona chahiye:
Knowledgeable in the business field
Independent in thinking
🧾 Example – Management Manipulation
Bonus ke liye management ne:
Budget me fake targets set kiye
Revenue artificially book kiya
Bina cash aane ke receivables bana diye
Auditor ne detect kiya:
Bank statement me cash nahi tha
Entries unsupported thi
Profit actually loss tha
📉 Resulting Impact on Shareholders
Artificial profit show karna = misleading the shareholder
Jab paisa recover nahi hota, to:
Wo receivable ban jaata hai bad debt
Profit P&L me convert ho jaata hai loss
🧾 Financial Statements & Signatures
Financial statements (FS) consist of:
Balance Sheet
Profit & Loss
Cash Flow
Changes in Equity
Notes to Accounts
Auditor apni audit report tab deta hai jab:
Directors aur CFO ne FS par sign kar diye hoon ✅
Sign karna = Management ka claim ke data sahi hai
Auditor report me likhta hai:
“In our opinion, FS are free from material misstatement”
📌 Responsibility Flow
Management banati hai FS → Directors approve karte hain → Auditor verify karta hai
Directors aur CFO sign kar ke confirm karte hain:
Existence, Rights, Valuation, Completeness, Presentation sahi hai
Auditor unhi signed FS ko audit karta hai
🕵️ Auditor as Independent Party
Auditor ka role hai independent evaluation karna:
Fake revenue detect karna
Receivables verify karna
Assets ki value assess karna
Auditor agar koi misstatement detect kare:
Management ko rectification ke liye kehta hai
Agar refuse kare, to BOD ko escalate karta hai
Phir bhi na ho to, report me qualify karta hai (i.e. not free from material misstatement)
🔄 Internal Governance vs. External Audit
BOD governance karta hai = internal system
Auditor verify karta hai = external control
Directors ko governance ka charge hota hai, but audit independent person karta hai
📑 BOD Approval is Mandatory
Bina BOD approval ke auditor report nahi de sakta
FS per CEO & CFO ke signatures mandatory hain
Audit report bina management ke claim ke invalid hogi
🧠 Concept: Who Appoints the Auditor?
Auditor appoint karta hai user — jiska stake hota hai
Usually: Shareholders
Kabhi kabhi: Banks (loan requirement), Govt. authorities
Example: In public listed companies, auditor is appointed in AGM by shareholders
🏛️ Example: Government Oversight
Govt. grants deti hai (e.g., telecom expansion in remote areas)
Govt. ko ensure karna hota hai:
Paisa sahi jagah use hua
Funds ka misuse na ho
Auditor confirm karta hai ke:
Network towers lage
Equipment installed
Grant ka accounting transparent hai
🧾 Chain of Evidence and Approval
Management prepares FS
CEO/CFO sign as ownership of data
Auditor audits signed FS
If error found → management ko correct karne ko kehta hai
Refusal → escalate to Board of Directors
Still unresolved → reflect in audit report
🧠 Concept: Prospective Shareholders
Not just existing owners — prospective investors are also users
They rely on audited FS to:
Make buying decisions
Assess profitability and financial position
Agar management FS me fake profit dikha kar exit le le — naya investor mislead hota hai
📌 Tax Authority as a User
Jab aap tax return file karte ho:
Tax authority expects reliable, audited figures
Accounting profit adjusted to tax profit
Agar audit nahi hua:
Authority doubt karegi ke figures genuine hain ya nahi
📅 Government Grant Audit Example
Government ne telecom companies (e.g. PTCL, Telenor) ko grant di:
Northern areas me signal lane ke liye
PTCL ne kaha: “Hum apne paison se kyun lagayen?”
Govt ne kaha: “Hum paisay dete hain, aap laga do network”
Yeh paisay Govt ko justify karne hote hain:
Kitna paisa diya
Kitna utilize hua
Kya actual asset bana
Audit report tayyar hoti hai jisme yeh sab prove hota hai
🔍 Understanding the Users of Financial Statements
Users: Jis kisi ka business ke sath interaction hai (Stakeholder)
Ho sakta hai:
Existing shareholder
Prospective shareholder
Bank, Tax authority, etc.
Auditor un sab ke liye credibility enhance karta hai
📆 Governance Structure Recap
CFO signs financials kyunke woh saal bhar ka record rakhta hai
Directors supervise operations aur governance
Director has rights to:
Approve loans
Approve acquisitions
Control large expenditures
👉 Independent Auditor ka Role
Internal log (CFO, Director) apni financials finalize karte hain
Independent auditor verify karta hai:
Kya financials sahi hain?
Kya misstatements to nahi?
Auditor sign karta hai sirf tab jab:
Directors ne apne sign de diye hon
Financials ko own kiya gaya ho
🏢 Company Articles & Resolutions
Har company ke apne Articles of Association hote hain:
Jisme define hota hai: kis cheez ki authority kiske paas hai
Kuch transactions (e.g. capital change) ke liye special resolution chahiye hota hai
Directors se upar shareholder approval
🔒 Quality of Audit Oversight
Audit oversight board bhi check karta hai ke:
Audit proper tarah perform hua ya nahi
Quality maintained hai ya nahi
🧰 Internal vs Independent Audit
Internal audit = Day-to-day checking
Example: Portal access vs. receipts match karna
Independent audit = By external party, objective review
Isliye shareholder ko chahiye ke external audit karaaye

GSK me audit team gai thi: Sales audit karne
Procedure:
Sales ledger liya
Invoices verify kiye

🤔 Problem: Lack of Understanding Operations
Auditor ne yeh nahi samjha:

🏛️ Audit Planning Lessons
Audit hamesha shuru hota hai: Understand the Entity (UoE)
Pehle samjho:
Company ka business model kya hai?
Operational restrictions kya hain?
External factors (e.g. import bans, licenses, etc.)
🔢 How to Understand the Entity (UoE)
Sources:
Financial Statements (Revenue streams)
Enquiry with CFO / CEO / Head of Ops
Inspection of business documents
Articles of Association
In sab se auditor samajhta hai:
Risk kahan kahan ho sakta hai
Kya kya misstatements possible hain
⚠️ Audit Strategy & Targeting
Agar auditor ko pata chal jaye:
3 mahine sales honi hi nahi chahiye thi
To audit ka focus wahi target karega
Isse fraud detect hota hai:
Overstatement of sales
Fake revenue booking
🔍 Understanding Industry Environment for Audit
Auditor ko industry-specific environmental knowledge hona chahiye.
Example: COVID-19 situation – auditor ko pta hona chahiye tha ke pharmaceutical ya kisi aur industry par kya asar para.
Research, news, aur regulatory updates ka use karke apne suspicion areas identify karo.
🧠 Identifying Risks Before Audit
Audit start karne se pehle understanding of entity and environment essential hai.
Example: If 3 months import ban thi, toh us period ki sales suspicious hongi.
Agar environment samjha hota toh risk pehle identify ho jata.
🏭 Highly Regulated Industries & Compliance
Pharmaceutical industry ka audit ho toh regulations samajhna critical hai.
DRAP (Drug Regulatory Authority) ke temperature, storage, licensing rules hotay hain.
Agar non-compliance hui hai, toh auditor should expect penalties.
Risk: Company ne penalty record nahi ki ho.
📧 Verification Techniques
Auditor ko management se demand karna chahiye:
Legal department communication with regulators (e.g., DRAP)
Emails, logs, or system records of such communication
Agar kuch delete hua hai, toh system logs verify kiye jaa sakte hain.
📄 Documenting Auditor’s Work
Agar auditor ne sab kuch verify kiya, aur phir bhi fraud ya misstatement reh gaya, toh documentation important hai to prove:
Maximum procedures perform kiye gaye
Auditor ne negligence nahi ki
🧾 Example: Concealed Penalties or Expired Inventory
Agar DRAP ne notice bheja ke koi chemical ya medicine defective hai, lekin management ne auditor ko nahi bataya:
Risk of material misstatement high ho jata hai
Auditor should’ve checked regulator notices
Expired inventory sale ka case:
Expiry date system me fixed hoti hai
Destroy karne ka record, DRAP witness certificate, etc., cross-checkable

🧠 Independence in Audit
Example: Auditor ki wife agar CFO hai, auditor independent nahi rahega
Auditor must be free from influence or threat
Independence ensures:
Neutral opinion
Genuine verification
🔎 Search Before Audit Begins
Audit randomly start nahi hota
Entity aur Environment ko pehle samajhna padta hai
Example:
Ayesha ne invoice verify ki — lekin operational understanding nahi thi
Some invoices were fake — audit fail ho gaya
📦 Operational Understanding Required
Entity kya karti hai?
Environment ka kya impact hai?
Example:
Import ban = 3 mahine sale honi nahi chahiye
Agar fir bhi sales record hui = overstatement risk
📄 Audit Evidence ke Types
Self-generated evidence = weak (e.g. company ke apne invoices)
Reliable evidence:
Third-party confirmations
Direct observations
Legal communication logs
🧾 Case: Penalties in Highly Regulated Industry
Pharmaceutical = highly regulated
DRAP compliance required (e.g. expiry control, temperature, etc.)
Risk: Penalties record nahi ki gayi
Auditor’s job:
Verify: Kya penalties sahi se record hui hain?
Check: Kya koi conceal toh nahi ki gayi?
📧 Verification Procedures
Auditor legal dept. se email trail le
System logs inspect kare — agar kuch delete hua ho
Confirmation DRAP ko bhej sakte hain:
“Iss year kitne notices diye the?”
⚖️ Acknowledged vs. Unacknowledged Penalties
Kabhi-kabhi management kehti hai: “Hum guilty nahi hain”
Toh penalty ko acknowledge nahi karti
In that case, auditor should ensure:
Proper disclosure in notes to accounts
Not necessarily record as liability
🧠 Legal Standard
Jab matter court me pending ho:
Penalty record nahi hoti
Sirf disclosure hoti hai
Auditor ko yeh sab points document karne chahiye
⚖️ Contingent Liabilities & Penalties
Agar regulator (e.g., DRAP) ne penalty ka notice diya hai:
Auditor ko assess karna hota hai: Kya yeh record karni chahiye thi ya disclose?
Lawyer ka opinion guide karta hai:
🔺 50%+ chance of losing → Record as expense
🔻 Less than 50% chance → Only disclose in notes
❗ Jab auditor FS dekh raha hota hai:
Wo yeh check karta hai: Kahi koi penalty misstate toh nahi ki gayi?
Disclosure vs. recognition ka decision correct tha ya nahi?
🧾 Penalties: Auditor’s Double Concern
Jab company highly regulated industry me operate karti hai (e.g. Pharma, Telecom):
Auditor ke 2 major risks hote hain:
Understatement of Penalties
Contingent Liabilities properly disclose ki ya nahi
📍 Risk ka analysis tab possible hota hai jab:
Auditor entity aur uske environment ko acche se samjhe
Regulator (e.g. DRAP) se confirmation bhi bheje: “Is saal me aap ne company ko kitne penalty notices bheje?”
📬 Confirmation Example
Regulator ne 10 penalty notices bheje
Company ne sirf 3 record kiye
7 ka status unclear hai
➡️ Auditor ne kya karna hai:
Legal opinion lena hoga for those 7 cases
Lawyer se poochna hoga: *“Kya lagta hai inme haarne ke chances hain?”
Agar 50%+ chance hai → Record as expense
Agar <50% chance hai → Disclose as contingent liability
🔍 Proper Legal Assessment Required
Management sirf keh de: “Hum jeet jaayenge” → Not enough
Auditor must:
Consult lawyer dealing with the case
Review legal files and case history
➡️ Agar auditor yeh kaam nahi karta, to:
Targeting of risk flawed
FS may be materially misstated
🏢 Telecom Sector Audit Example (PP&E Focus)
Auditor (nicknamed Chumpo) gaya PP&E audit karne telecom client ka
Without understanding entity or sector

❌ Major Mistake:

🧠 Learning: Understanding Comes First
Entity ko samajhne ke liye auditor should:
Business model samjhe
Regulatory changes dekhe
Industry professionals (e.g. engineers in telecom) se enquiry kare
Inquiry technique:
Na poore questions reveal karo
Seedha point pe na jao — indirect probing karo
e.g. “5G aa raha hai? Kya isme invest kar rahe hain?” → Info mil jaayegi
➡️ Once understood:
Auditor can decide: “Kya existing assets ki life overstate toh nahi hui?”
🔎 Resulting Audit Risk
Without proper understanding:
Depreciation expense understated
Asset value overstated
➡️ Material misstatement in FS
⚠️ Understanding the Impact of Environment on Asset Valuation
Auditor jab depreciation ya asset life assess karta hai:
Unhe environment ka analysis bhi zaruri hota hai
Example: 5G launch hone wala hai → existing 4G telecom towers obsolete ho jayenge
Iska matlab hai: Asset life galat ho sakti hai agar auditor ne environment ko consider nahi kiya
📉 Wrong Asset Life = Wrong Depreciation
Agar 4G tower ki life ab sirf 2 saal reh gayi hai:
Lekin company usay 10 saal ki life samajh ke depreciate kar rahi hai
To financial statements overstated assets dikha rahe hain
Auditor ko life revise karne ko kehna chahiye
🔍 Inquiry with Engineering Team
Auditor ne engineering team se inquiry ki:
“5G kab tak activate ho raha hai?”
Jawab: 2 saal ke andar 5G rollout
Auditor ne conclude kiya:
4G towers ki life 2 saal bachi hai
Ab life ko revise karo → depreciation increase hoga
📘 IAS 36: Impairment Standard
IAS 36 kehta hai:
Jab bhi asset ki life impact ho, ya uska future cash flow kam hone wala ho
Recoverable amount calculate karo
Agar carrying amount > recoverable → impairment loss recognize karo
🧮 Practical Step by Auditor
Engineering team se current updates lein
5G rollout ka timeline samjhein
Existing assets ki remaining useful life estimate karein
CFO ko kehna: “Life revise karo ya impairment book karo”
🧾 Conflict with Management
CFO ne pehle mana kiya:
“Depreciation meri marzi se decide hoti hai”
Auditor ne kaha: “Engineering ne bataya hai 2 saal life bachi hai”
Auditor ne kaha:
“Ya to revise karo, ya mai BOD se baat karta hu”
Agar phir bhi na mana → qualified or adverse opinion
🧠 Targeted Audit Thinking
Normal auditor:
Depreciation calculate karke satisfy ho jaata
Smart auditor:
Sochta hai: “Is environment me asset ki life realistic hai ya nahi?”
5G jaise changes ko link karta hai existing assets se
📌 Responsibility Chain
Engineering team: Input on technical obsolescence
CFO: Financial reporting responsibility

🔢 Concept: Environmental Risk & Audit Targeting
Auditor ko environment samajhna bohat zaroori hai — jaise:
“Floods” ki wajah se loan recoverability affect ho sakti hai
“5G rollout” ka asar existing 4G infrastructure par padega
Audit plan banate waqt auditor ko:
Affected areas identify karne hain (e.g., flood zones)
Loan portfolio ko location-wise assess karna hai

“Tum prove karo ke is area ke loans recover ho sakte hain”

Floods → Loss of income → Loan default → Bad debt risk

Situation: Company operates in flood-hit areas
Risk: Loans may not be recoverable
Financial Statement impact:
✅ Overstatement of Receivables

Examiner question me situation milegi:
e.g., “Company lends in flood areas”

“Since the company operates in flood-affected regions, there is risk of incorrect recoverability assumptions, leading to overstatement of receivables.”

🏢 Entity Understanding
Kisi bhi institution ka audit tab tak nahi ho sakta jab tak auditor us entity aur uske environment ko na samjhe.
Jab organization aur uske environment ki understanding milti hai to auditor ko andaza hota hai ke misstatement kaha se ho sakti hai.
Example: Agar kisi area me flood aya aur company ka warehouse wahan maujood hai to inventory ka NRV loss ho sakta hai.
Agar kisi debtor ka bank bankrupt ho gaya hai to us se recovery possible nahi hogi, is liye receivables me misstatement ka risk ho ga.
💰 Export Company Example
Ek company jo export karti hai balls Pakistan se bahar bechti hai.
Pakistan me presentation currency PKR hoti hai.
Customer US/UK me baitha hota hai to invoice USD me banti hai.
Concept:
Transaction currency: USD
Presentation currency: PKR
💱 Exchange Rate Application
Jab sale USD me hoti hai to record PKR me hoti hai.
Conversion us din ke exchange rate par hoti hai jis din transaction hui.
Example: 2 January ko $1000 ki sale hui, rate 281 tha → record: 1000 × 281 = 281,000 PKR.
Agar 31 January ka rate use kiya gaya to wo galat hoga.
⚠️ Risk of Incorrect Rate
Poore saal ka same rate use karna galat hai.
Har transaction apne date ke rate par convert honi chahiye.
Average rate lena bhi galat ho sakta hai agar rates fluctuate kar rahe hon.
Risk: Using incorrect exchange rate at time of recording sale.
📊 Exchange Gain or Loss
Jab payment receive hoti hai aur rate change ho chuka hota hai to difference gain/loss hota hai.
Example: Sale record hui 281 par, payment mili 285 par → difference exchange gain hoga.
🧾 Audit Point for Export/Import Companies
Exchange rate sahi date ka use hua ya nahi.
Translation PKR me sahi hui ya nahi.
Presentation aur transaction currency same hai ya different.
Agar dono same hain to conversion ka risk nahi hota.
🌍 Multinational Case
Agar company Pakistan me operate kar rahi hai lekin shareholders US ke hain to financial statements USD me banenge.
Presentation currency user ki understanding ke hisab se hoti hai.
⚖️ Audit Risk Assertion
Export/import companies me common risk: Incorrect exchange rate usage during transaction recording.
💱 Exchange Rate Source & Automation
State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) rates ko use karna best practice hai — system se daily rate fetch kar ke automatic conversion karwana.
Auditor verify kare ke company ne reliable source (e.g., SBP) se rate liya aur system properly daily rates apply karta hai.
Control check: sample invoices ki conversion rates system output se reconcile karo.
🧾 Transaction Date Rate vs Average Rate
Principle: Sales/receivables ko usi din ke exchange rate par record karo jis din transaction hui.
Average rate use sirf kuch specific items (e.g., interest income, small frequent receipts) ke liye apply ho sakta hai, lekin documentation required hai.
Audit check: agar company average rate use karti hai to policy aur calculation method verify karo — materiality aur volatility ke hisab se justify hona chahiye.
⚖️ FX Gain / Loss Treatment
Jab payment receive ho to actual receipt rate aur recorded transaction rate mein difference hota hai — is difference ko FX gain/loss me record karo.
Auditor ensure kare ke exchange differences timely aur correctly classify hue hon (revenue vs finance income/loss according to accounting policy).
🛡️ Hedging & Rate Locking (Practical points)
Hedging ya rate-locking ke arrangements jab present hon to:
Confirm hedging contract exists (bank confirmations, agreement).
Evaluate whether hedge accounting apply karta hai aur disclosures complete hain.
Agar company ne forward contract ke through rate lock kiya to auditor ko bank documentation verify karni chahiye.
Example: Agar company ne forward 281 PKR/USD lock kiya aur market 289 ya 275 ho gaya — hedging se jo result aaya wo hedge contract ke hisab se treat hoga.
💼 Cash-in-hand vs Bank FC Balances
Cash (physical USD) aur bank foreign-currency accounts ko alag consider karo.
Physical cash ka valuation aur custody controls check karo (who holds, when recorded).
Foreign currency bank accounts ko reporting currency (PKR) me translate karna hota hai — translation date aur rate clear honi chahiye.
Auditor ko ensure karna hai ke cash receipts aur bank receipts dono correctly record huye aur duplicate/missed recording na ho.
🧾 Foreign Currency Accounts (FC Accounts)
Banks multiple currency accounts provide karte hain (FC account, e.g., USD account).
Company ke FC balances ka PKR equivalent balance books me sahi tariqe se translate hona chahiye.
Audit check: bank statement confirmations for FC accounts and reconciliation of translated balances.
📌 Practical Audit Procedures (Checklist)
Check source of daily rates (SBP/system) and automation logs.
Recompute conversion for sample invoices using rate on transaction date.
Verify FX gain/loss calculation on receipts and bank settlements.
Inspect hedging contracts / forward confirmations if company claims rate-lock.
Confirm presence and reconciliation of foreign-currency bank accounts.
Review policy for average rate use — test appropriateness (e.g., interest income).
Test cut-off: verify that sale recorded on transaction date and receipt on payment date, with FX difference recorded at receipt.
Evaluate disclosure adequacy: presentation vs transaction currency disclosures required.
Foreign Currency Bank Account Concept
Foreign currency account wo hota hai jahan PKR nahi, balki USD ya koi aur foreign currency maintain hoti hai.
Agar USD rate increase kare → bank balance PKR terms me increase hoga.
Agar rate decrease kare → bank balance PKR terms me decrease hoga.
Auditor check: FC account balances translation at correct rate and accurate FX revaluation.
⚠️ Risk of Wrong Translation
Incorrect rate use hone se cash/bank balances overstated ya understated ho sakte hain.
FC accounts ka PKR equivalent har reporting date par revalue hona chahiye.
Auditor focus:
Revaluation ka method correct hai ya nahi.
Revaluation automated system-based hai ya manual input.
💻 Practical Check
System automation (e.g., SBP auto-fetch) ensure kare ke rate sahi tarah se update ho rahe hain.
Sample testing perform kare to verify translation accuracy.
🧪 Research and Development Example (Pharma Case)
Sir ne pharmaceutical company ki example se R&D aur intangible asset ka concept samjhaya.
Case: COVID-19 vaccine development example — pehle formula develop hota hai (research phase), phir medicine manufacture hoti hai (development phase).
⚗️ Research vs Development
Research Phase:
Market study, symptom analysis, infection data collect karna.
Objective: understanding and exploration.
Result uncertain hota hai — koi confirm benefit nahi hota.
Cost treatment: Expense.
Development Phase:
Formula established, testing aur production start hoti hai.
Feasibility, intention, ability, aur resource availability honi chahiye.
Cost treatment: Capitalize only if all IAS 38 conditions met.
📚 IAS 38 – Development Conditions
Development cost capitalize hone ke liye ye 5 conditions fulfill honi chahiye:
Technical feasibility – project technically possible hai.
Intention to complete – management ka plan clear hai.
Ability to use/sell – asset usable or saleable hai.
Resources available – staff, funds, facilities available hain.
Probable future economic benefits – expected inflows from asset.
Agar ye conditions fully meet nahi hoti to cost expense karni hoti hai.
🧠 Audit Implication
Auditor ko verify karna chahiye ke company ne R&D cost sahi classify kiya:
Research → Expense
Development → Capitalize (agar conditions satisfied hain)
Agar koi condition missing hai to capitalization galat hoga.
Example: Agar formula develop hua lekin resources ya funding available nahi, to cost expense treat hoga.

Scenario:
Audit of DPL Pakistan Ltd. (Pharmaceutical company, highly regulated).
Main product: Drug A
Raw material imported from Switzerland
Company operates in a highly regulated environment.
Identified Risks:
Regulatory Risk – Due to heavy regulation, understatement of penalty expenses is possible.
Foreign Exchange Risk – Raw material import involves FX risk; wrong exchange rate may misstate inventory/payables.
Inventory & Payables Misstatement Risk – Inventory valuation and related payables are impacted by FX fluctuations.
Low Margin Indicator – Underperforming product and lower sales margin signal risk of:
Impairment in inventory
Possible unrecorded losses
🔍 R&D and Technological Change Risk
Company is involved in Research & Development for improving product viability.
If new product version is launched → existing inventory might become obsolete → Inventory impairment risk.
Technology upgrade or new variant implementation is a red flag for obsolescence.
🧾 Cash Flow from Operations & PPE Risk
Continuous negative cash flows from operations over multiple years indicate:
PPE deployed is not generating recoverable cash flows.
Indicator of impairment in PPE.
Indicator: If Operating Cash Flow < 0 for 2–3 years → Evaluate whether PPE is impaired.
📑 Patent Rights – Intangible Assets
Scenario: Company has purchased a Patent Right to manufacture a drug.
If market demand drops, even though patent is capitalized, its recoverable value may fall.
Patent is now impaired → Should be written down to recoverable amount.
Auditor’s Focus:
Evaluate whether patent still provides future economic benefits.
Recalculate recoverable value based on market demand and utility.
⚙️ PPE Linked to Specific Product Line
If PPE is dedicated to production of a specific drug whose demand has declined, then PPE might also be impaired.
Audit trigger:
Drop in product sales
Drop in recoverable cash flows
No alternative use for PPE
📉 Indicators of Impairment
Decrease in product demand
Decline in margins/sales
Negative cash flows from operations
Technological change/new product launch
Legal/regulatory changes affecting viability
Physical damage or obsolescence of asset
🧠 Audit Planning Tip
Always understand the business first.
FX risk? → Is there import/export?
Impairment? → Is product underperforming?
Patent or PPE asset? → Is it still useful?
Risk indicators must be traced to accounting records:
Cash flow statements
Market analysis
Regulatory filings
🧑‍⚖️ Penalty Expense Risk – Regulated Industry Context
Company operates in highly regulated environment.
Risk: Non-compliance → Penalty → Understatement of expense.
Auditor ko assess karna chahiye:
Kya koi regulation violate hua?
Kya related penalty expense timely aur accurately record hua?
Key Assertion: Completeness & Accuracy of Expense
🧪 R&D Triggered by Competition
Company ne new version introduce kiya due to competition.
Indicates:
Product obsolescence
Risk of inventory impairment (old version ki demand kam ho sakti hai)
Future profitability under threat
💻 Revenue Manipulation Pressure (Listed Entity)
Company is listed → pressure to show positive performance.
If new competition has impacted product performance:
Risk of management bias to inflate results
Risk of delayed impairment recognition
🧾 Impairment Testing for PPE/Intangible Assets
PPE or patent linked to specific product must be tested for impairment if:
Product margins declining
Cash flow from operations negative
No alternate use of asset
Important Point: Indicators of impairment should come from:
Financial trends (cash flows, margins)
External environment (competition, regulation)
Internal developments (technology upgrades, process change)
⚙️ Evaluation of Risk Indicators
Revenue decline alone is not enough — needs supporting evidence.
Impairment indicator ho to auditor ko assess karna chahiye:
Recoverable value
Useful life
Asset carrying value vs. value in use or fair value less cost to sell
📉 Impairment Testing – Core Concept Clarification
Impairment testing sirf indicator ke basis par hoti hai.
Agar revenues ek year ke liye decline hue hain, wo impairment indicator nahi banta.
Auditor ko verify karna hota hai:
Kya decline multiple years ka hai?
Kya product performance consistently underperforming hai?
Kya future cash flows fall ho rahe hain?
💡 Indicators of Impairment – Reminder
Consistent negative cash flows from operations
Continuous margin decline
Product demand fall with no recovery
No alternative use for PPE
Technological obsolescence
Note: One-year drop in revenue = NOT a valid impairment trigger unless linked with other strong indicators.
🏭 Impairment of PPE & Intangibles
PPE linked to one product → if that product is obsolete → PPE is at risk of impairment.
Intangible (e.g., Patent):
Purchased for high cost
If demand for product falls → Patent ka recoverable value bhi fall karega
Auditor’s Judgment Required:
Assess whether the recoverable value < carrying value.
If yes → Impairment loss should be recorded.
🔍 Linking Risk with Question Facts
Jab question me mention ho ke “Drug A underperform kar raha hai for many years” → tab impairment write karna valid hai.
Only 1-year decline = Write only if paired with other indicators (e.g., tech change, cash flow loss).
Golden Rule:
Auditor guesses nahi karega — risk inference sirf given facts ke basis pe karni hai.
🧾 Drafting Insights & Examiner Expectations
Drafting quality = Link risk + explain reasoning + stay within facts.
Examiner ko wo answer chahiye jisme student:
Audit risk clearly identify kare
Reasoning likhe (“why this is a risk”)
Relevant accounting treatment highlight kare (e.g., IAS 36, IAS 38)
🧠 Concept Reinforcement
Example: Drug A ke margins gir rahe hain for several years → Indicator of impairment
If question sirf one-year loss bataye → don’t assume impairment unless tech change or product discontinuation bhi mention ho
Drafting Style: Safe, evidence-based, clear.
Avoid: Over-assuming
Focus: Triggers + supporting audit treatment

🏦 PIBTL – Real-Life Case of Going Concern Risk
Company: Pakistan International Bulk Terminal (PIBTL)
Auditor ne PIBTL ka audit kiya, jiska business model: coal import for cement industry.
Post-COVID period mein major disruption hua:
Exchange rate boom (FX rate spike 🪙)

Result:
Import ban → No coal imports → No business operations
Auditor ko ye sochna pada: “Is company still a going concern?”
💳 Loan Agreement Trigger – Debt Covenant Breach
PIBTL ne loan lia tha from IFC (International Finance Corp).
Auditor ne loan verify kia:
Loan agreement dekha 📄

Interest rate verify kar ke recalculate kiya
✅ BUT — One major issue:
Debt covenant ki condition thi: Interest Cover Ratio ≥ 3x
Actual ratio: 0.5x only ❌ → Covenant breached
Risk: Bank can trigger loan default and seize assets.
📉 Business Viability Doubt – Cash Flow Pressure
Import ban ne business halt kar diya → Revenue zero
Debt servicing pressure → Default risk
Auditor’s Judgement: Yeh situation major indicator hai ke company going concern nahi rahi unless supported.
🧑‍💼 CFO’s Response & Management Plan
CFO ne kaha:
“Yeh temporary issue hai.”
“Sponsor shareholder support dene ko tayyar hai.”
Auditor ne bola: “Proof chahiye.”
Sponsor ki wealth statement mangi gayi
Confirmation letter bhi liya gaya
Result:
Wealth statement proved capability ✅
Auditor ne going concern status retain kar diya, based on sponsor backing
🚨 Key Red Flags (Going Concern Risk Indicators)
Long-term asset investment without sufficient cash inflow
Debt covenant breach (e.g. interest cover, current ratio)
Import ban or regulatory restrictions
Inability to meet liabilities without external support
Major customer loss affecting future revenues
🧠 Concept Clarification – Going Concern vs. Impairment
Impairment: Asset-level risk (recoverable value ↓)
Going Concern: Entity-level risk (company may shut down)
Dono different but sometimes interlinked
Example:
Company has PPE impaired due to low revenue = impairment risk
But if company has enough capital or funding = going concern okay
🧾 Financial Statement Impact
If company not a going concern:
Financials must be prepared on NRV basis (Net Realizable Value)
IAS framework no longer applied; use liquidation basis
🔍 Auditor’s Responsibility
Management ka kaam hai declare karna ke they are a going concern
Auditor ka kaam hai: Evaluate management’s assertion

💸 Floating Interest Rate Mechanism (Banking Insight)
Jab koi loan lia jata hai, bank kabhi bhi fixed rate nahi rakhta permanently.
Bank interest rate ko floating rakhta hai to avoid long-term loss.
Typical mechanism:
Example: Loan diya at “6-month KIBOR + 2%”
Pehla 6 months ka rate lock hota hai
Phir har 6 mahine baad new KIBOR rate + agreed spread apply hota hai
Floating rate banks ko market ke hisaab se adjust karne ka option deta hai.
🧠 Reason:
Bank khud paisa depositors se leta hai aur government rate ke mutabiq unko return deta hai.
Agar government rate (e.g., KIBOR) barh jaye, to:
Bank ko depositors ko zyada dena padega
Isliye loans ka rate bhi automatically barhta hai
🏦 Risky Borrower → Higher Spread
Jitna risky borrower hoga, utna zyada bank spread rakhega
e.g., KIBOR + 4% (instead of +2%)
Spread = Bank ka margin of safety
🧾 Loan Agreement: Fixed ya Floating?
Auditor ko assess karna hota hai:
Kya interest rate floating hai?
Kya spread reasonable hai?
Kya loan terms bank ke interest ko protect karte hain?
❗ Har financial instrument ka rate term sheet me likha hota hai. Auditor ko check karna hota hai ke ye realistic hai ya nahi.

🔍 Aging Report & Receivable Risk
Aging ka matlab kya hota hai?
Jab company kisi ko maal bechti hai udhaar par, to us customer se paisa lena hota hai.
Har invoice ki date hoti hai, aur aging report yeh dikhata hai ke:
Kis invoice ko kitna time guzra hai?
Kis customer se kitna paisa lena hai?
Kis din follow-up karna hai?
Example:
Agar 50 invoices hain, to unka breakdown chahiye hota hai:
10 din purani invoices
30 din purani
60 din purani etc.
Audit Risk:
Agar aging report nahi ban rahi → company ko pata hi nahi chalega ke kis se paisa lena hai
Recovery delay → receivables ka recoverability doubtful ban jata hai
Control Indicators:
Aging regularly update ho rahi hai?
Kya company timely customers ko follow-up karti hai?
Kya access control hai jahan customer portal band ho jata hai agar payment na ho?
🧠 Understanding of Entity & Its Environment – Internal Controls
Auditor ko yeh assess karna hota hai ke:
Company apne objectives achieve karne ke liye kya controls implement karti hai?
Kya controls effectively design aur apply ho rahe hain?
Example:
Company ne installment payment offer kiya, lekin uska follow-up mechanism nahi banaya → Control missing
Key Point:
Control hona alag baat hai, aur uska design effective hona alag.
💳 Credit Limit Control Example (Franchise & Telco Business)
Real-Life Case: Ufone Franchise Model
Ufone franchises ko prepaid card aur SIMs udhaar pe deti hai
Har franchise ke liye ek credit limit set hoti hai (e.g., Rs. 300,000)
Control Process:
Credit Department assess karta hai:
Franchise ki payment history
Uska turnover aur recovery cycle
Limit set hoti hai: e.g., Rs. 300,000
System monitor karta hai:
Agar franchise already Rs. 250,000 ka maal le chuki hai
Toh woh extra Rs. 200,000 ka maal nahi le sakti
Audit Implication:
Agar system timely update nahi hota (aging update slow hai) → Franchise limit exceed kar sakti hai → Receivable risk barh jata hai
⚙️ Control Design vs Implementation
Control ka hona ≠ Effective control
Design of control matters:
Kaun person responsible hai?
Kya unko access diya gaya hai?
Kya approval layers exist karti hain?
Example:
Agar credit approval ka control sales director ke paas hai → Conflict of interest Sales director wants to boost sales even if customer risk high hai
Audit Point:
Design deficiency agar wo banda approve kar raha hai jo beneficiary hai
Aise cases me auditor ko control design aur application dono test karne hote hain
🔐 Internal Controls – Design vs Implementation
Concept: Design aur Implementation alag cheezein hain
Control implement hai, lekin uska design effective nahi toh risk create hota hai.
Example: Camera laga hua hai (control hai) lekin wall ki taraf moora hua hai → design flawed.
➡️ Auditor ko evaluate karna hota hai:
Kya control exist karta hai?
Kya wo control effectively designed hai?
