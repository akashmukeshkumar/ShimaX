📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
👉 Start of new chapter: Review Engagement (ISE 2400)
Engagements ke 2 types hotay hain:
Audit Engagement → Reasonable Assurance (High Level)
Review Engagement → Limited Assurance (Lower Level)
🔹 Audit Engagement (Reasonable Assurance)
High level assurance milta hai ✅
Extensive audit evidence gather karna parta hai:
Documents inspection
Controls testing
Substantive procedures (sampling, confirmations, stock count, invoices check, etc.)
Even going concern factors evaluate kiye jate hain.
At the end → Positive Opinion diya jata hai (FS prepared in accordance with framework).
Example: “Opinion on FS” clearly state hota hai.
💡 High level ≠ Absolute assurance (100% nahi hota, reasonable hota hai).
🔹 Review Engagement (Limited Assurance)
Lower level assurance → Limited work kiya jata hai.
Procedures:
Inquiry (management se poochna)
Analytical procedures
Evidence gathering limited hota hai → sampling, detailed verification nahi hoti.
Conclusion format: “Nothing has come to our attention that causes us to believe FS not prepared in accordance…”
Yani agar koi unusual cheez samney aati hai tabhi further investigate karte hain.
⚖️ Audit vs Review Engagement (Comparison)
Audit = Extensive testing, sampling, confirmations, stock count, supporting docs → Reasonable assurance.
Review = Only inquiries & analytical procedures → Limited assurance.
Audit opinion = Positive opinion.
Review conclusion = Negative assurance style (Nothing has come to our attention…)
📝 Example (Sir’s analogy)
Review = “Limited knowledge” → Jitna dekha, utna bataya (Mustafa ke rishtay ka example 😂)
Audit = “Personal knowledge & verification” → Har cheez verify ki (Reasonable assurance).
🔹 Analytical Procedures in Review
Trend analysis, ratio analysis, monthly comparisons.
Example: Year-end sales ↑ suddenly → Inquiry from management.
Mgmt reply: “Marketing scheme thi, discount offer kiya tha.”
Auditor: Accept if reasonable, no further deep check (unlike audit).
Related party transactions & unusual transactions bhi inquire ki jati hain.
⚖️ Audit Engagement vs Review Engagement
Audit = Reasonable Assurance (High Level)
Review = Limited Assurance
Only Inquiries + Analytical Procedures
No confirmations, no GDN checking, no deep vouching 🚫
👀 Example:
Agar client bole end me sales zyada hain → hum check karte hain via analytical procedures (discount schemes, last month sales trends etc.)
Audit hota to invoices, GDN, confirmations sab check hota.
Review me bas inquiry aur analysis. ✅
✍️ Engagement Letter (Review Engagement)
Objective → To obtain limited assurance that FS are free from MM.
Scope → Only inquiries + analytical procedures.
Auditor yeh nahi likhega ke confirmations bhejunga, GDN verify karunga, etc. ❌
Review ka kaam → Bas review the FS, kuch ajeeb aya → report karo.
🎯 Example: Government Grant Case
Company ko Govt Grant related to assets mili thi.
Engagement = Review Engagement (Limited Assurance)
🛠️ Work performed:
FS data ko analytically analyze kiya.
Grant ki value divide by asset’s life → expected income har saal calculate.
Compare kiya with actual P&L recognition.
🔍 Findings:
BS me grant ka balance zyada tha ❌
Analytical calculation se kam aana chahiye tha.
Company ne grant ko apni marzi se deferred rakha aur thoda thoda release karta tha (income smoothing 📉📈).
Agar audit hota → agreements verify karte, lekin yahan sirf analytical detection hua.

📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔹 Intro to ISRE (International Standards on Review Engagements)
Full form: International Standards on Review Engagement (ISRE)
Relevant standards:
ISRE 2410 → When auditor of the company performs review engagement (listed company ke half yearly FS).
ISRE 2400 → When non-auditor performs review engagement (jab company ka statutory audit required nahi hota).
⚖️ ISRE 2410 – Applicable Scenario
Jab auditor already appointed hai as statutory auditor of the company.
Listed companies ke liye half-yearly FS review required by law 📜.
Auditor ko review karna hai aur report deni hai according to ISRE 2410.
Assurance = Limited (Inquiry + Analytical Procedures).
👀 Example: 6-monthly review of listed company FS → auditor ko ISRE 2410 follow karna hi hoga.
⚖️ ISRE 2400 – Applicable Scenario
Jab company ki statutory audit requirement hi nahi hoti (capital threshold se neeche).
Company khud voluntary review karwana chahe.
Auditor (ya koi bhi practitioner) → ISRE 2400 follow karega.
Assurance = Limited hi hogi.
💡 Capital Threshold for Audit Requirement
Private companies ko statutory audit tabhi required jab unka paid-up capital ek threshold se zyada ho.
(Sir ne bola exact figure confirm karenge – approx 1M ya 3M ka discussion tha). 🤔
Agar threshold se neeche → Audit requirement nahi.
Usi case me agar review karwana ho → ISRE 2400 apply hoga.
🧐 Acceptance of Review Engagement (Non-Audit Case)
Jab auditor ke paas aisi company aaye jiska audit required nahi → auditor ko pehle purpose (“RATIONALE”) samajhna hoga:
Kyun review chahiye?
Agar sirf naam associate karna hai (EY review kare to credibility ho) → Risky ⚠️
Bank loan ke liye review? → Bank sirf audit report pe rely karega, review report pe nahi.
Matlab → Client’s motive samajhna zaroori hai before accepting.
🔹 Acceptance vs Engagement Letter
Step 1: Acceptance decision lena hota hai ✅
Step 2: Uske baad Engagement Letter issue hota hai (formal written agreement).
Matlab pehle decide karna hai → “Kya yeh client accept karna bhi chahiye ya nahi?”
🚫 Kab Accept Nahi Karna?
Management involved in fraud / NAB cases, integrity issues.
Independence compromised → Auditor independent nahi hai.
Data unavailable / poor history of cooperation.
Client ka objective suspicious ho → e.g., sirf naam associate karna chahta hai.
💡 Engagement letter tabhi likhna hai jab acceptance ki conditions clear ho jayein.
🔹 Parent Co. vs Subsidiary Case
Agar parent company bole ke apni subsidiary ka review karao (auditor of parent being asked to do subsidiary review):
Possible hai ✅
Lekin dikkat: Subsidiary ke internal controls & records auditor ko directly nahi maloom.
⚠️ Risk: Understanding of business aur control testing ka base missing hoga.
Isliye → Accept sirf rational purpose ke liye karo.
⚖️ Difference: Auditor of FS vs Non-Auditor
Auditor of FS (e.g. ISRE 2410 situation):
Already has knowledge of business.
Controls test kar chuka hota hai.
Review me inquiries + analytics easier.
Non-Auditor (ISRE 2400 situation):
No prior knowledge of ICFR.
No understanding of accounting system.
Hence → Extra cautious acceptance.
✅ Conditions for Acceptance
Practitioner shall not accept review engagement unless:
Rational purpose hai.
Review engagement us purpose ko fulfil karne ke liye appropriate hai.
📌 Example:
Bank Loan case → Rational purpose = Loan lena. BUT ❌ Review acceptable nahi (Bank demands audited FS).
Shareholder/Board request → Rational purpose valid ✅ Review possible.
🏛️ Link with Company Law
First Statutory Meeting (after incorporation, before AGM):
Company ne apna initial performance shareholders ko present karna hota hai.
Agar statutory audit requirement nahi hai → Company apni interim FS review report submit kar sakti hai.
🔹 Purpose & Acceptance
Engagement accept karne se pehle purpose check karna zaroori hai.
Ye confirm karna ke kya engagement approach (Review Engagement) purpose achieve karne ke liye appropriate hai.
Old engagements mandatory nahi, purpose clear hona chahiye.
Kisi bhi engagement ko accept karte waqt ye ensure karo ke auditor ka name associate na ho client ke decisions se.
🔹 Financial Reporting Framework
Financial reporting framework engagement ke liye acceptable hona chahiye.
Agar framework acceptable nahi → engagement accept nahi karna.
Review Engagement me auditor ki detailed internal control testing nahi hoti, unlike audit.
Management ki responsibilities:
Data provide karna
Inquiries ka response dena
Internal controls ka disclosure
🔹 Ethical Requirements
Independence aur ethical requirements mandatory.
Agar management ki integrity pe doubt ho → engagement accept nahi karna.
Scope limitations ko clearly document karna zaruri.
🔹 Engagement Letter
Engagement letter me specify karna:
Review Engagement hai, audit nahi
Limited scope aur procedures (inquiries + analytical procedures)
Management ki responsibilities
Practitioner responsibilities
Reporting type aur reference standard (ISE 2400)
ISE 2410 only apply hota hai agar auditor is appointed for half-year review, not general review.
Engagement letter se expectation gap close hota hai.
🔹 Acceptance Decision Factors
Client ka rational purpose clear ho.
Auditor independent ho.
Management ki integrity clear ho.
Financial reporting framework acceptable ho.
Scope limitations clearly define ho.
Legal/regulatory compliance meet ho.
✅ Agar above conditions nahi meet → engagement accept nahi karna.
🔹 Practical Examples
Bank loan case → Review Engagement appropriate hai agar purpose loan obtain karna hai.
Parent-subsidiary scenario → Auditor of parent cannot perform review for subsidiary if not appointed, to avoid name association.
New company formation → Statutory meetings (pre-AGM) check karte hain ki company ka performance acceptable hai.
Lecture- 3
📚 Reference: Book Pages -
📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔹 Recap: Review Engagement vs Audit
Review Engagement = Limited assurance, mainly inquiries + analytical procedures.
Audit = Detailed procedures, testing, verification.
Objective difference: Review focuses on high-level checks, audit on detailed compliance.
💡 Agar review ka scope limited hai → limited assurance provide hoti hai.
🔹 Purpose Check Before Acceptance
Engagement accept karne se pehle purpose samajhna zaroori.
Check karo: Client ka objective kya hai?
Example: Loan apply karna, statutory requirement, donor compliance.
Risk: Agar client sirf auditor ka name use kar ke credibility chahta hai → accept na karein.
✅ Rational purpose samajhna → engagement acceptance ki key condition.
🔹 Independence & Ethical Considerations
Independence = Mandatory
Agar auditor ki independence compromised ho → engagement reject.
Agar management ki integrity pe doubt ho → engagement accept nahi karna.
Practitioner ko ethical standards follow karne honge.
🔹 Financial Reporting Framework
Framework engagement ke liye acceptable hona chahiye.
Agar framework acceptable nahi → engagement accept nahi karna.
Example: Simple small business loan statement vs complex corporate FS.
🔹 Practical Examples & Scenarios
Government Agency Loan
Small business loan from government agency
Purpose: Regulatory submission, simple calculations → Review Engagement acceptable
Bank Loan
Bank demands audited FS → Review not acceptable
NPO / Donation Compliance
Purpose: Report donations to regulatory body → Review engagement acceptable
High-level review of compliance and statement accuracy
Parent-Subsidiary Case
Auditor of parent cannot perform review for subsidiary if not appointed
Reason: Avoid name association, IC knowledge missing
🔹 Decision Process for Acceptance
Determine purpose of engagement
Evaluate rationality of purpose
Check independence & integrity
Verify financial reporting framework is acceptable
Identify any scope limitations
Confirm legal/regulatory compliance
✅ Agar above conditions meet → engagement accept ❌ Agar conditions fail → engagement reject
🔹 High-Level Risk Consideration
Agar audit risk zyada hai → Detection risk kam hona chahiye.
Limited procedures (Review Engagement) me detailed testing nahi hota.
Result: Limited assurance, high-level detection only.
💡 Matlab: Review Engagement me kam detection hota, sirf analytics aur inquiries pe depend karta hai.
🔹 Private Company Review Scenario
Private company pre-incorporation ya newly incorporated.
Management chahta hai updated FS for review.
Agar FS highly complex hai → Review engagement may not capture all risks.
Conclusion: High-risk, complex FS → review may miss material misstatements.
🔹 ISRE 2400 Applicability
Agar practitioner not statutory auditor of company:
No prior knowledge of ICFR.
Limited review only.
Cannot detect all errors/misstatements.
Statutory auditor for half-year review → better control understanding.
🔹 Control Testing & Risk Assessment
Control deficiencies se risk ka idea milta hai.
Example: Salary calculation error → detected via control review.
Bad debts → may result from weak control (e.g., approval errors, aging issues).
Limited review cannot test controls, only analytical procedures.
💡 Matlab: Review engagement me control weaknesses fully detect nahi hote.
🔹 Understanding Operations & Environment
Practitioner must understand operations:
Key operations and applications.
Regulatory environment and applicable industry standards.
Economic factors (e.g., exchange rates impact on exports).
Ye understanding se relevant inquiries design kar sakte hain.
📌 Example:
Export company → check for revenue impact due to foreign exchange rate fluctuations.
Inquiry: “Has drop in sales occurred due to exchange rate changes?”
Analytical procedure: Link financial data with operations & environment.
🔹 Designing Inquiries for Review Engagement
Identify key performance applications from operations understanding.
Formulate relevant inquiries to verify:
Performance allocation.
Revenue recognition.
Key transactions (exports, imports, loans, etc.).
Scenario-dependent: One-size-fits-all approach nahi hota.
💡 Matlab: Analytical and inquiry procedures must be tailored for each entity & situation.
🔹 Practical Exercise (Example)
Examiner asks: “Provide 4-5 examples where you use operations understanding to design inquiries for review engagement under ISRE 2400.”
Step 1: Understand operations and environment.
Step 2: Identify key performance applications.
Step 3: Design specific inquiries.
Step 4: Link findings with analytical procedures.
✅ Unique scenario, requires logical thinking outside the standard course outline.
🔹 Risk Assessment in Review Engagement
Risk Assessment bhi review engagements me hoti hai, jaise audit me hoti hai.
Practitioner limited procedures perform karta hai (analytical + inquiry).
Risk identify karne ke liye:
Observation
Inspection
Analytical procedures
💡 Example: Samsung ne correctly identify kiya, observation aur analytical procedure ke through.
🔹 Inquiry Form & Shortcut
Inquiry ko document karne ke liye short-cut/form use karte hain.
Inquiry ke responses ko systematically record kiya jata hai.
💡 Example: Export sales me increase, foreign exchange rate neechay → inquiry sahi hai, response record karna.
🔹 Comparative Analysis
Sales increase ya decrease ke liye quantitative comparison zaruri hai.
Documents check karna: Bills of Lading, export documents, quantities.
Analytical procedure ke through limited assurance milti hai.
🔹 Operations & Environment Understanding
Operations aur environment ka understanding lete hain:
Company ka operations ka nature
Export, production, technology use
Regulatory framework, industry aspects
Inquiry target: Implementation, performance indicators, technological updates.
💡 Example: Payment testing → target inquiries on input, process, and future influence.
🔹 Implementation Testing & Inquiry
Inquiry target questions:
Are you aware of any implementation indicators?
How do you perform payment testing?
What inputs and calculations are used?
Practical example: Plant with fire incident → new technology implemented.
Inquiry shows if implementation testing is done properly.
🔹 Going Concern Assessment
Cash flow/Working capital ka understanding limited assurance me assess karte hain.
Future plans included: allowable in review engagement.
Cash losses, projections, and business plan ke base pe going concern assessment.
💡 Example: Provided cash losses used for going concern check.
Historical cash data aur future projections use kar ke rational conclusion.
Inquiry aur analytical procedures combined to validate assessment.
1. Going Concern Assessment:
Assessment period: 12 months.
Focus on cash flows, loan obligations, and negative net positions.
Evaluate management plans for covering deficits (e.g., parent loans, future inflows).
Important to distinguish between actual cash position and future projections.
Apply stress testing to cash flows, net position, and debt servicing.
Use analytical procedures to link cash losses to potential going concern risks.
Key insight: Future business plans are considered only if they are realistic and supported by evidence.
2. Audit Inquiry Process:
Targeted inquiries help identify misstatements or high-risk areas.
Conduct inquiries based on operations understanding and environment factors.
Focus on unusual or abnormal transactions.
Verify responses using available documentation and analytical procedures.
Track significant events and changes in business operations (e.g., new products, branches, technology).
Minutes of meetings, management communications, and financial projections are key sources.
3. Analytical Procedures:
Compare current period data with prior period for trend analysis.
Evaluate impact of foreign exchange fluctuations on export sales.
Cross-check cash flows, loans, and liabilities for consistency.
Use ratios, net positions, and expected vs. actual outcomes to identify anomalies.
Apply element-wise and experience-based calculations for precise evaluation.
4. Related Party Transactions:
Identify all related parties and their transactions.
Focus on unusual, non-standard, or outside-the-normal-course-of-business transactions.
Verify completeness and consistency of related party listings.
Compare opening balances with current year to identify anomalies.
Inquiries: Determine responsible personnel and verify the purpose of each transaction.
5. Linking Understanding to Audit Actions:
Operations understanding → design inquiries → perform analytical procedures → limited assurance.
Understand management controls and their design to assess potential risk areas.
Significant events and conditions are evaluated for their impact on going concern.
Document all observations and link them to specific audit conclusions.

📚 LECTURE FULL EXPLANATION:
🔹 Practical Considerations in Review Engagement
Engagement start: quick assessment & response ki importance.
Practitioner ko inquiries aur analytical procedures design karni hoti hai situation ke mutabiq.
Procedures globally standard nahi hoti, har situation ke liye tailored approach chahiye.
💡 Example: Multiple scenarios diye ja sakte hain practical application test karne ke liye.
🔹 Inquiry & Analytical Design
Pehle operations samjho, phir inquiry design karo.
Analytical procedures depend karti hain operations, environment aur transaction nature par.
Hamesha going concern perspective mind me rakho, review engagement me bhi.
📌 Example: 6-month review me next 12 months ke liye going concern consider karo.
🔹 Reporting Considerations
Report send/sign karte waqt:
Company ko going concern declare karo ya nahi.
Agar zarurat ho to material insert related to going concern include karo.
Review reports me material insert paragraph usually available nahi hota; EUM ya disclosure statements use karo.
💡 Basic difference: Audit vs Review reporting – paragraphs, procedures aur assurance levels alag hote hain.
🔹 Use of Experts in Review Engagement
Management expert appoint kar sakti hai specific valuation ya computation ke liye.
Practitioner ka role:
Expert ka kaam samajhna
Inquiry aur review karna
Practitioner ko basic knowledge honi chahiye expert ke field me, taake relevant questions pooch sake.
💡 Example: Expert appointed for 200 investment properties valuation.
Practitioner expert se report aur supporting evidence lete hain.
Practitioner review aur inquiry karta hai, direct valuation nahi karta.
🔹 Practical Use of Experts & Valuation
Practitioner expert ko appoint kar sakta hai valuation ya calculations ke liye.
Expert report aur evidence provide karta hai, practitioner usko review karta hai.
Practitioner ko samajhna chahiye ke limited assurance purposes ke liye expert kaise appoint karna hai.
Management specific task ke liye expert hire kar sakta hai; practitioner valuation dobara nahi karta.
💡 Example: 200 properties ki real estate valuation.
Practitioner expert ka kaam review karta hai.
Agar regulator audit question kare to practitioner explain karta hai limited assurance aur expert appointment.
🔹 Limited Assurance & Reporting
Practitioner full assurance nahi de sakta agar sirf limited work hua ho.
Responsibilities engagement letter me clearly document honi chahiye.
Practitioner ensure karta hai ke financial reporting framework follow ho raha hai.
💡 Example: Practitioner document karta hai ke management ne complete information di aur relevant suspicions report kiye gaye.
Limited assurance engagements inquiry aur analytical review pe depend karte hain, detailed valuation pe nahi.
🔹 Prepaid Expenses & Trace
Prepaid = future period ke liye advance payment.
Payments trace karo through bank statements, recalculation as necessary.
Example: 180 properties ke prepaid tax, total 205 properties me se sirf part review hua.
📌 Entries:
Debit: Expense
Credit: Prepaid
Recalculate kar ke correct allocation ensure karo.
Practitioner understanding link karta hai review procedures se.
Sampling use ho sakti hai due to time limitations.
🔹 Management Meetings & Legal Claims
Legal/financial claims management ke saath discuss karo.
Example: Properties as gift from government, check tax applicability.
Practitioner ensure karta hai ke claims properly document huye aur responsibility assign hui.
Meetings me finance aur legal team bhi hoti hai, practitioner aur assistant ke saath.
🔹 Review Engagement – Practical Walkthrough
Jab mistakes pakdi jaati hain, purpose ye hota hai ki practical understanding develop ho.
Practitioner ka goal show-off nahi, balki professional judgment aur evidence collection.
Work instructions clearly communicate karna zaroori hai, jaise property name, area, jurisdiction, regulatory elements note karna.
💡 Example: 3,265 properties review karna aur check karna ki tax apply hua ya nahi.
🔹 Client Interaction & Evidence
Scope aur limitations client ko clearly explain karna.
Missing information identify karna aur scope limitation pe highlight karna.
Practitioner ensures proper supporting evidence collected from client before concluding.
💡 Example: Karachi ke different areas (Clifton, DHA, Saddar) properties verify karna aur tax records check karna.
🔹 Retention & Representation
Practitioner can obtain retained representations from client for evidence.
Purpose: Evidence ka documentation aur confirmation that client ne saari information provide ki hai.
Representation ensures accountability aur risk mitigation.
💡 Note: Client representation ko evidence ke liye use kar sakte hain, but documentation proper hona chahiye.
🔹 Review vs Audit – Opinion Section
Audit report mein opinion deta hai; review report mein conclusion based on evidence.
Key focus areas:
Accounting errors
Disclosure issues
Classification matters
Materiality consider karte hue conclusion draw ki jaati hai.
💡 Example: Misstatement aur scope limitations ko identify karke report mein highlight karna.
🔹 Drafting & Reporting
Practitioner drafts report section-wise based on collected evidence.
Report includes:
Management responsibilities
Basis for conclusion
Evidence supporting conclusion
Drafting ensures consistency aur professional presentation.
💡 Example: Opinion section draft karna based on review evidence; clearly distinguish audit vs review report sections.
🔹 Review Engagement Responsibilities
Management responsibility: Financial statements bananay ka aur framework apply karna.
Practitioner responsibility: Review aur inquiry karna without full audit.
Internal control ka test practitioner ke liye optional, focus material misstatements aur risk assessment par.
💡 Example: Agar management ne internal control prepare kiya hai, practitioner usko test nahi karta, bas understanding aur review karta hai.
🔹 Property Valuation & Prepaid Expenses
Properties ka valuation aur tax assessment crucial hai.
Prepaid expenses (PP): Advance payments ko correctly debit aur credit entries me record karna.
📌 Example:
Agar future liability 120 aur prepaid amount 100 hai → 100 debit, 20 negative adjustment aur liability credit.
Practitioner review karta hai, calculation ya capitalization errors check karta hai.
🔹 Adjustments & Financial Reporting
Present value adjustments: Future liabilities ka present value calculate karna.
CWIP (Construction Work in Progress) ka recording aur adjustment correct hona chahiye.
Errors in capitalization or depreciation corrected through proper journal entries.
💡 Example: Agar gross 120 aur net 100 required hai → debit aur credit entries adjust karke net value record karni.
🔹 Drafting & Reporting
Review report me opinion nahi hota, sirf conclusions based on evidence.
Practitioner ensures supporting evidence complete hai.
Material misstatements ya scope limitations report me note kiye jaate hain.
💡 Example: Agar property tax disclosure missing hai → scope limitation ya representation note ki jati hai.
